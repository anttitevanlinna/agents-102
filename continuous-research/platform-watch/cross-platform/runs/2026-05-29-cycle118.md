### Finding A1 — Atlassian NORA: HR staff build production onboarding agent without engineers
**Source:** https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/06/ai-native-teamwork-spotlighted-atlassianteam/ — [domain trade publication]
**Supporting:** https://community.atlassian.com/forums/Atlassian-AI-Rovo-articles/How-to-Create-a-High-Impact-Rovo-Agent-Lessons-from-NORA/ba-p/3157915 — [practitioner direct, Dec 4, 2025]
**Date:** May 6, 2026 (SiliconAngle); Dec 4, 2025 (Atlassian community post)
**Agent level:** Team → Company (shared internal agent)
**What:** Atlassian's HR team built NORA (Newlassian Onboarding Rovo Agent) without engineering involvement, using Atlassian Rovo. Outcomes: 70%+ of new hires used NORA in first month, 2,000+ questions asked, top 5 internal agent by weekly active users and message volume. SiliconAngle coverage at Atlassian Team '26 (May 6, 2026) explicitly calls this out: "NORA — an onboarding agent built by HR staff rather than engineers — a signal that agentic AI is widening access to automation well beyond technical teams."
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company self-report; vendor platform — note confirmation bias risk, but specific metrics are strong)
**Key claims:**
- HR staff, not engineers, built a production agent used by 70%+ of new hires
- Platform: Atlassian Rovo (no-code agent creation)
- Top 5 internal agent by WAU — organizational utility, not a toy
- "85% of knowledge workers use AI, but only 29% have embedded it in actual flows of work" — Atlassian stat from Team '26
- Atlassian CEO: "Intelligence is the engine; context is the fuel"

**Why this matters:** This is the second named non-engineering team building a production agent independently (after F-Secure finance, March 2026). Pattern emerging: finance and HR are the non-engineering beachheads. Both domains have verifiable correctness proxies (bookkeeping balances, onboarding completion) that enable feedback loops — consistent with the verification gap (Pattern E) analysis.

### Finding A2 — ARE role and spec-writer evaluation metrics (practitioner framework)
**Source:** https://optimumpartners.com/insight/engineering-management-2026-how-to-structure-an-ai-native-team/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** December 18, 2025
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** Optimum Partners proposes "Centaur Pod" structure: Senior Architect + AI Reliability Engineer (ARE) + Autonomous Agent Fleet. The ARE role replaces junior developer and is evaluated on output quality, not code volume. Four new metrics: (1) Defect Capture Rate — % of AI-generated errors identified before staging; (2) MTTV (Mean Time to Verification) — velocity of human review/merge of AI PRs; (3) Change Failure Rate (AI-Specific) — regression/rollback frequency from AI code; (4) Interaction Churn — prompt iterations needed per usable output (high churn = poor spec quality).
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner framework, no deployment data, no named companies)
**Key claims:**
- Spec quality is measurable via Interaction Churn — prompt iterations as proxy
- MTTV is a new engineering metric that has no equivalent in pre-AI teams
- Evaluation shifts from "lines of code" to "defect capture rate"
- No named companies deploying this evaluation system

**Why this matters:** Partially addresses the Level 3 absence finding about spec-writer evaluation. These metrics are proposed, not deployed. But they give CTOs a vocabulary. The absence finding holds — no company has published "here's how we evaluate whether a person is good at directing AI" at scale.

### Finding A3 — EY EYQ → GPT-5 pivot: buy beats build even at professional services scale
**Source:** https://www.ey.com/en_gl/insights/ai/building-an-enterprise-scale-agentic-ai-operating-system — [vendor case study, Level 0]
**Date:** 2026 (exact date not specified on page)
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** EY deployed EYQ (custom AI platform) to 300,000+ professionals, achieving 80%+ active use, 80%+ training completion, 2M learning hours, 115,000 AI badges. Then EY pivoted: when GPT-5 launched with "significantly more advanced capabilities," EY became the second-largest GPT-5 enterprise adopter globally, abandoning the custom EYQ approach. New stack: Microsoft Azure Foundry (orchestration) + Copilot + Copilot Studio + Microsoft Fabric (data). Multi-function coverage: Tax, Assurance, Consulting, internal.
**Evidence level:** Level 0 (EY's own case study about their own deployment — no independent verification). Scale metrics are credible but unverifiable.
**Key claims:**
- 300,000+ professionals, 80%+ active use (vendor-reported)
- Pivoted FROM custom to Microsoft/GPT-5 when commercial AI outpaced custom build
- Multi-function: Tax, Assurance, Consulting, internal — closest thing to "floor redesign" in professional services
- EY Nordic offices (Sweden, Norway, Finland) promote same case study

**Why this matters:** EY is NOT a traditional industrial enterprise (it IS a tech-forward services firm). But the pivot signal matters: even buyers with resources and incentive to build custom chose commercial AI. Counter-evidence for "build your own stack" CTOs. Level 0 source — note caveats.

## Orientation

Two patterns are hardening:
1. **Non-engineering agent adoption is real but confined to domains with verifiable outputs** (HR onboarding completion, finance bookkeeping). Pattern E (verification gap) is holding as the structural predictor.
2. **EY's pivot from custom to GPT-5 is important counter-evidence:** even tech-forward buyers with resources chose to buy rather than build. The custom AI OS path appears difficult to sustain when commercial models advance monthly.

Newcode.ai fills the Nordic legal gap — a category-appropriate AI-native startup with Nordic roots, distinct from the Harvey/Legora duopoly.
