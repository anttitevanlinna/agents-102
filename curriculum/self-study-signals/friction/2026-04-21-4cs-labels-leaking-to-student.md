**Context:** Module 1 opening, live test 2026-04-21. Teacher said *"C1: Connections"* to the student.

**Signal:** The 4 Cs framework labels (C1/C2/C3/C4 — Connections/Concepts/Concrete Practice/Conclusions) are pedagogy for curriculum designers and facilitators, not vocabulary the student should ever meet. Naming them to the student is jargon-by-implication: opaque labels that announce the structure rather than letting it be felt. Worse, "C1 with C and number" is a designer shorthand — even if expanded ("Connections phase"), it adds noise the student doesn't need.

**Root cause:** SKILL.md uses `### C1 — Connections (5 min)` etc. as section headings (legitimate for the SKILL author), and Teacher Claude paraphrased that structure into chat as if it were content.

**Fix applied:** SKILL.md `## The cadence per module` now leads with an explicit rule: *"Never name the framework to the student. Don't say 'C1', 'Connections phase', 'Phase 2 — Concepts', or any 4 Cs label. Just do the move."* The rhythm is felt, not announced.

**Generalize:** anywhere SKILL.md uses internal pedagogical scaffolding (4 Cs labels, Bloom levels, mood-arc names like "M3 unsettled competence"), Teacher should not name it to the student. The student experiences it; the designer names it.
