### Finding 3: EO 14409 August 1 Voluntary Framework — Confirmed Legal Details

**Source:** https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/ — [primary government source]
**Source 2:** https://www.lw.com/en/insights/president-trump-signs-executive-order-establishing-ai-cybersecurity-and-frontier-model-framework — [domain trade publication, Latham & Watkins legal analysis]
**Source 3:** https://www.wiley.law/alert-New-AI-Executive-Order-Addresses-Frontier-Models-and-Cybersecurity-Vulnerabilities — [domain trade publication, Wiley Rein legal analysis]
**Date:** June 2, 2026 (EO signed); August 1, 2026 (60-day deliverable deadline)
**Agent level:** company
**What:** Executive Order 14409 "Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security," signed June 2, 2026 — 10 days BEFORE the Fable 5 ban on June 12 — mandates that NSA, Treasury, and CISA deliver within 60 days: (a) a classified benchmarking process for assessing cyber capabilities of frontier AI models, and (b) a voluntary pre-release engagement channel between developers and the federal government. The NSA director holds "covered frontier model" designation authority. Key structural detail: the framework is **voluntary**, not mandatory licensing or pre-clearance. Developers may opt in, provide 30-day pre-release government access, and collaborate on trusted-partner selection. August 1 = the structural deadline; the Mythos "Annex A" letter (June 26) appears to be an interim mechanism created while the formal August 1 framework is being built. This EO predating the ban by 10 days suggests the ban itself was enforcement action under this pre-existing authority, not a new policy.
**Evidence level:** 2 (primary government source + independent legal analysis from two major law firms)
**Key claims:**
- EO 14409 signed June 2, 2026 — predates Fable 5 ban by 10 days
- August 1 = 60-day deadline for NSA/Treasury/CISA to deliver voluntary framework
- Framework is VOLUNTARY for developers — not mandatory licensing
- NSA director designates "covered frontier model" status
- Mythos "Annex A" letter is an interim mechanism pending the formal August 1 framework
- Regulatory risk for AI is lower than worst-case scenario (voluntary, not mandatory pre-clearance)
