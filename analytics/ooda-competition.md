# Blind OODA — Competitive Landscape for AE101 (Nov 2025 – Apr 2026)

## Method

Eight WebSearch sweeps across six categories (paid cohorts for engineers, corporate write-ups, Nordic consultancies, YouTube deep courses, conference workshops, books-with-courses), plus WebFetch on the Every Compound Engineering page. Surfaced ~25 distinct offerings; kept the 12 that plausibly compete for an L0→L3 engineering-IC training budget in a Nordic enterprise. Confidence: **medium-high on paid cohort space** (well-indexed, Maven-centric), **medium on Nordic density** (consultancies don't publish curricula; hidden custom training likely undercounted), **low on internal corporate programs** (write-ups describe the platforms they built, not the training curricula).

## Corporate upskilling programs (public write-ups)

What's public is **platforms, not training curricula** — a useful negative finding.

- **Ramp** built an internal coding agent platform; 30% of engineering PRs flow through it ([infoq.com](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/01/ramp-coding-agent-platform/), [tech press]).
- **Stripe, Shopify, Airbnb** — "AI coding agent harness" pattern writeups ([mindstudio blog](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/ai-coding-agent-harness-stripe-shopify-airbnb), [practitioner analysis]).
- **Intercom** rebuilt CS platform on GPT ([stripe customer story](https://stripe.com/customers/intercom), [vendor case study] — L0).

None published a "here's how we train our engineers on agents" curriculum in the last 6 months. The absence is the story: big-name companies compound by building infrastructure, not by running internal Bloom-ladder trainings. AE101 competes against *hire-and-osmose* at these firms, not against a published course.

## The #1 direct threat to an AE101 sale

**Matt Pocock, Claude Code for Real Engineers.** Reasoning:
- Same target (experienced SWE ICs, not data scientists).
- Same platform centre (Claude Code specifically, not "agents in general").
- Same arc shape: context → plan → execute → delegate autonomous agents. That's AE101 M1→M2→M3→M7 compressed.
- $795 anchors the market at **half the Maven price point**.
- Pocock's TypeScript/React reputation carries directly into the buyer's frame.

The #2 threat is **Klaassen's Compound Engineering at Every** — narratively the most overlapping (AE101 literally seeded its M5/M6 language from Klaassen). If Every runs a public cohort in the Nordic enterprise's time zone, it directly competes for the "I want the real thing" budget.

## Trainings with material arc overlap

- **Pocock (CCfRE)** — M1/M2/M3/M7 overlap. No M8 horizon, no multi-agent deliberation, no team/promotion layer.
- **Klaassen (Compound Engineering)** — M4-M6 test-learn-encode + M7 multi-agent overlap. Light on M1 foundations (assumes already technical).
- **Agent Engineering Bootcamp (Farooq/Hasan)** — M7 multi-agent heavy; light on compound / self-improving loop.
- **Swyx Level-Up** — M1/M2 foundations; skips M4-M8.

None hit the full M1→M8 arc. AE101's differentiator is the arc completeness + the L0→L3 promotion frame.

## "Compound engineering" / "agents building agents" positioning cluster

Only **Klaassen/Every** owns the phrase in market. Pocock uses "AFK agents" and "feedback loops" (same idea, different brand). Ryan Ahmed's Maven bootcamp talks "agent fleets" — adjacent vocabulary, different mechanic (orchestration, not self-improvement). The narrative is still uncrowded — AE101 can use the language without sounding like an also-ran, **provided Klaassen is credited explicitly** (which is current practice).

## Nordic competitive density

**Clear air for cohort training of engineering ICs.** No Nordic consultancy (Reaktor/Futurice/Solita/Gofore/Knowit/Tietoevry/Vincit/Siili) publishes an agent-training curriculum for engineers. NobleProg and Encertify offer generic Nordic-delivered courses but target "AI developers" broadly and teach LangChain/CrewAI — not Claude-Code-native compound engineering for staff engineers.

**The competition is conferences** (AgenticDevDays, AgentCon Stockholm, Data Innovation Summit) which eat awareness/budget but don't train to L3. **Solita launched a product, not training** — they're selling the finished orchestrator, not teaching the craft.

Counter-check: searched Reaktor training page, Futurice blog, Gofore, Knowit — no named agent cohort. Absence is real.

## Price ceiling

- **Floor**: $795 (Pocock, 2 weeks, asynchronous replays)
- **Maven cluster**: $1.5k–$2.5k (7–10 week cohorts)
- **Premium/editorial**: Every's Compound Engineering likely $2k+ (inferred from Every's other products; not confirmed)
- **Conference workshop**: $195–$500 half/full day, plus conference pass ~$2k
- **Corporate private**: Dan Mason's Stride offers private team-of-50+ custom pricing (not public)

**Market bearing**: **$1.5k–$2.5k per seat for 4–8 week cohort**; **$3k–$5k+ per seat achievable only for in-person + enterprise-wrapped delivery.** A Nordic enterprise B2B engagement (in-room, 2 days, 10–20 engineers + sponsor) can price at €25k–€60k without market resistance — but that's a services sale, not a per-seat comparison.

## Strategic reads for Bosser

1. **Pocock at $795 is the anchor — don't compete on it.** AE101's in-room/remote cohort is a different SKU. Price by seat-equivalent only in self-study; price by engagement when sponsor-attended.
2. **Klaassen is the narrative neighbour, not the competitor.** Every runs 4-week remote Zoom cohorts in US time. A Nordic CTO wanting in-room + local trainer has no Every option. Credit Klaassen by name, don't hide from him.
3. **Nordic clear air is real but shrinking fast.** Stockholm events suggest awareness is peaking Q2 2026. First Nordic trainer to publish an engineering-IC curriculum wins the category. **Move now.**
4. **Arc completeness is the moat.** Nobody runs M1→M8. The training-as-forcing-function + sponsor-attends model is also unique — no competitor sells the CTO a seat in the room.
5. **Corporate write-ups tell us what to teach, not what to compete with.** Ramp/Stripe/Shopify publish their platforms. AE101 should teach *how to build those patterns in your own company* — the platform posts become the evidence base, not the competition.

## Confidence

**Medium-high** on paid cohort landscape (Maven-dominated, well-indexed). **Medium** on Nordic density — absence is confirmed in public surfaces, but Nordic consultancies frequently run **unpublished custom training** for enterprise clients; Bosser should assume Reaktor/Futurice/Solita *could* mobilise a private curriculum within 60 days if a Nordic CTO asks. **Low** on internal-corporate programs — the interesting competitors here aren't curriculums, they're senior-engineer osmosis inside AI-native companies, which doesn't show up in search.
