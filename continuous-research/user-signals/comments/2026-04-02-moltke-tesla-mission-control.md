Practitioner insight: the absorption bottleneck has a symmetric upstream partner — the intent bottleneck. Humans specify intent slower than AI can consume it. The organization is squeezed from both ends.

Combine Moltke's Auftragstaktik (communicate intent, not instructions — decentralized execution, centralized purpose) with Tesla's "data is the manager" (real-time metrics replace supervisory oversight). Result: don't evaluate output, monitor trajectory against intent. Fundamentally different cognitive task — one scales, the other doesn't.

Program and project management is the organizational middleware that routes intent down and aggregates results up. AI removes both reasons this middleware exists: (1) execution is now cheap, so careful planning loses its ROI, (2) information is now ambient via data, so human routing is unnecessary.

Line org planning — hierarchical cascading, annual budgets, cross-functional coordination meetings, escalation paths — are synchronization mechanisms designed for expensive execution and human-routed information. Remove both constraints and the apparatus compresses.

Hard irreducible core: intent alignment (humans agreeing on priorities) doesn't automate. But it compresses to fewer, higher-leverage decision points. Everything between quarterly strategy and exception-triggered intervention is management theatre.
