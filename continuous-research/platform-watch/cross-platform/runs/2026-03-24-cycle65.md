1. **No HR practitioners deploying agents and reporting results** — ninth consecutive cycle with zero HR signal.
2. **No operations/supply chain practitioners who publish independently** — every finding traces to vendor channels or trade press, not practitioner blogs/X.com. The practitioner-publishing gap is structural in operations.
3. **No Finnish operations/supply chain practitioners** — despite Finland's strong AI infrastructure position and RELEX being Finnish.
4. **No independent verification of any claimed metrics** — Danfoss 80%, Coca-Cola 90min→seconds, US Cold Storage 87% all trace to vendor announcements.
5. **No Copilot Cowork reviews** — 31st consecutive zero.
6. **No convergence (Level 3) in operations/supply chain** — each finding is a single experiment. Closest: autonomous order processing (Danfoss + Go Autonomous platform with multiple customers) but that's one platform, not independent convergence.

## Orientation

**The Nordic cluster is this cycle's most valuable discovery.** Go Autonomous (Copenhagen) + Danfoss + Grundfos + Nilfisk + Maersk represents the first Nordic-origin agentic operations cluster found in 65 cycles. All are Level 2 (vendor-sourced metrics) but the cluster pattern is more credible than any single deployment — multiple independent Danish companies choosing agentic automation for B2B order processing.

**Operations/supply chain is further along than the roster suggested.** The previous roster had only Uppuluri/Walmart. This cycle found 5 named practitioners and 1 corporate deployment, all with specific metrics. The domain is moving — but practitioners don't publish, so our people-first research method has a structural blind spot. Trade press (FreightWaves, Logistics Viewpoints, FleetOwner) and vendor award pages are the discovery path for this domain.

**Compliance/AML is the under-represented domain closest to convergence.** Greenlite (Lawrence & Jin) + Diligent AI (Maschio/Gaber, with Scalapay deployment) + the existing Goldman Sachs KYC work = 3 independent signals in AML/KYC. Rules-based + verifiable + talent-constrained = the same Level 4 meta-pattern that drove finance/accounting to convergence.

**HR remains the black hole.** Zero new practitioners in 65 cycles. The structural explanation is clear: HR practitioners who automate don't publish. Alternative discovery paths (trade press, conferences) haven't yielded results either.

## Meta-Learning

Operations/supply chain research requires different source strategies than other domains: trade press (FreightWaves, Logistics Viewpoints, FleetOwner, Chain Store Age) and vendor award/case study pages are the primary discovery paths because practitioners in this domain don't blog or post on X.com. Platform-level research and person-specific searches don't work here — the signal is in domain trade press covering specific company deployments, not in practitioners sharing their work publicly. This is the opposite of the coding/engineering domain where X.com and GitHub are primary.
