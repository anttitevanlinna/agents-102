## Orientation

**The OpenAI picture is crystallizing into a clear narrative:** consumer dominance (900M users) masking enterprise weakness. Five simultaneous strategic initiatives (super-app, Frontier, PE JV, AWS deal, IPO) confirm fragmentation, not strength. The super-app merge is a correction, but one that takes months. Meanwhile:

- Frontier has no independent validation after 7+ weeks
- Enterprise market share declining at record pace
- Codex business expansion is aspirational, not delivered
- EEA/Nordic remains structurally disadvantaged
- GPT-5.4 computer use is promising but 16 days old with one field test showing reliability gaps

**For the CTO question:** OpenAI remains the default personal AI tool (ChatGPT). But for business agent deployment — multi-step, governed, integrated — the platform gaps documented since cycle 15 persist. No convergence-level evidence of OpenAI powering production business agents beyond customer service chatbots.

**New signal worth tracking:** AAIF's explosive growth (146+ members) and MCP Dev Summit (April 2-3) suggest the standards layer is consolidating faster than any single platform. The CTO answer may increasingly be "standards + best engine" rather than "pick a platform." This favors Anthropic (MCP originator + strongest model) over OpenAI (Frontier platform play with no traction).
