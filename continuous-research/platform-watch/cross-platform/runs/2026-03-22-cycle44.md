### Finding 1: EU AI Act Agent Governance Gap — Regulators Admit They Don't Know What Agents Are
**Source:** [EU AI Office — digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai) — [regulatory/government]; [The Future Society report](https://thefuturesociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/Report-Ahead-of-the-Curve-Governing-AI-Agents-Under-the-EU-AI-Act-4-June-2025.pdf) — [academic/research]; [artificialintelligenceact.eu analysis](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** Q1 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (regulatory reality confirmed by multiple independent legal analyses)
**What:** The EU Commission explicitly states its regulatory considerations for AI agents are "only preliminary at this stage" and acknowledges that "the understanding of what constitutes an AI agent varies greatly." No standalone guidance on agents exists. The AI Act was not designed with agents in mind but applies to them through the risk-based classification — agents in Annex III domains (employment, credit, essential services) are high-risk. Multi-purpose agents are presumed high-risk unless providers take "sufficient precautions" (undefined). The Future Society's report (June 2025, still the most comprehensive analysis) proposes 10 governance measures across 4 pillars (risk assessment, transparency, technical controls, human oversight) including agent IDs, shutdown protocols, and permission scaffolding — but these are proposals, not law. Guidelines for high-risk practical application coming Q2 2026 — mere weeks before the August 2 enforcement date.

### Finding 2: Singapore Published First State-Backed Agentic AI Governance Framework (Jan 2026)
**Source:** [IMDA Singapore](https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2026/new-model-ai-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai) — [regulatory/government]; [Baker McKenzie analysis](https://www.bakermckenzie.com/en/insight/publications/2026/01/singapore-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai-launched) — [domain trade publication]; [Computer Weekly](https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366637674/Singapore-debuts-worlds-first-governance-framework-for-agentic-AI) — [general press]
**Date:** January 22, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (regulatory framework, not deployment evidence)
**What:** Singapore's IMDA launched the Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI at Davos — the first state-backed governance framework specifically designed for AI agents. Four dimensions: (1) bounding risks upfront (limiting tool access, permissions, operational scope), (2) meaningful human accountability (clear responsibilities across developer/deployer/operator/end-user), (3) technical controls (least-privilege, plan reflections, staged rollouts, real-time monitoring), (4) end-user responsibility (transparency on capabilities, escalation points). Non-binding but influential — a living document open for feedback and case study contributions. **The contrast with the EU is striking:** Singapore has agent-specific governance published and evolving; the EU has general-purpose rules and admits it hasn't figured out how they apply to agents yet.
**Key claims:**
- First state-backed agentic AI governance framework, launched Jan 22, 2026 at Davos — [IMDA](https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2026/new-model-ai-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai)
- Four governance dimensions with specific technical controls — [Baker McKenzie](https://www.bakermckenzie.com/en/insight/publications/2026/01/singapore-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai-launched)
- Living document, open for public feedback and organizational case studies — [IMDA](https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2026/new-model-ai-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai)
