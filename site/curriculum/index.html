Eight Modules from Chat to Agent Builder

For leaders who believe competence comes before strategy. Your people build real agents. With your data, your policies, your systems. Working systems they can show the rest of the organization.

8 modules
20 people per cohort
100% hands-on
Format
Building is thinking. Every concept emerges from doing it first.
Who it's for
Mixed groups. CTOs, product leads, practitioners. Anyone who's had a real conversation with an LLM and wants to see what happens next.
What you need
A laptop and curiosity. No coding. Everyone works on one company initiative you all care about.
Modules 1–3

Build, Break, Understand

Your first agent in the morning. A multi-agent system by the end. One project that keeps growing. Not throwaway exercises.

01

Getting Going

You build your first agent. A real one, with your real data.

What you build
A personal site agent from your LinkedIn profile. Not copy-paste. Genuinely better, with guardrails that enforce differentiation and storytelling.
The teaching moment
You use best-in-class positioning frameworks as guardrails. The agent steers toward something you could not write yourself. That is the first insight: with the right constraints, the output is higher quality than what you would produce alone.
Read more: "Imagination feeds off on examples"
02

Building Agent Systems

One prompt got you started. Now you design a system. Multiple steps, each with a job.

What you build
A multi-step pipeline for your company initiative. The agent doesn't just generate. It researches, structures, drafts, and refines. Each step has clear inputs, outputs, and quality expectations.
Why this matters
This is the leap. From chatbot user to system builder. The shift from a single prompt to a designed pipeline changes how people think about AI.
Read more: "The ceiling"
03

Multi-Agent Systems

One agent, multiple steps. Useful. Multiple agents coordinating. Powerful. And fragile.

What you build
You split your pipeline into specialized agents. One researches, one structures, one writes, one reviews. They hand off work like an assembly line. Each focused on what it does best.
Why this matters
Coordination is where agent projects succeed or die. You experience both: the power of specialization and the pain of agents that don't understand each other.
Read more: "The mechanical duck and processes"
Modules 4–6

Discipline

You built something that works. Now make it something you can trust.

04

Security

An agent that works but isn't secure is worse than no agent.

What happens
Your agent breaks your organization's compliance rules. You watch it happen in real time. Then you build the guardrails to fix it.
Your organization goes in here
Your security policies, your data classification, your compliance requirements. Not our defaults. Yours.
Read more: "AI and certainty don't mix"
05

Output Quality and Hallucination Control

Module 1, you caught fabrication by eye. Now you catch it with a system.

What you build
An evaluation system for your company initiative. Not "does it look good?" but "can I measure good?" Evals as strategic steering. Making output more differentiated, not just correct.
Why this matters
From hoping it works to knowing. They say evals is the new black. I say so too.
Read more: "Evals is the new black"
06

Evaluations at Scale

Individual evals catch problems. Evaluation systems catch patterns.

What you build
A systematic evaluation pipeline. Automated checks that run every time the agent produces output. Same discipline software teams apply to code, applied to agent output.
Why this matters
An agent without evals is a demo. An agent with evals is a system you can trust and improve over time.
Read more: "Shifting toward customers, finding the truth"
Modules 7–8

Landscape and Flywheel

Zoom out. Where do agents live in the real world. And what happens when they start building each other.

07

Agent Platforms

You've built agents. Now: where do they live?

What you learn
The current platform landscape. What's real, what's hype, what actually works in production. How to evaluate platforms against your requirements. No vendor recommendations. A framework for making your own assessment.
Why this matters
After building in Modules 1–6, you know what you actually need from a platform. You can evaluate for yourself.
Read more: "Skating where the puck is going"
08

Agents Building Agents. The Flywheel

The flywheel. The insight that changes how you think about scale.

What you build
An agent that builds other agents. Multiplication, self-improvement, and the shift from project to capability. This isn't a one-time thing. It's a new way of working.

An executive recommendation, co-created live by the group. Not a report about what you learned. A concrete proposal for what to do next.
