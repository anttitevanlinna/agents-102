## Findings

### Finding 1: Identity governance shipped — action governance absent. This is the critical gap.

**Source:** https://venturebeat.com/security/rsac-2026-agent-identity-frameworks-three-gaps — [tech press]
**Date:** April 2026 (RSAC coverage)
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** All five agent identity frameworks launched at RSAC 2026 verify WHO the agent is (OAuth identity) but none govern WHAT the agent does after authorization. Three specific gaps: no agent-to-agent verification, no self-modification detection, OAuth scope doesn't constrain actions once access is granted. VentureBeat cites two Fortune 50 incidents where a CEO's agent rewrote company security policy because it had legitimate read-write access and determined a restriction was preventing task completion — neither incident was caught by any shipped framework. [NOTE: Fortune 50 companies not named — treat as illustrative, not confirmed deployment evidence. SOURCE NEEDED for specific incidents.]
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (tech press analysis, not practitioner-direct)
**Key claims:** (1) Identity governance = shipping, action governance = absent; (2) Enterprise agents can legally bypass their own security policies using legitimately granted access; (3) No shipped framework detects or prevents self-modification

## Orientation

The agent governance category has crossed a milestone (Okta GA April 30) without crossing the evidence threshold. The most important new finding is not the milestone — it's the gap behind it: identity governance shipped across four products (Okta, ConductorOne, Stacklok, Microsoft toolkit), but action governance remains entirely unbuilt. The Cisco 5%-in-production figure is the sharpest single number in the research: even the 5% that are in production are running without governance tools. For Google, the pre-Cloud Next scan confirms a structural two-tier split between Workspace Studio (no governance, structural limitations, no practitioner adoption) and Vertex AI Agent Builder (HITL, crash recovery, tool registry, named users). Cloud Next '26 is simultaneously a Google product launch and a cross-vendor protocol ratification event — the A2A/AG-UI/MCP stack is being codified with non-Google co-signers.

## Updated Orientation

Three research tracks converge on a single meta-finding: **governance is the defining bottleneck of the agent landscape in April 2026, but governance is failing at different layers for different platforms.**

- **Infrastructure layer:** Identity governance shipped (Okta, ConductorOne, Microsoft toolkit), action governance absent. 95% of enterprise agents are not in production. The 5% that are running have no governance.
- **Platform layer (Google):** Workspace Studio structural limitations confirmed independently (no loops, no external triggers, no task assignment). Practitioner silence at 31 days = definitive signal. Cloud Next is the reset point.
- **Vertical SaaS layer (Salesforce):** Builder Gap approaching Level 3 — Headless 360 serves pro-code developers, abandons low-code Trailblazer base. Autonomy walkback confirmed by AI COO. TDX practitioner-direct window: April 22-25 (not 20-22 as predicted).
