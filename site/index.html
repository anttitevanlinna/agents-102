The Gap

The gap is between strategy and competence.

Most organizations have an AI strategy. The board has seen the McKinsey report. A governance framework has been approved. An initiative portfolio is underway. These are the right moves. But without hands-on agent competence, they tend to produce governance of something nobody in the room has actually touched. Agents are different from earlier transformations in one specific way: they are too new and too specific to each organization to picture from a strategy deck alone. You could envision a digital company without personally building software. You cannot envision an agentic company without seeing what agents do with your actual data. The vision emerges when people build.

Without agent competence

  • Governance frameworks that govern an abstraction
  • Initiative portfolios that produce slides
  • Transformation that stays at the strategy layer
  • Strategy disconnected from what agents actually do

With agent competence

  • Your people have built real agents with your data
  • Leadership has seen what's possible firsthand
  • Strategy grounded in lived experience
  • Transformation driven by competence, not consultancy
