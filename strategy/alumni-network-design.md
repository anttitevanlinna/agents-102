# Alumni Network Design — Bosser Agent Builders

How the Bootstrap cohort compounds into a network that outlives any single training. Business-level delivery/commercial design — not curriculum. Curriculum ships the identity ("Agent builder"); this doc ships the infrastructure that makes the identity mean something on Tuesday morning six months after graduation.

## The insight

The best training programs are alumni networks with a curriculum attached — not curricula with an alumni list. Harvard Business School sells the MBA; what sticks is the classmates. Y Combinator runs a 3-month program and a 15-year network. Corporate training programs under-invest here; the best ones quietly win on it. Agents 102 has the conditions to build a network Nordic builder leaders can't get anywhere else — practitioners who went through the same chat-to-systems leap, with real deployments to compare notes on, in a market where no such peer group exists today.

## Two distinct mechanisms

Alumni-facing work splits cleanly into two:

### 1. Alumni → cohort = video, not live

**The cohort-facing moment is a recorded video interview, not a live appearance.** 5 minutes, well-coached, produced once, plays to every cohort for a year. Scales infinitely, zero scheduling risk, quality controllable, refresh annually with the year's best story.

**Two slots:**
- **Module 1 close — direction-setter.** An alum from a past cohort (ideally 6–12 months post-training) leads with *"I was exactly where you are on day 1"* and talks about what the M1 joy turned into. Coached hard to NOT open with the highlight reel — that would make the student feel behind and steal M1's joyful creation mood.
- **Module 8 close — identity witness.** A different alum (ideally 12+ months post-training) speaks to what the *Agent builder* identity meant in year one. Comes right after the sponsor confers the name. Three-beat sequence: sponsor names them → video alum says *"welcome in"* → graduate receives first invitation to the in-person alumni gathering.

**Cost:** honorarium per recorded interview (professional speaking gig, not a favor). One session produces reusable content across multiple cohorts; amortises fast. Usage rights covered by the honorarium.

### 2. Alumni → alumni = in-person, peer density is the product

**The actual network happens at alumni-only gatherings.** Cohort graduates get invited at Module 8 close. Alumni go because *other alumni* go — the new cohort isn't the draw; the peers are.

**Recommended format:** **demo night, not dinner.** Meeting room, catering, drinks, every alum does a 3-min show-and-tell of what their agent does now. Cheaper than a restaurant (~€30/head vs ~€150), higher work-intensity, peer-density-optimised. Nordic engineers and builder-leaders show up for demos in a way they don't always for dinners. Self-organised dinner after for anyone who wants to keep going.

**Cadence:** semi-annual at first (Helsinki). Expand to other Nordic capitals once a single city's alumni base reaches ~30–40.

## The dinner economics — phased

Nordic SVPs and VPs will pay for their own dinner if asked; they do so regularly for industry meetups and networking events. But asking them to in the first 2–3 years would be a strategic mistake.

| Phase | Bosser covers | Alumni cover | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1–2** (network seeding) | Full event — venue, catering, drinks | Nothing | Network doesn't exist yet; free event = low friction = people show up. Build muscle. Gift-shaped, not transactional — creates the referral-next-year kind of debt. |
| **Year 3+** (network established) | Venue and drinks | Their own meal (if dinner format) | Being at the gathering IS the point by then, not the food. Paying filters for seriousness. Companies expense it as professional development. |
| **Year 5+** (network at scale) | Potentially zero — sponsor-funded | Their own | A customer or partner sponsors the event to associate with the network. Commercial upside. |

**Rory's law:** people don't value free things, but they value *gifts* differently from free. The first event is a gift; the fifth is a subscription. The transition from gift → subscription has to be timed well — too early and the network never coalesces; too late and Bosser subsidises indefinitely.

**Back-of-envelope cost (year 1):** 20 alumni × €100 all-in (venue share, catering, drinks) = €2,000 per gathering × 2 gatherings/year = €4,000/year. Less than a single prospect-call's time. This is the cheapest possible investment in network compounding; do not penny-pinch it.

## Motivations for alumni (ordered by leverage)

Why would busy senior leaders show up — to record a video for the cohort, or to attend the in-person gathering?

1. **Peers.** A Bootstrap alum who engages with the network over 18 months ends up knowing agent builders across 10–30 Nordic companies at their level. That network is the compensation. Everything else is garnish. **This only works if we design it** — cohorts leaving with real contact info, alumni signal channel (shared Slack / Signal group), intros made explicitly.
2. **Being asked.** Senior people say yes to things that imply status. "Would you record a 5-min interview for our next cohort?" is a compliment before it's an ask.
3. **Demo opportunity.** Engineers and builder-leaders do conference talks for less than €500. A 3-min slot in a peer room that *gets it* is actually a gift.
4. **Coaching from Antti.** A 30-min pre-call to shape a story is high-value coaching for busy leaders who rarely get structured reflection on their own work.
5. **Learning loop.** Alumni attending the gathering see what the curriculum evolved into, pick up new ideas from this year's cohort and each other.
6. **Visible ladder.** Alumni speaker → paid advisor → co-delivered training with Antti → Champion in Step 3 of the 5-step funnel. The video interview is rung 1 of a real progression.
7. **Honorarium.** Modest fee for the recorded interview. Signals professional engagement, not favor. Does not motivate attendance on its own.

**The deepest honesty:** the alumni program IS the wedge for building the network, not a perk after it. If we don't design the network (peer introductions, signal channel, gathering cadence), speaking becomes a favor to Antti and the second cohort is harder to staff than the first.

## Infrastructure needed to make the network real

Without these, the program collapses back to "Antti asks favors":

- **Alumni signal channel** — private Slack or Signal group. Joined at M8 close. Low-traffic, high-value: job referrals, agent war stories, "has anyone seen X." This is the compounding substrate.
- **Alumni contact directory** — simple list (name, company, what their agent does, opt-in contact method). Every graduate gets it at M8 close. Updated annually.
- **Gathering calendar** — semi-annual, dates set 6 months ahead so alumni can protect them.
- **Video library** — 2–4 alumni interviews at any time, refreshed annually. Paired: M1 direction-setters (newer alumni) and M8 identity witnesses (tenured alumni).
- **Alumni→prospect intros** — prospects considering Bosser can ask to speak with a past alum. Alumni who consent become social proof in sales; their agreeing signals the network has value.

## Connection to the 5-step funnel

Bootstrap → Make Your Own → Champions → Scale → Drive into Value Chains. The alumni network is NOT Step 2; it's the connective tissue across all five.

- Bootstrap alumni → natural Step 2 candidates (Make Your Own, change strategy)
- Bootstrap alumni → natural Champions (Step 3 train-the-trainer)
- Bootstrap alumni at different companies → mutual support during Scale (Step 4)
- Bootstrap alumni across customers → source of Value Chain case studies (Step 5)

Without the network, each step has to be re-sourced. With the network, each step draws from the same growing pool.

## Commercial implications

- **Sponsor pitch gets a new leaf.** "Your sponsor attends the training, and your graduates join a network of Nordic agent builders that doesn't exist anywhere else." Differentiation vs. McKinsey/Deloitte training offerings.
- **Alumni-as-advisors.** Soft commercial tie. Alumni who want more income can take 1–2 paid advisory calls per quarter with prospects considering Bosser. Bosser takes no cut initially; the introduction IS the value.
- **Network as moat.** A competitor can clone the curriculum. They can't clone two years of accumulated alumni relationships with agent deployments in their pockets.
- **Pricing protection.** A training that comes with an active peer network justifies premium pricing that a training-in-isolation can't.

## What to decide now vs later

**Decide now:**
- Commit to the video-library approach for M1 + M8 touchpoints. Record first two alumni interviews from the F-Secure cohort.
- Set aside €4,000/year for in-person gatherings; schedule first one within 60 days of second Bootstrap cohort.
- Stand up the alumni Slack/Signal channel before the next graduating cohort.

**Decide later (after first in-person gathering):**
- Dinner vs demo night format (run one of each; see which alumni prefer).
- Expansion beyond Helsinki (needs critical mass per city first).
- Formal alumni→advisor commercial program (let it emerge, don't force it).

**Revisit annually:**
- Phase transition — when does Bosser stop covering meals? Data signal: alumni organically suggesting "we should just pay for ourselves next time."
- Video refresh — which M1 and M8 alumni are this year's best stories?

## Open questions

- Do we need explicit alumni-council structure, or keep it flat? Bias: flat for year 1–2; formalise only if a real coordination need emerges.
- Cross-cohort confidentiality: if alumni from Bosser customer A and Bosser customer B end up in the same network, how do we handle competitive sensitivity? Default: alumni are individuals, not company representatives — they share their own work, not their employer's strategy. Worth a short Chatham House norms line.
- International expansion (Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Baltics): at what scale does it pay to run events beyond Helsinki?
