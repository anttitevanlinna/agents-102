**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — approaching Level 3 but not yet there.**

We now have 7 signals in AML/KYC compliance. However:
- Goldman and JPMorgan lack named individual practitioners
- CleverChain's metrics are vendor-sourced only
- Goldman's exact agent capabilities remain unclear (CNBC-sourced, general press)

**Independent signals that pass all three gates:** Greenlite (#2), Diligent AI (#3), Nasdaq Verafin (#4), WorkFusion (#6) = 4 signals with independent trade press coverage, specific measurable outcomes, and truly agentic (multi-step autonomous) work.

**The mechanism:** AML/KYC compliance is structurally suited for agentic automation because: (1) rules-based with clear decision frameworks, (2) high-volume repetitive tasks (alert triage), (3) verifiable outputs (audit trails), (4) severe talent shortage (75% of FIs increased compliance headcount yet still under-resourced per Nasdaq Verafin survey), (5) regulatory pressure forces formalization that makes agent development tractable.

**Counter-arguments:** (1) Regulatory acceptance remains uncertain — no regulator has formally blessed autonomous AML decisioning; (2) Most "90% productivity" claims trace to vendor sources; (3) The human-in-the-loop requirement for SARs (Suspicious Activity Reports) means full autonomy is structurally impossible in AML; (4) False positive reduction is easier than true positive detection — the hard problem remains.

**What this means:** AML/KYC is the under-represented domain closest to convergence. Two more independent signals with named practitioners and verified metrics would push this to Level 3. The pattern is: talent shortage + rules-based work + verifiable outputs + regulatory audit requirements = agent-friendly domain. This is the same Level 4 meta-pattern identified in finance/accounting.

## Orientation

**Compliance/AML is now the strongest convergence candidate outside of customer service and coding.** With 7 total signals and 4 passing all three quality gates, it's the under-represented domain making the fastest progress toward Level 3. The structural explanation (rules-based + talent-constrained + verifiable + regulatory pressure) maps exactly to the Level 4 meta-pattern. Two more independent signals with named practitioners would confirm convergence.

**Anthropic's distribution is accelerating while Microsoft's stalls.** Self-serve Enterprise, 21 plugins, B2B marketplace, task scheduling — Anthropic is shipping faster than any competitor on the business user surface. Meanwhile Copilot Cowork remains vapor (32 zero-checks) and E7 won't GA until May 1. The gap is widening.

**Google Workspace Studio's real test starts April 1.** When promotional credits expire and real pricing kicks in, we'll see if the no-code agent promise survives contact with enterprise procurement. The current silence (zero independent reviews post-GA) suggests adoption may be slower than the GA timeline implies.

**A2A is a standard nobody uses yet.** Despite massive industry backing and growing SDK maturity (Spring AI integration is significant for Java shops), zero production deployments after 11 months makes this the most over-committed, under-deployed protocol in the agent space.

## Meta-Learning

Compliance/AML research is more productive through domain trade publications (RegTech Analyst, PaymentsJournal, FinTech Magazine, A-Team Insight) than through general web search. The RegTech press actually understands what an agent does vs. a chatbot — rare among trade publications. This source channel should be prioritized for future compliance convergence testing.
