## Findings

### Track A: Microsoft Agent 365 Post-GA Reality

#### A1 — Agent 365 GA: Autonomous agents NOT covered — OBO only at launch
**Source:** https://ragnarheil.de/microsoft-agent-365-what-it-cant-do-yet-limitations-you-need-to-know/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** May 8, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** At GA on May 1, Agent 365 covers only OBO (On-Behalf-Of) agents — agents that act in the context of a specific human user. Autonomous agents (Agent-to-Agent, Agent-to-Tool — the main use case for operations and finance unattended workflows) remain in "Frontier Preview only" with undefined licensing. Shadow agent discovery requires manual one-by-one onboarding (no automated conversion). Pre-GA Studio agents cannot auto-inherit new Entra Agent IDs — rebuild/republish required. Third-party agents (ChatGPT Enterprise, AWS Bedrock) require separate SDK integration and separate vendor licensing.
**Evidence level:** Level 2
**Key claims:** Autonomous agents = Frontier Preview only at GA; OBO-only scope confirmed; no bulk migration path for pre-existing agents; Agent 365 license does NOT grant E5-level Purview or Defender

#### A2 — E7 multiplexing audit risk: May 2026 Product Terms expansion
**Source:** https://rixmind.com/microsoft-365-e7-agent365-multiplexing-itam-2026/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** May 6, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Microsoft's May 2026 Product Terms update explicitly expanded multiplexing definitions to cover AI agents, RPA bots, attended and unattended automation. Organizations with existing automation portfolios now face audit risk for automation built without licensing review under the new terms.
**Evidence level:** Level 2
**Key claims:** May 2026 Product Terms = new multiplexing scope; existing automation portfolios at audit risk; E7 consumption costs still unpublished (SAMexpert, March 2026)

**Corroborating source (prior cycle, still current):** https://samexpert.com/microsoft-365-e7-licensing-guide/ — [practitioner analysis] (March 11, 2026): No per-agent consumption cost baseline published; effective cost >$200/user/month with consumption; bundle math works only if all four components actively used.

#### A3 — Copilot Studio CUA: Java, Citrix, Electron excluded at GA
**Source:** https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/copilot-studio-computer-use-agents-ga-deep-dive — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** May 2026

#### B6 — SAP API policy blocks external agents: forced Joule gateway ("walled garden")
**Source:** https://www.techzine.eu/blogs/applications/141323/sap-blocks-external-ai-agents-salesforce-and-servicenow-dont/ — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** May 13, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** SAP API Policy v4/2026 Section 2.2.2 explicitly prohibits external AI systems from independently scheduling or executing API calls. All agentic use cases must route through Joule via Agent-to-Agent (A2A) communication — creating mandatory double inference (increased latency + cost). SAP's own CTO acknowledged deterministic processes lose certainty when inference layers are involved — an internal contradiction. Partners at Sapphire used "lock-in" and "walled garden" language. Contrast: Salesforce (60+ MCP tools, no gateway required) and ServiceNow (Action Fabric MCP, open) both explicitly open their APIs to external agents.
**Evidence level:** Level 2
**Key claims:** External agents blocked by API policy; double-inference mandatory; partner/attendee "lock-in" reaction; SAP CTO acknowledged the determinism problem

#### B7 — Agentforce back-office "quiet failure" modes — independent consultancy analysis
**Source:** https://www.siroccogroup.com/back-office-agents-and-quiet-failure/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** May 4, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Sirocco Group (independent consultancy, no Valoir affiliation) independently documented five Agentforce back-office failure modes: (1) silent error propagation — no complaint ≠ correct, surfaces in quarterly audits; (2) data quality — agents act confidently on inconsistent Salesforce data without flagging anomalies; (3) institutional knowledge loss — humans only see difficult cases, easy-ticket training stream eliminated; (4) governance gap — audit logs show what happened, not whether it should have; (5) European regulatory risk — uniform agent logic across jurisdictions creates GDPR/DORA policy drift. Three prerequisites required before back-office deployment.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single consultancy, independent)
**Key claims:** Convergence with Valoir's 31% active finding from independent angle; five specific named failure modes; GDPR/DORA regulatory drift risk is new

## Orientation

Three structural patterns confirmed across all tracks: (1) The 27-day Microsoft Agent 365 post-GA deployment silence matches the pre-GA pattern exactly — autonomous agents are excluded from GA scope, TCO is unknowable, and the adoption ceiling is structural (3.3% Copilot penetration, negative NPS). (2) ServiceNow is converging toward Level 3 for IT service desk specifically, but the "every major business function" claim remains Level 0 for all non-IT functions — standard Knowledge 2026 announcement-to-deployment gap. SAP Joule is now the clearest vapor story in enterprise AI (3% production adoption by its own customers, locked API gateway). (3) The "floor redesign" gap has a structural explanation for the first time: Cutler's fiefdom mechanism (the people who built organizational complexity are the same people whose authority depends on it) + Mollick's KPI/IT blocker + Tieto's governance finding converge on WHY published cases don't exist — making the absence a finding, not a research failure.
