Discussion: What's the ceiling for "engineering for broad learning"?

**The ceiling is human judgment about what's worth learning.** Not attention (absorption bottleneck — has engineering solutions), not generation (solved), not verification (being solved). The ceiling is: who decides what's worth learning?

**Scaling progression:**
- 1 team: one person curates judgment. Works.
- 10 teams: 10 curators, mostly aligned. Works with coordination.
- 100 teams: judgment diverges. Teams learn contradictory things. Who arbitrates?
- 1000 teams: meta-absorption — the learning system generates more learning than anyone can curate.

**The recursive problem:** A system that learns broadly eventually generates more learning than any human can curate. The meta-learning question ("what should we learn?") becomes harder than the original learning question.

**Unlearning is the hardest part.** A system engineered for broad learning accumulates stale patterns faster than any human system. The zombie stat audit (MIT 95% propagated across 10+ files) is exactly this at small scale. At 100x scale, how do you unlearn? Stale patterns propagate at machine speed but auditing them requires human judgment.

**The straight answer:** It scales until the meta-learning problem gets harder than the original learning problem. But that ceiling is high — most organizations haven't started learning at the 1-team level. The value gap between "not learning at all" and "hitting the meta-learning ceiling" is enormous.
