## Findings

### Finding 1: Credit System Replaces Promotional Limits (March 31 Cliff)

**Source:** [Google Workspace Updates Blog — AI Expanded Access](https://workspaceupdates.googleblog.com/2026/02/google-workspace-ai-expanded-access.html) — [vendor blog]
**Date:** February 5, 2026
**Agent level:** Personal / Team
**What:** After March 31, 2026, Workspace Studio's promotional higher limits expire. Standard access continues free in Business/Enterprise plans but at reduced limits. To maintain higher usage: AI Expanded Access add-on (2,000 monthly Flow credits) or AI Ultra Access (25,000 monthly Flow credits, includes Deep Think). Credits don't roll over. Pricing is localized — not publicly listed. This creates a three-tier system: standard (free, low limits) → Expanded → Ultra. The credit model means agents have a per-execution cost, fundamentally changing the economics for business users building automations.

**Evidence level:** Level 0 (vendor blog announcing own product pricing)
**Key claims:**
- Promotional limits expire March 31, 2026
- AI Expanded Access: 2,000 monthly Flow credits
- AI Ultra Access: 25,000 monthly Flow credits + Deep Think
- Credits don't roll over monthly
- Standard access remains free at lower limits

**CTO implication:** The credit cliff is 10 days away. Any org that built Studio agents during the promotional period may see them throttled or stop running. This is a forcing function — orgs must decide whether to purchase add-ons or accept reduced automation. The per-credit model also makes it harder to justify "every employee builds agents" — the costs scale with usage.

## Orientation

**The March 31 credit cliff is the biggest near-term story.** Google is moving from "free higher limits" to a three-tier credit system. This affects every business user who built agents during the promotional window. The credit model (2,000/month Expanded, 25,000/month Ultra, no rollover) means agent economics are now per-execution — fundamentally different from Microsoft/OpenAI's per-seat pricing. For high-volume automations, this could be expensive. For light usage, the free tier may suffice. But the uncertainty (no public pricing, credits don't roll over) creates planning difficulty for CTOs.

**Mattel's PQA agent at Cloud Next could be significant** — it's the first multi-system (Google Cloud AI + Jira) business agent from a named enterprise. But it's a conference session, not a deployment report. Wait for April 22-24.

**The capacity + credit cliff combination is dangerous.** Users already hitting capacity limits will now also hit credit limits. Two resource constraints stacking on top of each other. If an org's agents stop mid-month because credits ran out AND capacity errors continue, the "every employee builds agents" narrative collapses.

**ADK 2.0 and GEAR are supply-side investments** — Google building tools and training for developers. But the gap remains: the business user surface (Workspace Studio) is capacity-constrained, credit-limited, and governance-free. The developer surface (Vertex + ADK) has governance but no business user path. The two-tier problem identified in cycle 22 persists.
