# Agents 102: Eight Modules to Practitioner

> Your people build real agents with your data, your policies, your systems. Not slides. Not theory. Working systems they can show the rest of the organization.

## What This Is

Agents 102 is an intensive hands-on program for 20 people. Everyone works on one agreed company initiative — something familiar enough that everyone in the room can evaluate whether the agent's output is good. By the end, participants have built working agent systems and understand both the power and the limits.

**Format:** Exercise-led. Every concept is learned by building, not by listening. The exercises ARE the curriculum — there are no slides.

**Who it's for:** Mixed groups work best. CTOs, product managers, team leads, practitioners — anyone who can have a lengthy conversation with an LLM and wants to understand what happens when you give it tools, memory, and real work to do.

**What participants need:** A laptop and curiosity. No coding experience required.

## Build, Break, Understand

### Module 1: Getting Going

Everyone builds their first agent — a real one, working with real data. The focus is creative abundance with control: what becomes possible when you combine generative AI with clear guardrails and your own domain knowledge.

**What you build:** A personal site agent that takes your LinkedIn profile and creates something genuinely better than copy-paste — with guardrails that enforce differentiation and storytelling.

**The teaching moment:** You keep pushing until the agent fabricates. It will invent things about you, your experience, your skills. You catch it because you're the world's leading expert on your own life. That tension — useful AND unreliable — is the foundation everything else builds on.

### Module 2: Building Agent Systems

The personal agent was one step. Now you build a system: multiple steps, each with a purpose, working together on the company initiative everyone agreed on.

**What you build:** A multi-step pipeline for the company initiative. The agent doesn't just generate — it researches, structures, drafts, and refines. Each step has clear inputs, outputs, and quality expectations.

**Why this matters:** This is where participants cross from "chatbot user" to "system builder." The shift from a single prompt to a designed pipeline is the conceptual leap that changes how people think about AI.

### Module 3: Multi-Agent Systems

One agent with multiple steps is useful. Multiple agents coordinating is powerful — and introduces new failure modes.

**What you build:** You split your pipeline into specialized agents. One researches, one structures, one writes, one reviews. They hand off work like an assembly line — each agent focused on what it does best.

**Why this matters:** Coordination is where most real-world agent projects either succeed or fail. Participants experience both: the power of specialization and the pain of agents that don't understand each other.

## Discipline, Landscape, Flywheel

### Module 4: Security

An agent that works but isn't secure is worse than no agent at all. This module makes that visceral.

**What happens:** A controlled exercise where participants see a compliance violation happen in real time — their agent accesses or generates something it shouldn't, using their organization's actual policies as the test.

**What you build:** Security guardrails retrofitted onto the agent system built in earlier modules. Participants experience "I built something that works but I can't trust yet" — and then fix it.

> **Your organization's policies go in here.** This is the first plug point: participants integrate their own security policies, data classification rules, and compliance requirements. Not our defaults — yours.

### Module 5: Output Quality and Hallucination Control

The fabrication you caught by eye in Module 1 — now you learn to catch it systematically.

**What you build:** An evaluation system for the company initiative agent. Not "does it look good?" but "can I measure whether it's good?" Evals as strategic steering: making output more differentiated, not just correct.

**Why this matters:** This is where participants shift from hoping the agent works to knowing it works. The eval becomes a tool for making output strategically better — more differentiated, more aligned with what the organization actually needs.

### Module 6: Evaluations at Scale

Individual evals catch individual problems. Evaluation systems catch patterns. This module builds the infrastructure for ongoing quality.

**What you build:** A systematic evaluation pipeline — automated checks that run every time the agent produces output. The same discipline that software teams apply to code, applied to agent output.

**Why this matters:** This is the bridge from "experiment" to "production." An agent without evals is a demo. An agent with evals is a system you can trust and improve over time.

### Module 7: Agent Platforms

Where do agents live in the real world? This module zooms out from building to deploying.

**What you learn:** The current agent platform landscape — what's available, what's hype, what actually works in production. How to evaluate platforms against your organization's specific requirements. No vendor recommendations — a framework for making your own assessment.

**Why this matters:** Participants who built agents in Modules 1-6 now understand what they need from a platform. They can evaluate vendor claims against their own experience — not from a slide deck, but from having built the thing.

### Module 8: Agents Building Agents — The Flywheel

The final module. The insight that changes how participants think about scaling.

**What you build:** An agent that creates other agents. Code-generating agents as the meta-tool. Participants experience three things: the multiplication effect (one agent creates many), the self-improvement loop (agents that make themselves better), and the organizational capability shift (this isn't a one-time project — it's a new way of working).

**The deliverable:** An executive recommendation for the sponsoring leader, co-created live by the group. Not a report about what they learned — a concrete proposal for what to do next, grounded in two days of building.

## What Comes After

Bootstrap is Step 1 of five. After two days, participants have built working agents and understand the landscape. The next steps:

**Make Your Own** — A cohort of your change leaders designs the rollout. 4-6 weeks. Change strategy lab with mentoring.

**Champions** — The strongest people from Bootstrap learn to facilitate and carry it forward. Train-the-trainer.

**Scale** — Champions run it inside your organization. Your priorities, your pace. No external dependency.

**Put Agents to Work** — Map your processes. Rework them with agents. Learn from peers in the network who have already done it.

All materials are co-branded with your organization. You co-own everything. If you stop working with us, you keep it all.

## Design Principles

- **Exercise-led, not lecture-led.** Concepts emerge from doing. We never explain something before the exercise that demonstrates it.
- **Real data, not toy data.** Participants use their own profiles, their own organization's policies, their own domain. Not sample_data.csv.
- **Show the failure mode.** For every capability, participants see what goes wrong without the discipline. Security: the violation. Quality: the undetected fabrication. Coordination: the agent that misunderstands.
- **Plug points for your organization.** Security policies, data classification, compliance requirements, evaluation criteria, domain knowledge — these are clearly marked places where your reality replaces our defaults.
- **Incremental complexity.** Each exercise adds one thing. Never two new concepts at once.
- **The four pillars are woven through, not bolted on.** LLM-based, secure, lifecycle-managed, reliable — every module touches all four.
