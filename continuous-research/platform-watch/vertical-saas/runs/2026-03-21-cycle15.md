### Reasoning Model Hallucination Paradox (Cross-Platform Insight)
Reasoning models (GPT-5 thinking, Claude extended thinking, DeepSeek-R1) are WORSE at factual accuracy despite being better at logic:
- OpenAI o3: 33% hallucination on PersonQA (vs 16% for o1). o4-mini: 48%.
- DeepSeek R1: 14.3% (vs V3 base: 3.9%) — 4x worse.
  **Source:** [Suprmind benchmarks](https://suprmind.ai/hub/ai-hallucination-rates-and-benchmarks/) — [practitioner analysis]
  **Date:** 2025-2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (academic/benchmark convergence)

**Implication:** The "smartest" models are the least reliable on facts. For customer-facing agent deployment, factual reliability must be tested separately from reasoning capability.

### Supervision Architecture > Model Quality
- Vertical Insure achieved "zero customer-facing hallucinations" — not by eliminating model hallucinations but by catching them via supervision layer.
  **Source:** [Yuma AI](https://yuma.ai/blogs/ai-hallucinations-in-customer-service-why-quality-control-architecture-matters) — [practitioner direct]
- Industry emerging standard: source-attributed answers, 95%+ accuracy on test suites, observability layers.
- 89% of orgs have implemented observability for agents; quality issues are primary production barrier (32%).
**Evidence level:** Level 2

## Orientation
The biggest story is the gap between vendor claims and practitioner evidence. Salesforce's own Help Portal at 62% (vs 80% target and 93% claimed) is the most revealing data point in the entire vertical SaaS space. The reasoning model hallucination paradox is a cross-platform insight that affects all agent deployments. Nordic remains pre-deployment except for Finnair. Consolidation is accelerating — expect the platform landscape to look different in 6 months.
