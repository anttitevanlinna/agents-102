# OODA stance-axis test — Huntley & Yegge — 2026-05-13

**Hypothesis tested:** 5 stance-axes shared by practitioners who got agentic coding working:
1. Scientist-not-engineer disposition
2. Writing-down IS the work
3. Ship cheaper than argue
4. Taste as the irreducible asset
5. Reagents-not-opex cost frame

**Freshness window:** Nov 2025 – May 2026 inclusive. Older pieces marked as historical context only.

---

**Per-axis verdict:**

- **Axis 1 (scientist disposition): HELD.** The Ralph loop *is* a controlled experiment in production. "Watch the loop as that is where your personal development and learning will come from" — *Everything is a Ralph Loop*, Jan 17 2026 ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/loop/), [practitioner direct]). Cursed (historical, Sept 2025) is a 3-month single-variable experiment with the failure mode published: "There's still a lot to be fixed, happy to take pull-requests" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/cursed/), [practitioner direct]). Pattern: run-in-loop, observe, publish, hand the failure mode to the public.

- **Axis 2 (writing-down IS the work): HELD, strongly.** *Porting* (Mar 15 2026) makes the artefact explicit — three Ralph loops where the first two produce *specs with citations back to source*, and only the third implements: "The key theory here is usage of citations in the specifications which tease the file_read tool to study the original implementation" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/porting/), [practitioner direct]). Specs-as-artefact precede implementation. *Back-pressure* generalises: "engineers must focus on preventing the wheel from turning over through back pressure to the generative function" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/pressure/), [practitioner direct]) — the written-down feedback rules are the engineering work, not the code.

- **Axis 3 (ship cheaper than argue): HELD.** From Cursed (historical, Sept 2025): "doing so is faster than explaining yourself. Outcomes speak louder than words" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/cursed/), [practitioner direct]). In-window, *Real* (Feb 27 2026) is the same stance applied to organisational change: "competitive timelines are measured in months, not years" and one founder "cut 2/3rds" of staff to achieve "30x the output" — argued via cited example, not abstract case ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/real/), [practitioner direct]). His publishing cadence itself (10+ in-window posts) is shipping-over-arguing in operation.

- **Axis 4 (taste as irreducible): HELD.** From Cursed: "problems found in cursed can be solved by just running more Ralph loops by skilled operators…who shape it through prompts from their expertise" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/cursed/), [practitioner direct], historical). The skilled-operator framing recurs in-window in *Teleport* (Feb 5 2026): the maturation phase is "understanding what *not* to build—despite newfound capability" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/teleport/), [practitioner direct]). Taste is what selects the loop's target, not what the loop produces.

- **Axis 5 (reagents-not-opex cost frame): HELD, strongly.** Yegge cites Huntley's $4,400/month token spend as the canonical pay-to-play example (*Stevey's Birthday Blog*, Jan 19 2026, [URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/steveys-birthday-blog-34f437139cb5), [practitioner direct] — Yegge on Huntley). Huntley's own framing in *Real* (Feb 27 2026) reframes the unit cost: "the cost of software development is $10.42 an hour" ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/real/), [practitioner direct]) — token spend is measured against software-development-hours-produced, i.e. as research input not opex line. *Teleport* (Feb 5 2026) reinforces the reagents frame implicitly: the "creative psychosis" 2–3 months is a *burn window*, the price of which is the value mined.

**Beyond the five:** **Sovereignty as stance-input.** *Warfare* (Mar 16 2026) and *Cogsec* (Mar 17 2026) argue that frontier-lab dependency is a strategic vulnerability ([URL](https://ghuntley.com/warfare/), [URL](https://ghuntley.com/cogsec/), both [practitioner direct]). Local-inference as discipline is an axis the five don't cover — sits adjacent to Axis 5 (cost frame) but the lever is geopolitical not financial. Worth a candidate sixth axis: *control-the-substrate (local model) as agent-coding hygiene.*

**Contradictions:** None against the five. Huntley's rhetoric is dramatic ("software development is dead", *Real*) but he ships the loop posts, the cursed compiler, the decompiled Claude Code, the porting recipe — the practice underwrites the rhetoric.

## Steve Yegge

- *Vibe Coding* (book) co-authored with Gene Kim — referenced by reviewers, no in-window byline-direct quote retrieved.

**Note on Yegge's style:** combative and provocative — "developers still using IDEs by January are bad engineers"; veterans become "interns" (per Latent Space, Dec 26 2025, [URL](https://www.latent.space/p/steve-yegges-vibe-coding-manifesto)). The style is mostly *carrier*, not *obscurer*: each provocation is paired with an artefact (Gas Town, Beads, the orchestrator trilogy) so the rhetorical heat lands on top of demonstrable practice. Where it obscures: the meme-coin and $290k profit story (Wikipedia summary, Jan 2026) creates noise that's easy to mistake for the stance — it's adjacent, not the stance itself.

**Per-axis verdict:**

- **Axis 1 (scientist disposition): HELD.** *Welcome to Gas Town* (Jan 2026) is a self-published iteration log: "Gas Town (in Go) is my fourth complete, functioning orchestrator of 2025" — three failed orchestrators (v1, v2, Beads, Python Gas Town) preceding the working one, with the failures named ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-gas-town-4f25ee16dd04), [practitioner direct]). Public reversal-on-evidence: *Stevey's Birthday Blog* (Jan 19 2026) cites that his March 2025 "Kubernetes for agents" prediction was initially dismissed and is now validated; he publishes the trajectory ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/steveys-birthday-blog-34f437139cb5), [practitioner direct]).

- **Axis 2 (writing-down IS the work): HELD.** *Gas Town* embeds operational philosophy in agent prompts: the "Gastown Universal Propulsion Principle" (GUPP) — "If there is work on your hook, YOU MUST RUN IT" — is rule-as-artefact in the system itself ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-gas-town-4f25ee16dd04), [practitioner direct]). Beads (persistent worker identities in Git) makes workflows reproducible and auditable — the data plane IS the writing-down. *Six New Tips* (Dec 7 2025): "iteratively refined Beads (his issue tracker) by watching agent errors and adding aliases/flags accordingly" ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/six-new-tips-for-better-coding-with-agents-d4e9c86e42a9), [practitioner direct]) — same harness-engineering pattern Hashimoto names, applied to tool surface.

- **Axis 3 (ship cheaper than argue): HELD, strongly.** Four orchestrators in one calendar year (*Welcome to Gas Town*, Jan 2026, [URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-gas-town-4f25ee16dd04), [practitioner direct]) is the practice of the axis. *Vibe Maintainer* (Mar 2026): the entire essay is anti-argument — "if you refuse enough PRs, the community will consider you a dead-end street and begin routing around you" — and the operational move is fix-merge / cherry-pick over change-request ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/vibe-maintainer-a2273a841040), [practitioner direct]). 88% PR merge rate via various mechanisms is the data.

- **Axis 4 (taste as irreducible): HELD.** *Vibe Maintainer* (Mar 2026): "roughly 25% of PRs need human evaluation because there's still a thing called taste that current models can't be trusted with" ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/vibe-maintainer-a2273a841040), [practitioner direct]). *Software Survival 3.0* ranks "Minimizing Friction" via designing for "agent UX through iterative desire-path implementation" ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/software-survival-3-0-97a2a6255f7b), [practitioner direct]) — taste relocated from code to tool surface. From Ben Lorica's write-up of Yegge (out-of-window, historical): "Software development with AI becomes a verification process rather than a creation process" — taste as verifier ([URL](https://thedataexchange.media/vibe-coding-chop-steve-yegge/), [practitioner analysis], historical).

- **Axis 5 (reagents-not-opex cost frame): HELD.** *Stevey's Birthday Blog* (Jan 19 2026) names the frame directly via Huntley's example: "Geoffrey Huntley's extreme token spending ($4,400/month) as evidence of pay-to-play productivity boosts he'd previously predicted" ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/steveys-birthday-blog-34f437139cb5), [practitioner direct]). Earlier (Mar 2025 essay, historical context): "$50k/year per developer for LLM spend in 2026, which will separate companies into those with budgets and those without" — the frame is *budget separates competitors*, not opex line. *Welcome to Gas Town* (Jan 2026): "You won't like Gas Town if you ever have to think...about where money comes from" — throughput over efficiency, API spend as infrastructure investment ([URL](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-gas-town-4f25ee16dd04), [practitioner direct]).

**Beyond the five:** **Fleet-level orchestration as a stance, not just a tool.** Yegge's six-wave framing (traditional → completions → chat → coding-agents → agent-clusters → agent-fleets) and his orchestrator-trilogy obsession (Gas Town, Beads, the upcoming Gas City) point to an axis the five don't carry: *the unit of work is the fleet, not the agent.* Hashimoto's harness is one-agent-deep; Yegge's harness is fleet-deep. Worth a candidate sixth axis: *fleet-as-unit, not agent-as-unit.*

Also: **Fork-as-feedback.** *Vibe Maintainer*'s "routing-around-you" risk treats the open-source community fork pressure as a forcing function on maintainer behaviour — a discipline-via-exit axis adjacent to Axis 1 (scientist) but operationally distinct.

**Contradictions:** None against the five. The meme-coin / $290k profit story (Jan 2026) is adjacent noise — *not* a contradiction of the stance, but evidence that Yegge's persona-shaped publishing can absorb non-stance signal into the trajectory. Audit-trail discipline (Beads, Gas Town's auditability) is consistent with Axis 1 even when the meme-coin layer isn't.

## Summary (<150 words)

**Stance-convergence verdict: STRONG across all five axes for both practitioners.** Huntley and Yegge both clearly hold Axes 1–5 in-window. Huntley's evidence is densest on Axes 2 (specs-with-citations, back-pressure, porting recipe) and 5 (his $4,400/month is Yegge's canonical example). Yegge's evidence is densest on Axes 3 (four orchestrators in a year) and 4 (vibe-maintainer's 25% taste carve-out, agent-UX design). Yegge's combative style mostly *carries* signal — each provocation is paired with a shipped artefact — and only the meme-coin story is genuine noise. Two beyond-the-five candidates surfaced: *control-the-substrate / local-model sovereignty* (Huntley) and *fleet-as-unit, not agent-as-unit* (Yegge). The Huntley–Yegge pair, paired with the Hashimoto–Ball pair, brings the five-axis hypothesis closer to L3 convergence than the four practitioners alone would suggest.
