## Findings

### Finding 1: Training Landscape Maps to a Clear Phase 1-3 Ceiling — Phase 4 Remains Absent

**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence — 15+ independent training programs examined, consistent pattern)

Mapping every training program found against the four-phase progression reveals a clear ceiling. Virtually all available training — from vendor academies to university certificates to MOOC specializations — teaches Phase 1-2 (prompting, individual agent building). A smaller subset reaches Phase 3 (multi-agent orchestration). Nobody teaches Phase 4 (designing verification systems where agents check agents at organizational scale).

**Phase 1 (CollabAI — learn to prompt):**
- OpenAI Academy: AI Foundations course — "core AI skills applied to real tasks: planning, summarising with citations, drafting workflows" — piloted with Walmart, Accenture, BCG, John Deere. Source: https://academy.openai.com/ [vendor education portal]
- Anthropic Academy: Claude 101, AI Fluency courses — "learn how to use Claude for everyday work tasks." Source: https://anthropic.skilljar.com/ [vendor education portal]
- 68% of enterprises now provide prompt engineering training to staff. Source: https://sqmagazine.co.uk/prompt-engineering-statistics/ [general press — bare fact only]

**Phase 2 (Compound Engineering — solo + AI, build agents):**
- Andrew Ng's "Agentic AI" course on DeepLearning.AI (Oct 2025): four design patterns — Reflection, Tool Use, Planning, Multi-Agent Collaboration. Vendor-neutral, raw Python. Free to audit, $25/month for certificate. Source: https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/agentic-ai/ [practitioner direct — Andrew Ng]
- Anthropic Academy: Claude Code in Action, Skills in Claude Code, Sub-agents in Claude Code, MCP development (intro + advanced). 13 free courses total, launched March 2026. Source: https://anthropic.skilljar.com/ [vendor education portal]
- LangChain Academy: AI Agents in LangGraph (Harrison Chase), Long-Term Agentic Memory. Source: https://academy.langchain.com/collections [vendor education portal]
- Udacity Agentic AI Nanodegree: "from advanced prompting to designing agentic workflows with Routing and Parallelization." Source: https://www.udacity.com/course/agentic-ai--nd900 [vendor education portal]
- Udemy: 6-week intensive covering OpenAI Agents SDK, CrewAI, LangGraph, Autogen. Source: https://www.udemy.com/course/the-complete-agentic-ai-engineering-course/ [vendor education portal]
- Zero To Mastery AI Agents Bootcamp: "Build multi-agent systems using CrewAI, LangGraph, MCP." Source: https://zerotomastery.io/courses/ai-agents-bootcamp/ [vendor education portal]

**Phase 3 (SwarmAI — multi-agent orchestration, team-level):**
- Johns Hopkins Agentic AI Certificate: 16-week program, four phases including "multi-agent frameworks" and "reinforcement learning in agents." Requires STEM background. Source: https://online.lifelonglearning.jhu.edu/jhu-certificate-program-agentic-ai [academic/research]
- NVIDIA NCP-AAI Certification: 60-70 question proctored exam. Covers "agent orchestration, multi-agent frameworks, evaluation, observability, deployment, reliability guardrails." Requires 1-2 years production AI experience. Source: https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/learn/certification/agentic-ai-professional/ [vendor certification]
- IBM/Coursera "Building AI Agents and Agentic Workflows" Specialization: "multi-agent systems that collaborate through orchestration, CrewAI for structuring agents, tasks, and tools." Source: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/building-ai-agents-and-agentic-workflows [vendor education portal]
- Coursera "Build Powerful AI Agents with OpenAI Tools" Professional Certificate: "orchestrating complex multi-agent systems using the OpenAI Agents SDK." Source: https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/build-ai-agents-with-openai-tools [vendor education portal]

**Phase 4 (Scaled AI — verification systems, agents check agents): NOT FOUND.**
No training program, course, bootcamp, or certification explicitly teaches how to design verification architectures where agents evaluate other agents' output at organizational scale. No program teaches how to manage the absorption bottleneck (human capacity to process agent-generated output). No program teaches the meta-skill of designing systems where humans set goals and agents do the heavy lifting with layered verification.

### Finding 2: Eval Training Is Emerging — But Stops at Component Level, Not System Architecture

**Evidence level:** Level 2 (two practitioner-led programs, strong signal)

The closest thing to "verification architecture" training is the emerging discipline of AI evals — but it focuses on evaluating individual agent performance, not designing organizational-scale verification systems.

- **Hamel Husain & Shreya Shankar, "AI Evals for Engineers & PMs" (Maven):** The #1 highest-grossing course on Maven. Trained 2,000+ PMs and engineers at 500+ companies. Covers "error analysis, synthetic data generation, LLM-as-judge evals, RAG debugging, CI/CD integration." Explicitly designed for both engineers AND product managers. Next cohort: March 2026. Source: https://maven.com/parlance-labs/evals [practitioner direct]
- **Reforge "AI Evals" course:** "Practical introduction to AI Evaluation for product managers... AI PRDs, golden datasets, eval rubrics, trace analysis, automated evaluators." Targets PMs through VPs of Product. Source: https://www.reforge.com/courses/ai-evals [practitioner direct]
- **DeepLearning.AI "Evaluating AI Agents" (with Arize AI):** "Build an AI agent, add observability to visualize and debug its steps, evaluate its performance component-wise." Source: https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/evaluating-ai-agents/ [practitioner direct — Andrew Ng platform]

**Critical gap:** These teach how to evaluate ONE agent's output. They do not teach how to design a SYSTEM where Agent B verifies Agent A's work, Agent C audits the verification, and a human reviews only exceptions. That's the Phase 4 skill — and it's not taught anywhere.

**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence — examined all major platforms, consistent pattern)

The Reforge and Maven evals courses (Finding 2) come closest for PMs, but they teach evaluation of individual agents, not system-level verification architecture.

## Counter-Evidence: Is Phase 1-2 Sufficient?

The confirmation bias guard requires asking: maybe prompt engineering training IS enough for most organizations?

**Evidence that Phase 1-2 delivers value:**
- "Organisations implementing structured prompt engineering frameworks report 67% productivity improvements" — Source: https://sqmagazine.co.uk/prompt-engineering-statistics/ [general press — unverified statistic, likely vendor-sourced]
- 68% of firms now provide PE training — massive adoption suggests it delivers enough value to justify investment. Source: same.

**Counter-counter:** These statistics are unverifiable (no named methodology, likely survey-based with vendor sponsorship). More importantly, they measure productivity at the INDIVIDUAL level (Phase 1-2). They don't address organizational-scale agent deployment (Phase 3-4). The 95% pilot failure rate (MIT, cited by Yi Zhou) suggests that individual productivity gains don't translate to organizational transformation without higher-phase skills.

**Honest assessment:** Phase 1-2 training IS sufficient for individual productivity gains. It is NOT sufficient for organizational transformation. The question is what the buyer cares about — and a CTO asking "how do I transform my organization with AI" needs Phase 3-4, not better prompting.

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

1. **Any training program explicitly teaching "agents check agents" verification architecture.** This is the core Phase 4 skill. It does not exist as formal training anywhere. The Hamel/Shreya evals course and Reforge come closest but stop at single-agent evaluation.

2. **Any curriculum progressing from prompting → individual agent building → multi-agent orchestration → verification system design → agents building agents.** This complete progression does not exist. Courses jump from prompting to multi-agent orchestration (skipping verification architecture entirely) or stop at Phase 2.

3. **Any non-engineer training beyond "how to prompt."** Business people can learn to prompt (Phase 1). They cannot learn to design agent workflows, specify verification criteria, or architect multi-agent systems — because no training exists for this audience at this level.

4. **Any training on managing the absorption bottleneck.** When agents generate output faster than humans can review, how do you design systems to manage this? Nobody teaches it. The phrase "absorption bottleneck" doesn't appear in any training curriculum found.

5. **Any training equivalent to the practitioner's "Agents 101" progression** — mob AI → compound engineering → swarm AI → scaled AI → agents building agents. This specific progression appears unique. No competing curriculum maps the same journey.

6. **Nordic-specific agentic training programs.** Conferences exist. Formal training beyond vendor workshops does not.

7. **Consulting firms teaching clients agent architecture** (vs. deploying agents FOR clients). The dependency model dominates.

## Orientation

### The Gap Is Real and Massive

The training landscape in March 2026 looks like this:

| Phase | What it teaches | Available training | Target audience |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Phase 1** (CollabAI) | Learn to prompt | Everywhere — OpenAI Academy, Anthropic Academy, every MOOC platform, enterprise programs | Everyone |
| **Phase 2** (Compound) | Build individual agents | Abundant — Andrew Ng, LangChain Academy, Udemy, Coursera specializations, Udacity | Engineers |
| **Phase 3** (Swarm) | Multi-agent orchestration | Emerging — JHU certificate, NVIDIA certification, some Coursera specializations | Engineers with 1-2 years experience |
| **Phase 4** (Scaled AI) | Verification architecture, agents check agents, absorption management | **DOES NOT EXIST** | Nobody |

### Three Structural Gaps

1. **The Phase 4 gap:** Nobody teaches verification system design — the skill that makes agents trustworthy at organizational scale. This is the gap between "I can build an agent" and "I can build a system of agents that my organization trusts."

2. **The non-engineer gap:** Every Phase 2+ program requires engineering skills. Business leaders who need to COMMISSION and GOVERN agent systems (not build them) have no training beyond Phase 1 prompting. The Reforge evals course for PMs is the sole exception, and it covers evaluation, not architecture.

3. **The independence gap:** Consulting firms (McKinsey, Accenture, Deloitte, BCG) are deploying agents FOR clients, not teaching clients to be self-sufficient. 30,000 Accenture people trained on Claude ≠ the client's people trained on Claude. This creates permanent dependency, not organizational competence.

### What This Means for the Research System

This finding validates the practitioner's four-phase progression as a genuinely novel framework. The Phase 4 gap is not just a research insight — it's a market signal:

- **For CTOs:** If your training vendor teaches "prompt engineering" or even "build agents," they're giving you Phase 1-2. You need Phase 3-4 to transform. Nobody is selling Phase 4 training right now.
- **For the training market:** The first program that teaches verification architecture, agent-evaluates-agent patterns, and absorption management to non-engineers has no competition. Literally zero.
- **For the competence-first thesis:** This reinforces the core insight — without Phase 3-4 competence, organizations can't evaluate whether their agent deployments actually work. They're dependent on vendors and consultants who deploy agents they can't verify.
