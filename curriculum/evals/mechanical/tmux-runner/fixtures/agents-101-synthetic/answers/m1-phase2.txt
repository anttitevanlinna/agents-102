Here are all five StoryBrand beats in one go (don't ask one at a time — just take these and regenerate):
- Character (the colleague): a founder or GM who just signed off a pricing or packaging change and now has to make it real without breaking the customers they already have.
- Problem: they have the strategy deck but nobody who owns the messy middle — the rollout, the pilot, the angry SMB account on day three.
- Guide (why me): I run the pilot that de-risks the change before it hits everyone, and I own the handoffs nobody else wants.
- Plan (what working with me feels like): low-friction, informal, decisions made with incomplete data instead of waiting for certainty.
- Success (a year on): the change shipped, churn didn't spike, and the sales org actually knew what to say.
My one-line guide-to-the-colleague: "I live with the consequences of the decision, not just the recommendation."
