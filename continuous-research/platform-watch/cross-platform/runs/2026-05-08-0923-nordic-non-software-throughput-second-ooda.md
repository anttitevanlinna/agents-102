### Danfoss got stronger as a Nordic manufacturing scale case, but still vendor-ecosystem evidence

**Source:** https://cloud.google.com/customers/danfoss — [vendor case study]
**Date:** current page crawled 2026; exact publication date not exposed
**Agent level:** function / operations
**What:** Google Cloud's Danfoss case is richer than the previous Go Autonomous page. Carlos Garcia says horizontal AI platforms could not scale to Danfoss's B2B order needs; the goal was to make as many transactions fully autonomous as possible while keeping seamless human decisions for exceptions. Reported results: up to 80% transactional decision-making/communication automated, average customer response time potentially from 42 hours to near real time, 50% processing-time reduction, up to five systems consolidated into one interface, live in over 20 countries, and average savings of about five minutes per order.
**Evidence level:** Level 0 as vendor case study, but strong Level 2 potential if Danfoss publishes directly. Qualifies on scale/industry; fails source-independence.
**Key claims:** 42 hours to near real time; 80% decisions by AI agent; 50% processing time reduction; >20 countries; domain experts needed to design vertical agents.
