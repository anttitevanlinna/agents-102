# Tesla — AI-Native Organisation at Scale (70K)

**Type:** Designed AI-native | **Size:** ~70,000 | **Evidence:** Deep case (Joe Justice practitioner accounts)
**Key sources:** Joe Justice talks and workshops, Crisp blog (Traton-Scania MobAI)

---

## Core Model

- **Thousand-year goal → KPI cascade:** Goal → company KPIs (crowd-voted by ALL employees) → team sub-KPIs (self-chosen) → daily experiments. No management cascade.
- **Teams of 3-5.** Fluid. Cross-functional (procurement/testing/SW/HW). No specialty silos.
- **Daily self-organisation (Open Space Technology):** Employees choose project daily. Law of Two Feet: leave if not adding value.
- **"Your manager is data."** DSM apps replace what managers did. All data visible to all people all the time.
- **27 hardware updates per week.** Modular architecture enables independent team deployment.
- **Daily rebudgeting.** "Company can't change faster than it can reallocate capital."

## Key Patterns

- **KPI-Driven Autonomy:** 100% decision freedom respecting 15 core rules
- **Information Symmetry Replaces Management:** ALL data visible to ALL people. No status meetings. No budget committees. No management layers for information brokering.
- **Testing as Integration:** Automated testing replaces coordination meetings. 24/7 testing, programmatic forking.
- **No portfolio management.** Emergent portfolio from 70K daily self-selection. Musk as override switch.

## What Transfers

- KPI-driven autonomy model
- Architecture enables organisation (Conway's Law used deliberately)
- Budget speed = learning speed
- Information symmetry as management replacement (most transferable)

## What Doesn't Transfer

- Built greenfield, never transformed legacy
- Musk as forcing function
- Manufacturing context (3 x 12-hour shifts)
- "No portfolio management" overstated — Musk IS portfolio management, just informal
- Survivorship bias (paid speaker)

## Key Insight

**Coordination through information infrastructure at 70K people.** Tesla proves that self-organisation at massive scale is possible — but requires radical information transparency that most organisations' political structures will resist.
