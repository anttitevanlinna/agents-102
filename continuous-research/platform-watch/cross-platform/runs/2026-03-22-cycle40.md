## Orientation

**Vertical SaaS is pulling further ahead of horizontal platforms.** Agentforce's expansion beyond customer service — IRS, Williams-Sonoma, RBC — is the first evidence that vertical agents can work in non-CS domains. But the evidence quality decreases as you move from CS (Level 3 convergence) to government/financial services (Level 2, mostly vendor-sourced). ServiceNow's Autonomous Workforce is the most architecturally serious enterprise agent play — genuinely agentic with governance built in — but still pre-GA with only internal deployment evidence.

**The vertical advantage is deepening:** Agentforce has revenue ($800M ARR), deployment evidence (12K customers), and is expanding into sales/finance/government. ServiceNow has the strongest governance-first architecture. Zendesk is adding self-improving agents. Meanwhile, horizontal platforms (Microsoft, Google, OpenAI) still have zero business agent deployment evidence. The gap between vertical SaaS (real deployments) and horizontal platforms (announcements) is wider than in cycle 25.

**For the CTO question:** If your use case maps to a vertical SaaS domain (customer service, IT service management, CRM-adjacent), the platform decision is straightforward. If it crosses domains, you're still in no-man's land.
