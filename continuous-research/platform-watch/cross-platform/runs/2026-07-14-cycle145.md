### Finding 9: Enterprise 2x Study — Accumulated Use Slope + Review Constraint
**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01904 — [academic/research]
**Date:** July 2026
**What:** 27-month study, 802 developers, 196,212 PRs, single mid-sized AI-forward company. 2x mandate achieved (2.09x baseline April 2026). Mechanism: gains split into one-time adoption jump (+0.143 log points) and accumulated use slope (+0.041 log points per log(cumulative AI-written lines)) — bulk from intensity, not merely starting. Review capacity is the binding constraint: per-reviewer load doubled; automated review rose ~19% → ~84%; substantive human review fell ~39% → ~21%; AI-authored PRs faced +22% longer cycle time. Gains concentrated in newer repos, barely appeared in legacy code. Management tier +86% — AI lowers threshold for leader code contribution.
**Evidence level:** L2 (single enterprise, rigorous quasi-experimental design)
**Key claims for "what comes after 2x":** The accumulated use slope means gains do NOT plateau at 2x — they continue rising with cumulative intensity. The next multiplier is not a new tool but deeper use of existing tools. But review capacity is already the active constraint; teams that don't solve review automation will see gains stall.
