## Orientation

Three developments compound into a single cost-shock signal for enterprise AI planners. First, Fable 5 restoration confirms Anthropic is globally live again (geographic uncertainty resolved) — but now under a new governance structure: pre-release government evaluation access is a named standing condition. Any future Anthropic frontier model may face the same 19-day (or longer) access disruption pattern. Second, the Sonnet 5 cost reality is sharper than the launch materials suggested: per-task cost doubles vs. Sonnet 4.6 and exceeds Opus 4.8, with the tokenizer as the mechanism (English +42%, confirmed by Willison AND The Decoder's Opus 4.7 precedent data). Organizations budgeting Sonnet 5 as a drop-in replacement at "similar cost" will face an immediate 2× shock before the August 31 list-price step-up. Third, Agentforce A2A's beta status for cross-cloud orchestration means the GA announcement on June 15 was materially narrower than most trade press framed it — only within-org Salesforce orchestration is live; the multi-cloud, cross-platform vision is still beta.

The Cursor revenue-vs-share divergence is the most structurally important signal for enterprise AI tool planning: locked multi-year contracts mask real-time preference shifts. This means vendor contract metrics overstate current adoption health — a structural bias in the data that affects how enterprises should read "market share" data across the AI tool category.

Nordic signal sharpens: the BCG finding that 40-50% of Nordic AI spend goes to off-the-shelf (vs. 8-11% globally) is the proximate cause of the Nordic evidence desert. Nordic enterprises are paying for AI but not building with it. The Tieto Copilot Specialization confirms the channel: Nordic SIs are positioning to serve this off-the-shelf demand, not the custom/agentic demand. The training-first intervention is the right response — but the obstacle is organizational priority, not capability awareness.
