## Orientation

**Three shifts since last cycle:**

1. **Market share reversal is the biggest strategic story.** Anthropic at 40% vs OpenAI 27% of enterprise spend fundamentally changes the competitive picture. OpenAI's consumer super-app pivot may further widen the enterprise gap. For CTO advisory: "OpenAI has the most users" is no longer the same as "OpenAI leads in enterprise."

2. **Computer use has its first production evidence — but curated.** Mainstay and Pace are real companies with real deployments, but both were OpenAI launch partners. Independent verification needed. The consumer reliability convergence (Level 3: "impressive but unreliable") is the real assessment for enterprise readiness.

3. **EEA connector restriction is a Nordic-specific structural blocker.** Our Agents 101 participants in Finland will not have access to ChatGPT connectors unless they're on Team/Enterprise plans. This affects curriculum design — exercises requiring connectors need a fallback or require Team+ plans.

**The CTO answer updates:** OpenAI still has the broadest adoption (900M users, 9M business) and the most ambitious platform vision (Frontier). But the evidence gap is widening: 7 weeks post-Frontier-launch with zero independent deployment evidence, write actions with zero adoption, scheduled tasks called "half-baked," and market share declining. The vision-to-execution gap is the defining characteristic of OpenAI's enterprise position.
