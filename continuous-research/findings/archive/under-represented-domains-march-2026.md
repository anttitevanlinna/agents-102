## 1. Operations / Supply Chain

### Lior Ron — CEO, Uber Freight
- **Domain:** Logistics / Supply Chain
- **What they built:** 30+ AI agents ("Insights AI") automating the full shipment lifecycle — procurement, pricing, execution, tracking, settlement. Also deployed agentic AI in customer support as first line of defense.
- **Measurable outcome:** 10-15% reduction in empty miles; support wait times reduced from 5 minutes to 30 seconds; $20B in freight moved through the platform.
- **Source:** https://www.uberfreight.com/en-US/blog/how-ai-will-transform-logistics [vendor blog — Level 0 for claims, but Ron is a practitioner speaking about his own deployment]; https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/05/21/3085594/0/en/Uber-Freight-Launches-Industry-First-AI-Logistics-Network-at-Scale-Ushering-in-a-New-Era-of-Intelligent-Supply-Chains.html [vendor press release — Level 0]; https://ctl.mit.edu/news/how-uber-freight-leveraging-ai-make-truck-routes-more-efficient [academic/practitioner analysis — Level 2]
- **Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, practitioner-reported results, partially verified by MIT CTL)
- **Caveat:** All measurable outcomes are self-reported by the company. No independent verification of the 10-15% empty miles claim found.

### DHL Supply Chain + HappyRobot
- **Domain:** Logistics / Warehousing
- **Named practitioner:** Not individually named — DHL Supply Chain division-level deployment
- **What they built:** HappyRobot AI agents handling appointment scheduling, driver follow-up calls, high-priority warehouse coordination across phone, email, WhatsApp, SMS. Unified AI worker orchestration layer.
- **Measurable outcome:** "Hundreds of thousands of emails and millions of voice minutes annually" handled by agents across several regions. 15% lower operational costs and 20% improved delivery speeds (aggregate DHL AI claims).
- **Source:** https://group.dhl.com/en/media-relations/press-releases/2025/dhl-boosts-operational-efficiency-and-customer-communications-with-happyrobots-ai-agents.html [vendor press release — Level 0]
- **Evidence level:** Level 0-1. The press release is a vendor announcement. No independent verification. The 15%/20% figures appear in multiple secondary sources but all trace back to DHL's own claims.
- **Caveat:** No named individual practitioner found. This is corporate-level deployment announcement.

### What We Did NOT Find (Supply Chain)
- **No named supply chain practitioners publishing about their agent deployments.** The supply chain domain is dominated by vendor announcements (ServiceNow, GEP, ORO Labs, project44) and analyst predictions. We found zero practitioner blog posts, zero X.com threads, zero conference talk writeups from someone who actually deployed and ran supply chain agents.
- **The "medical device manufacturer" and "transportation company" referenced in SCMR articles were not named.** Anonymized case references only.
- **No independent verification of DHL or Uber Freight claims.** The MIT CTL webinar with Lior Ron is the closest to independent analysis.
- **4 in 5 organizations report no tangible bottom-line impact** despite AI rollouts (per logistics industry survey) — this contradicts the optimistic vendor narratives.

### What We Did NOT Find (HR)

The signal, if it exists, lives in closed-door conferences and vendor case studies we can't verify.

## 3. Compliance / Legal

### WorkFusion — AI Agent "Evan" (AML Compliance)
- **Domain:** Financial Crime / AML
- **Named practitioner:** No individual named — this is a product deployment.
- **What it does:** Automates negative news screening for AML/KYC. Sources content, analyzes context (political exposure, sanctions), filters false positives, generates audit-ready narratives.
- **Measurable outcome:** Handles up to 95% of article reviews automatically; reduces onboarding review time from 20 minutes to 2 minutes. Also deployed "Edward" agent for Enhanced Due Diligence.
- **Source:** https://regtechanalyst.com/why-ai-agent-evan-is-transforming-aml-compliance/ [domain trade publication — Level 2-3]; https://fintech.global/2025/10/13/how-workfusions-ai-agent-evan-redefines-kyc-efficiency/ [domain trade publication]
- **Evidence level:** Level 2 (product with reported deployments, trade publication coverage, but no named customer with verified results)

- **Measurable outcome:** "Over 70% cost savings" by removing manual effort; shortened onboarding times. Named "Best KYB" by Chartis Research 2024-2025.
- **Source:** https://cleverchain.ai/introducing_vera_your_ai-powered_due_diligence_specialist/ [vendor blog — Level 0]; https://regtechanalyst.com/ai-agents-drive-automation-in-financial-crime-compliance/ [domain trade publication]

### Logan Brown — Founder, Soxton (AI-First Law Firm)

## Cross-Domain Synthesis

### The Practitioner Publication Gap Is Real
Across all four domains, we found **zero practitioners who both (a) deployed business-domain AI agents and (b) publish openly about results.** The closest are:
- **Lior Ron** (Uber Freight) — but he's a CEO doing company marketing, not a practitioner sharing learnings
- **Logan Brown** (Soxton) — genuine practitioner, but very early-stage and in legal, not operations/HR/compliance
- **Philip Widegren** (Nordea) — one quoted comment, not an active publisher

### Vendor Announcement vs. Deployment Reality
Every domain shows the same pattern:
- Vendors announce agents (Workday, ServiceNow, SAP, Oracle, UKG, ADP)
- Analysts predict transformation (Bersin, Gartner)
- Actual practitioner adoption is 10-20% at best
- Named companies with verified outcomes: essentially zero

### The Evidence Ladder Position
- **Supply Chain:** Level 2 at best (Uber Freight self-reports, DHL self-reports). No convergence.
- **HR:** Level 0-1. Entirely vendor announcements and analyst predictions. No practitioner evidence.
- **Compliance/Legal:** Level 2 (WorkFusion Evan has trade publication coverage; Logan Brown is a genuine practitioner). No convergence.
- **Nordic:** Level 1. Nordea is operational but chatbot-level. No truly agentic Nordic business deployments surfaced.

### Counter-Evidence Found
- 4 in 5 organizations report no tangible AI bottom-line impact (supply chain survey)
- 95% of GenAI pilots deliver zero measurable ROI (MIT, August 2025)
- 40% of AI agent projects expected to be canceled by 2027 (Gartner — but Gartner is Level 0)
- Only 14% of SHRM members use AI in core HR systems
- Only 20% of lawyers at largest firms use AI regularly
- Courts catching AI citation fabrication accelerating (120 → 660 cases in 7 months)
