## Notes

### Key Signals
1. **Domain remains thin on practitioner evidence.** 80 links examined; only 5 pass full quality gates. The vast majority are vendor announcements (Amplitude, AlphaSense, Crayon) or conceptual/opinion pieces about PM+agents.

2. **Vendor announcement dominance.** 26 of 66 unique links are vendor announcements. This domain is driven by product companies (Amplitude, AlphaSense, Crayon, Klue, Productboard, Glean, ChatPRD) announcing agent features, not by practitioners reporting deployment results.

3. **New since archive (Feb 2026 updates):**
   - **Amplitude** launched 5 specialized agents (Feb 2026) — Dashboard Monitoring, Session Replay, Web Experimentation, AI Feedback, Global Agent. NTT DOCOMO cited as early customer. Built on Amazon Bedrock. Also listed on Google Cloud Agent Finder.
   - **AlphaSense** launched two new products: "Deep Research" (automated market analysis) and "Autonomous AI Agent Interviewer" (conducts market research interviews). Significant capability expansion.
   - **HBR** published "To Thrive in the AI Era, Companies Need Agent Managers" (Feb 2026) — legitimizing the "agent manager" role as a new organizational function.
   - **LangChain State of Agent Engineering** survey: 57% of respondents have agents in production (but this is a developer-heavy sample, not enterprise PM teams).

4. **Ethan Mollick's "Management as AI Superpower"** is the most interesting practitioner-direct signal. His thesis: management skills (scoping problems, giving feedback, evaluating work) are the key competency for working with agents. This aligns directly with Agents 102 positioning.

5. **Counter-evidence is strong:**
   - 75% of agentic AI tasks fail (Superface data)
   - CRM agents below 55% goal completion
   - Gartner: 40% of agentic AI projects scrapped by 2027
   - 42% of organizations still developing agentic strategy roadmap
   - 65% cite system complexity as top barrier

6. **Competitive intelligence sub-domain (Crayon/Klue) is the most mature.** Multiple independent sources confirm production deployments. Crayon's MCP server launch is an infrastructure signal.

7. **"Agent manager" as a new role** — HBR and multiple Medium posts converge on this. Product managers are being reframed as "agent managers" who scope, evaluate, and govern AI agents. This is a Mollick-aligned signal.

8. **No new Nordic signals found.** No Nordic company deployments of product/strategy agents beyond what's already in the archive (Klarna APP, Nordea Enterprise Agent).

9. **No academic research found.** Zero academic papers on AI agents in product management. The domain is entirely practitioner/vendor driven.

10. **High duplicate rate across queries.** Product School and Medium posts appear across 3+ queries, suggesting a small content pool recycling the same ideas.
