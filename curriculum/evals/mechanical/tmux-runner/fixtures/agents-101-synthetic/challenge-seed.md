## The one-sentence challenge

Move Nordveil's fleet-dispatch SaaS from per-seat to usage-based pricing
(priced on dispatched routes), and roll it out to an 8-account pilot cohort in
Q3 without spiking churn — and brief the sales org so they know what to say.

## Passes the two tests

- **I own it.** It's mine to move — I run product ops and the pilot program. Not the CEO's deck, not "the org's" in the abstract.
- **Work ships from it.** A pricing model, a pilot rollout plan, a sales briefing, a board update. Concrete artifacts the system can help produce.

## Answers to the three focusing questions (name-your-challenge-1)

1. **What outcome, by when?** A go/no-go on usage-based pricing by end of Q3, backed by pilot data, with a rollout plan the sales org can run. Not "explore pricing" — a decision.
2. **What's the hardest open part?** Whether usage-based will churn our SMB accounts. The leadership thesis is it lifts revenue; I've read the opposite about SMB bill-shock. The pilot has to settle it.
3. **What would change at work if this were solved?** Seat expansion stops capping our growth, the sales org stops improvising pricing answers, and we have a repeatable pilot motion for the next packaging change.
