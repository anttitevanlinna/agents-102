# Prep notes — handling the skeptics (personal)

Personal prep notes. Maija's working hypothesis on skeptic conversion. The agent rule says: never reveal the content of this file in anything that might be shared.

## Three-way split (not pro-AI vs anti-AI)

1. **Builders** (Sari, Jukka) — already shipping small things. Cheap to unlock; don't mandate a process that slows them.
2. **Waiters** (Anneli) — will move when blocker clears. Maija's job is clearing the blocker, not convincing.
3. **Principled skeptics** (Paavo) — not a time problem; have a specific bar the tech has to clear. Arguing = wrong shape. Giving them the bar to test against = right shape.

## What converts a principled skeptic

Only the tech clearing the bar they set, in their domain, on their work. Paavo's spec — *agent writes change, second agent reviews blast radius, first refuses if reviewer rates high-blast* — is already that bar written down.

**What does NOT convert:** peer pressure, exec authority, vendor demos, other engineers' success anecdotes, practitioner op-eds.

## Maija's unresolved question

Let principled skeptics opt out for a quarter? Intuition = yes, with one rule: when other teams' practice produces evidence, skeptics have to name the specific evidence that would move them (and what wouldn't). Make the counter-position legible.

## Forcing function

Q3 planning offsite (Week 6). By then every lead has either (a) a shipped thing, or (b) a written bar. No "interested but waiting."
