# Finland AI-usage baseline — Finnish workforce / individual layer

**Compiled:** 2026-06-25 · **Layer scope:** The INDIVIDUAL / WORKFORCE layer — how Finnish working people *personally* use AI, where the "it just means chat" reality is most visible. Sibling to fragment (a) (enterprise official statistics, the company headline).

**One-line takeaway:** The bottom-up reality is confirmed. Across every independent Finnish workforce survey, "using AI at work" overwhelmingly means an **individual employee typing into a chatbot** — and the dominant tasks are **information search and text generation/improvement**, the two most conversational uses there are. Statistics Finland's population survey (the official-statistics anchor) puts the most-common generative-AI use at **information search (33%)** then **text production (25%)**, with **coding stuck at 6%**. Adoption is **self-taught, personal-initiative**: only **~20%** of highly-educated professionals got employer AI training (Akava Works), and only **~1/3** organisation-wide (ETLA). The skill arrived through the side door — people brought ChatGPT to work, work did not hand them an integrated agent.

## (b) The workforce reality — is Finnish AI use predominantly individual conversational chat?

**Yes — strongest evidence, in order of weight:**

1. **The task mix is conversational by composition (Level 3 convergence, official + union + research).** Every survey that itemises *what people do* lands on the same two-task core: **information search and text production**. Statistics Finland's population data: search 33% / text 25% / coding 6%. Even Akava's *engineers* — the population most able to do integrated/agentic work — itemise data analysis, "concept research," and content creation ahead of coding (34%), and self-classify AI as a **"tool" (58%) or "assistant" (39%)**, with only a sliver (work-partner 1.5% + primary-partner 0.5% as reported) treating it as a genuine delegated partner. When the most technical cohort in the country still uses AI as a chat-assistant rather than a delegated agent, the chat-not-integration read is robust.

2. **Adoption was self-taught and personal-initiative, not employer-deployed.** Akava Works: **~50% learned AI by self-study, only ~20% via employer training.** ETLA: only **~1/3** of employed received employer generative-AI training. This is the signature of bottom-up chatbot adoption — the employee brought ChatGPT to the desk; the organisation did not hand them an integrated agent. (This mirrors fragment (a)'s enterprise finding that the corporate headline is "language/text/generative-shaped" — the same generative-chat shape, seen from the employer side.)

3. **"Available at workplace" ≫ "actively used by me."** Statistics Finland Työolotutkimus (2023): **28%** had AI tech *available*, but only **~10%** *actively used it themselves*. The gap between deployment-adjacent availability and personal active use is exactly where "we have AI" inflates past "people do agentic work."

4. **Frequency is shallow for most.** Akava 2024: **half never use it; only ~26% weekly+; just 6% daily.** Even the 2026 engineer cohort at 80% adoption skews to **low weekly time (most under ~3h/week; only ~5% over 5h/week)** — minutes-a-day chat-assistant use, not workflow-embedded agency. The fast-rising "daily use" figures (Yrittäjät: 27% daily, May 2026) are real growth, but daily ≠ integrated; a daily ChatGPT-search habit is still chat.

**The conversational verdict:** Finnish workforce AI use in 2026 is **broad, fast-growing, individually-initiated, and overwhelmingly chat-shaped** — typing questions and text-drafting prompts into a chatbot. Integrated/agentic use (AI embedded in workflows, acting with tools across multiple steps) is present at the technical frontier but is nowhere the modal experience. The maintainer hypothesis holds at the workforce layer.

## (c) Confirming vs disconfirming

**Confirming (chat-not-integration):**
- Task-mix data — search + text dominate, coding marginal (Statistics Finland; convergent across surveys). **Confirms.**
- Self-study >> employer training (Akava, ETLA). **Confirms** personal-initiative/bottom-up.
- "Tool/assistant" self-classification (97% combined), with only ~2% calling AI a delegated work/primary partner (Akava engineers). **Confirms** conversational framing even among the technical elite.
- Availability >> active personal use (Työolotutkimus). **Confirms** the headline-inflation mechanism.

**Disconfirming / tension (genuine integration may be larger than "just chat"):**
- **Akava engineers at 80% with 34% coding** is real agentic-adjacent activity in a specific cohort — the frontier is not chat-only. Don't over-generalise the chat verdict to *every* segment; software/engineering is ahead.
- **Rapid daily-use growth** (15%→27% daily in 11 months, Yrittäjät) means the shallow-frequency picture is a moving target; depth is increasing even if shape is still conversational.
- **Sector spread is enormous** — universities ~70% vs municipalities/welfare 21% (Statistics Finland); blue-collar 23% (SAK). "Finnish workforce AI use" is not one number; the chat verdict is strongest in white-collar/knowledge work and weakest (near-absent) in blue-collar.
- **None of these surveys directly measures "chat vs integrated."** The chat read is inferred from task-mix + tool-framing + adoption-path, not from a survey item that asked "do you use AI as a chatbot or as an embedded agent?" That item does not exist in the Finnish corpus (see (d)).

## (e) Four-persona inline audit

- **Source-type:** Strong tier mix — anchored on **[official statistics]** (Statistics Finland SUTIVI + Työolotutkimus, the highest non-academic tier), reinforced by **[union/labor survey]** first-party data (Akava 2,002 + 6,438; SAK 1,812) and **[academic/research]** (ETLA, VTT). Two soft venues flagged in-table: Yrittäjät's gallup carries an **advocacy commissioner** (Finnish Entrepreneurs — interest in an upbeat productivity story) and DNA is a **telecom commissioner**. Pro/Sitra correctly demoted to attitude-context, not usage. Clean.
- **Zombie-stat:** The at-risk round numbers ("half use AI," "joka kymmenes," "80%") are each pinned to a definition: Akava's 50% = "hyödyntää tekoälysovelluksia työssään" with a published frequency ladder; Työolotutkimus 10% = *active self-use* (explicitly distinguished from 28% *availability* — the exact distinction zombie usage erases); engineers' 80% = private-sector Tekniikan akateemiset only, **not** all workers. The headline I lead with (search 33% / text 25% / coding 6%) carries its own task definitions. One residual gap: SUTIVI's "used generative AI" = last-3-months recall, a *looser* bar than "uses at work" — flagged in-table. No naked round number shipped without its denominator.
- **Freshness:** Excellent. Anchor data is **2025–2026** (SUTIVI 2025; Tieto&trendit Feb 2026; Akava engineers Jun 2026; SAK Dec 2025; Yrittäjät May 2026; ETLA Sept 2025) — all inside the 6-month preferred window or just outside. The two **dated** items are explicitly labelled: Akava Työolotutkimus **spring 2024** and VTT×TEK **2023** (kept as trajectory baseline, not current state). Statistics Finland Työolotutkimus AI item is **autumn 2023** fieldwork — labelled dated; superseded by the Feb 2026 consumer-module 27%.
- **Evidence-ladder:** The core claim — *Finnish workforce AI use is predominantly individual conversational chat* — sits at **Level 3 (convergence)**: many independent surveys (official + 3 unions + ETLA + VTT), different populations, same task-mix and same self-taught adoption-path. It does **not** reach Level 4 — fragment (a)'s enterprise agreement is the *same domain (Finnish AI usage) seen from the employer side*, corroboration that strengthens the L3 read, not a genuine cross-domain meta-pattern. Held at L3. Sector-spread and the rapid daily-use growth are the honest Level-2 caveats that keep it from being overstated.
