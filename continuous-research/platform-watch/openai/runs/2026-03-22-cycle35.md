## Orientation

The picture for the CTO question has shifted further. OpenAI is now running five simultaneous strategic fronts (super-app merge + Frontier + PE subsidiary + AWS deal + IPO) while its enterprise market position erodes in real-time by transactional data. The API spend flip (Anthropic ~80%, was ~5% 8 months ago) is the most dramatic competitive shift in the AI enterprise market. The deployment gap at 8+ weeks post-Frontier is no longer a timing issue — it's structural evidence that the enterprise platform is not yet producing user-reportable results. For Nordic CTOs, the additional EEA restrictions make OpenAI the weakest choice for business agent deployment among the major platforms.

The Codex non-coding push is the most interesting new signal to watch — if Codex Skills genuinely become a business-user tool, it could bridge the gap between OpenAI's developer-dominant architecture and business user needs. But today it's vapor — announced, not adopted.
