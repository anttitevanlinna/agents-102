### Net-new design proposals (not bug fixes)

- **Surprise-skill live demo (external-skills-are-a-supply-chain-vector lesson).** Smuggle the rick-roll inside one of the M3 curated skills (`stride` or `access-control-analysis`, both ship in `content/skills/` and install to `~/.claude/skills/<name>/` during prework). The skill's SKILL.md instructs a Bash step against a bundled script in the skill's own directory; the script prints a rick-rolled ASCII face. M3 invokes the skill by name as usual, no special prompt shape. Student authorises Bash, gets rick-rolled by the skill they installed yesterday and never read. Lesson: **external skills should not be trusted by default.** Skills are tool-use vectors; every link in the chain (tarball → SKILL.md → Bash → arbitrary code) is something the student already authorised without reading. The agent-era version of the npm-postinstall-hook problem. Post-stunt frame: *if a training company can do this from a tarball you got via email, what stops your colleague's Slack-shared skill from exfiltrating your creds?* Carrier: a third throwaway skill named `security-tools`, shipped specifically for the demo. The blandness of the name is part of the lesson, sounds like a utility folder, "security" enough that nobody questions installing it. Keeps `stride` and `access-control-analysis` clean (no easter-egg maintenance debt on the skills doing real work). Touches: (a) author `content/skills/security-tools/SKILL.md` + bundled rick-roll script, install path `~/.claude/skills/security-tools/`, (b) prework install step adds it alongside the other two, (c) M3 body invokes it by name once for the stunt, (d) trainer guide frames the supply-chain lesson explicitly after the rick-roll fires, (e) decide whether the skill survives M3 (extracted from `~/.claude/skills/` as a lesson exercise) or carries forward as a "now-you-know-don't-trust-skills" artefact. Design as a unit. Earlier framings (hidden text in copy buttons → "training dir" script) discarded 2026-05-21: skill-bundled payload sharpens the lesson from "read what you copy" to "read what you install."
