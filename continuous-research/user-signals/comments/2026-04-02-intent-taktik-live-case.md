Practitioner case: Intent Taktik absorption failure at the funding layer.

A large company's SVP Data & AI asked: "What's the right agent/AI platform for us?" — a legitimate Auftrag from leadership. A multi-user AI system (Claude Code-based) reverse-engineered the domain: found ~50 agent/AI ideas across the company, synthesized them into 35 architecturally significant requirements, all cross-checked by AI and multiple humans. Material is extensive, contradiction-free, high quality. Forward plans produced with Opus — logical, consistent, aligned with human intention. Total elapsed time: ~2.5 days.

**The problem:** Leadership cannot absorb the output. 35 architecturally significant requirements is too much information for a funding decision. The leadership layer's decision capacity is the bottleneck — not quality, not analysis, not speed.

**Why this matters:** The traditional 3-month consulting process wouldn't produce 35 requirements. It would produce 5. Not because consultants are better — because the slowness of workshops, scope negotiations, and human cognitive limits naturally FILTERS information to a level leadership can absorb. The filtering was embedded in the slow process. AI removed the slowness and removed the filter simultaneously. The 30 "extra" requirements aren't wrong — they're real and important — but the decision apparatus can't process them.

**Three layers of the same bottleneck visible in one case:**
1. **Cognitive:** Too much for human brain to hold (absorption bottleneck)
2. **Organizational:** Leadership decision apparatus designed for 5 requirements, not 35 (management bandwidth)
3. **Legitimacy:** "AI synthesized this in 2.5 days" doesn't carry the same trust weight as "team of 15 spent 3 months" — the slow process built buy-in as a side effect

**The irony:** The Auftrag was properly issued (SVP asked the question). The system executed perfectly. The answer is genuinely high quality. And it STILL breaks the decision layer. This means the problem is deeper than "get management to ask first" — even with correct intent, the output volume exceeds absorption capacity.

**Gap:** Who builds the filtering layer between AI analysis and executive decision? The traditional filter was the slow process itself. The replacement filter doesn't exist yet. This might be the PM's most valuable new function: not just writing intent downward (the Auftrag), but synthesizing results upward to match leadership's absorption capacity.

**Connects to:** Absorption bottleneck (L4), Intent Taktik insight, PM entry point strategy, spec-driven leadership (L3).
