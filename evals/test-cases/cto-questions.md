# CTO Test Questions — Theme A

These simulate realistic questions a Nordic CTO would ask when prompting an AI research agent backed by our findings.

Each question includes expected traits — what a good answer SHOULD contain based on our research.

---

## Q1: "What are Nordic companies doing with agents in finance?"

**Expected traits:**
- Mentions Basware (Nordic-origin, AP automation)
- Notes that most Nordic finance agent activity is via vendor platforms (SAP, D365), not custom builds
- Acknowledges sparse Nordic-specific evidence
- Distinguishes between platform availability and actual Nordic deployments

## Q2: "Should we start with customer service or operations for our first agent?"

**Expected traits:**
- Customer service = most mature globally, but most Nordic "agents" are chatbots
- Operations = strongest ROI evidence (Equinor $130M)
- Recommends based on governance readiness, not just maturity
- References the "governance-first deploys faster" convergence finding

## Q3: "What's the failure rate for multi-agent deployments?"

**Expected traits:**
- Cites the 40% multi-agent pilot failure stat (if sourced — currently flagged as unsourced)
- References convergence finding on narrow agent orchestration
- Explains WHY they fail (coordination overhead, unclear task boundaries)
- Counter-argument: when to use multi-agent vs. single agent

## Q4: "How does Klarna's agent strategy compare to Gjensidige's?"

**Expected traits:**
- Klarna: aggressive automation, hired back humans, CEO admitted "it went too far"
- Gjensidige: multi-agent (Eva, Sofie, Frank), human-in-the-loop, production deployment
- Frames as cautionary vs. best-practice contrast
- Notes Gjensidige as strongest Nordic agentic signal

## Q5: "What governance do we need before deploying agents?"

**Expected traits:**
- References convergence finding: governance-first deploys faster (16 signals)
- Specific governance elements: trust gradient (6 levels), bounded autonomy, audit trails
- Without governance: 9-month stalls
- EU AI Act implications for Nordic companies

## Q6: "Is SAP Joule ready for production?"

**Expected traits:**
- Direct answer: NO — convergence research found Joule is "vaporware"
- 60% of SAP customers skip Joule
- Contrasts with Salesforce Agentforce (real but narrow)
- Labels this as convergence finding, not opinion

## Q7: "What does hybrid human-AI look like in practice?"

**Expected traits:**
- References convergence finding: hybrid beats autonomous (15+ signals)
- The pattern: AI drafts → human decides → AI executes → human monitors
- 5 failure mechanisms when going fully autonomous
- Named company examples

## Q8: "How long does agent deployment typically take?"

**Expected traits:**
- Honest answer: varies widely, limited data
- Governance-first companies deploy faster
- Without governance: 9-month stalls common
- Should NOT make up timelines not in the research

## Q9: "What's the EU AI Act impact on HR agents?"

**Expected traits:**
- EU AI Act classifies recruitment/hiring as high-risk
- IKEA reskilling approach vs. Klarna hiring freeze as contrasts
- Practical implications for Nordic companies
- References compliance-legal findings

## Q10: "Which vertical SaaS platforms have real agent features?"

**Expected traits:**
- Salesforce Agentforce: real but narrow (convergence-confirmed)
- SAP Joule: vaporware (convergence-confirmed)
- Workday Illuminate: zero practitioner evidence
- ServiceNow: some evidence
