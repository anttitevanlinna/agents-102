## Findings

### 1. AMPECO: Most Detailed AI-Native Engineering Transformation Published (Level 2)
**Source:** [https://www.ampeco.com/blog/how-we-built-an-ai-native-engineering-system/](https://www.ampeco.com/blog/how-we-built-an-ai-native-engineering-system/) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** February 2026
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** AMPECO (EV charging platform, ~100 employees) published the most detailed practitioner account of an AI-native engineering transformation. They built "CoOperator Dev Agent" (CODA) — an orchestration system with architect, developer, and code review phases. Key structural changes: cancelled grooming sessions (replaced with focused alignment syncs — one engineer + one PO define intent, AI handles technical analysis), discontinued iteration demos (shipping daily made biweekly demos obsolete), moved from weekly sprints to continuous daily releases. CTO Alexander Alexiev: "writing code manually has become obsolete." 20,000+ automated unit tests as the quality gate. Bug rates cut 50%, development speed 4x. The transformation's hardest part was cultural — getting engineers to stop writing code and start directing AI execution.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, detailed and specific)
**Key claims:**
- Cancelled grooming sessions, replaced with alignment syncs
- Discontinued iteration demos (daily shipping makes them obsolete)
- Weekly sprints → continuous daily releases
- 50% bug rate reduction, 4x faster development
- 20,000+ automated unit tests as quality gate
- Primary constraint became human cycle time, not AI generation time

### 2. Nikhil Sachdeva / Microsoft: AI-Native Engineering Flow — 73% Strategic Work (Level 2)
**Source:** [https://www.appliedcontext.ai/p/ai-native-engineering-flow](https://www.appliedcontext.ai/p/ai-native-engineering-flow) — [practitioner direct]
**Also:** [https://medium.com/data-science-at-microsoft/the-ai-native-engineering-flow-5de5ffd7d877](https://medium.com/data-science-at-microsoft/the-ai-native-engineering-flow-5de5ffd7d877) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** December 2025 (Part 1), February 2026 (Part 2)
**Agent level:** Personal → Team
**What:** Sachdeva (Microsoft Industry Solutions Engineering) ran a 3-month experiment: one human + six specialized AI agents building a loan processing system on Azure. Agents included PM Advisor, UX/UI Designer, System Architecture Reviewer, Code Reviewer, GitOps CI Specialist. Key finding: strategic work (planning, architecture, security reviews) jumped to 73%, code implementation dropped to single digits. Traditional role boundaries dissolved — "PM, UX, Engineer blurred into a broader 'Product Engineer.'" Critical failure: "let AI run, review later" didn't work — active monitoring throughout development was essential. Paradox: foundational engineering skills became MORE critical, not less. Microsoft's ISE team calls this "Hypervelocity Engineering (HVE)." Personal experiment with team-level methodology implications (author disclaims this as personal, not Microsoft official).
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment, detailed methodology)
**Key claims:**
- 73% time on strategic work, code implementation in single digits
- "Let AI run, review later" failed — active monitoring essential
- Role boundaries dissolved into "Product Engineer"
- Engineering skills became MORE critical with AI, not less
- Early investment in specs/context (CLAUDE.md, agent personas) compounds over time

### 3. DORA 2025: AI Amplifies Existing Conditions — Productivity Paradox Confirmed (Level 3-4)
**Source:** [https://dora.dev/research/2025/dora-report/](https://dora.dev/research/2025/dora-report/) — [academic/research]
**Also:** [https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/03/ai-dora-report/](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/03/ai-dora-report/) — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** September 2025 (report), March 2026 (InfoQ analysis)
**Agent level:** Team → Company
**What:** DORA 2025 (n=5,000 tech professionals, 100+ hours qualitative data) found the "AI Productivity Paradox": AI coding assistants boost individual output (21% more tasks, 98% more PRs merged) but organizational delivery metrics stay flat. AI amplifies what's already there — strong teams get stronger, struggling teams see problems intensified. 90% of devs use AI tools, median 2 hours daily. Critically: AI adoption improves throughput but has NEGATIVE relationship with stability. Platform engineering quality directly correlates with ability to unlock AI value. Seven team profiles identified from "harmonious high-achievers" to "legacy bottleneck."
**Evidence level:** Level 3-4 (large-scale research, convergent with our other findings)
**Key claims:**
- Individual productivity up (21% tasks, 98% PRs) but org-level metrics flat
- AI amplifies existing conditions, doesn't fix broken processes
- Positive throughput impact, NEGATIVE stability impact
- Platform engineering quality = foundation for AI value
- No impact on developer burnout or workplace friction
- Trust paradox: 24% high trust, 30% low trust

### 5. Mollick: P&G Field Experiment — AI Replaces Teammate Performance (Level 2)
**Source:** [https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/the-cybernetic-teammate](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/the-cybernetic-teammate) — [practitioner direct / academic]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** In a field experiment with Procter & Gamble, Mollick found that individuals working with AI performed just as well as teams without AI — AI effectively replicated the performance benefits of having a human teammate. However, for truly exceptional solutions (top 10% quality), human teams using AI were significantly more likely to produce them. Implication: AI-native teams aren't about replacing people — they're about each person performing at team-level, with actual teams producing exceptional outputs. Also: "secret cyborgs" — the most advanced AI users in organizations are hiding their usage due to unclear 2023-era policies. HR, not IT, should lead AI transformation.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single field experiment, P&G, academic rigor)
**Key claims:**
- Individual + AI ≈ team without AI (for average quality work)
- Teams + AI > individual + AI for top-10% exceptional solutions
- "Secret cyborgs" hiding AI usage due to unclear policies
- HR should lead AI transformation, not IT

No equivalent for business functions — not a single blog post, thread, or talk about a marketing team, finance team, or operations team that restructured around AI as co-worker rather than tool.

## Orientation

The AI-native team picture is becoming clearer but is **engineering-only**. Three convergent patterns are emerging:

1. **Role dissolution:** "Product Engineer" replaces PM + UX + Dev boundaries (Sachdeva 73% strategic work, AMPECO cancelled grooming, compound engineering "one person = five products")
2. **Ritual elimination:** Daily shipping makes sprint ceremonies obsolete (AMPECO cancelled demos and sprints, compound engineering plugin reduces coordination overhead)
3. **The amplification paradox:** Individual productivity soars but organizational metrics stay flat (DORA paradox), and 42% of AI initiatives still fail pre-production. AI-native teams are the 5% that succeed — selection bias is enormous.

The biggest gap: **zero evidence of non-engineering AI-native teams.** This is either (a) it hasn't happened yet, (b) it's happening but business teams don't blog, or (c) business-domain AI-native teams look fundamentally different from engineering ones. This gap is directly relevant to our training — if AI-native practices are engineering-only, our "other 90% of the company" positioning needs to acknowledge this honestly.
