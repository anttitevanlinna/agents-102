# Does the national "we use AI" adoption headline just mean plain LLM chat?

A builder leader preparing a presentation suspected the high national "we use AI in business" headline (recalled as 60–80%) actually decomposes to people *chatting* with an LLM (ChatGPT/Copilot), not integrated or agentic deployment. Question: what is the latest official statistic, and what does it really mean underneath?

**Answered by this cycle** (`findings/by-pattern/finland-ai-usage-baseline-2026-06-25/SYNTHESIS.md`):
- Finland enterprise (10+) "any AI" = **38%** (Eurostat #2 in EU, ~1.9× EU avg) — but the 60–80% memory is the *generative-AI / large-firm / individual-worker* cut, not the population-wide number. Name the denominator.
- The headline is **denominated in chat at the instrument level**: the EU-topping "66% genAI" is *literally defined* as "ChatGPT/Bard/Copilot"; the official type-split is all text/language/media generation; there is no depth question.
- Depth collapses: **91% permit → 28% transform → 12% replaced a task** (Finland). Workforce task-mix is conversational (info-search #1, coding 6%), adoption self-taught not employer-deployed.
- The chat-collapse is a **global law (L3)**, not a Finnish quirk — US Census microdata, OpenAI/NBER, Anthropic, McKinsey/Bain/Slack/Ramp converge: ≤3 functions per adopter, ~7% fully scaled.

**Gap remaining:** no survey (Finnish or otherwise) with a clean mutually-exclusive chat/integrated/agentic cross-tab; **no Finnish agentic-AI adoption denominator at all** — the strategic opening is to instrument and publish the first credible one. Norway/Iceland Eurostat cells uncollected.
