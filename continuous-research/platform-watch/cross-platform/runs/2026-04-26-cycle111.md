### Finding 1: OpenAI Workspace Agents — Team-Level Shared Persistent Agents Launched (Research Preview)
**Source:** https://openai.com/index/introducing-workspace-agents-in-chatgpt/ — [vendor]; https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/openai-unveils-workspace-agents-a-successor-to-custom-gpts-for-enterprises-that-can-plug-directly-into-slack-salesforce-and-more — [general press, independent]; https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/7760821 — [analyst, independent]; https://thenewstack.io/openai-shared-workspace-agents/ — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** April 22, 2026
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** OpenAI launched Workspace Agents as research preview for Business, Enterprise, Edu, and Teachers plans — shared, long-running agents that persist across sessions, run on schedules or event triggers, and connect to Slack, Google Drive, Microsoft apps, Salesforce, and Notion. Powered by Codex (cloud-hosted). This directly addresses the "single-player architecture" gap documented across 15+ prior cycles. **Custom GPTs are being deprecated for orgs** — Business/Enterprise must migrate existing GPTs to Workspace Agents (timeline TBD). Org controls: admins can restrict who can build, run, and publish agents, and which tools/actions are accessible. Agents can join Slack conversations where work already happens. Free until May 6, then credit-based pricing.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (research preview — architecture is real, shipping evidence is zero. Gartner same-day "First Take" confirms governance concerns as the key enterprise hurdle. No named enterprise deploying results yet.)
**Key claims:**
- Shared cloud-hosted agents persist across tasks and sessions (not sandboxed, not session-only)
- Event/schedule triggers: agents start on Slack message, cron, or API call
- Write access to connected apps: Slack, Google Drive, Microsoft apps, Salesforce, Notion
- Custom GPTs deprecated for Business/Enterprise — migration to Workspace Agents required
- Free until May 6, then credit-based pricing (no rate published)
- Gartner: "application leaders must act now to prepare for the governance challenges of giving AI write access across enterprise systems"
- Research preview = capability is real, enterprise deployment patterns are unknown
