## Findings

### Finding 1: Cross-Domain Trust Gap — Level 4 Meta-Pattern Confirmed

**Sources:**
- [HBR: Only 6% of companies fully trust AI agents to handle core business processes](https://fortune.com/2025/12/09/harvard-business-review-survey-only-6-percent-companies-trust-ai-agents/) — [general press reporting academic/research study]
- [Enterprise AI Trust Gap: Companies Hesitate to Deploy Agents on Core Business](https://techstrong.ai/features/enterprise-ai-trust-gap-companies-hesitate-to-deploy-agents-on-core-business/) — [domain trade publication]
- [Legal AI Access at 83%, But Trust Issues Remain](https://www.artificiallawyer.com/2026/03/23/legal-ai-access-at-83-but-trust-issues-remain/) — [domain trade publication] (from cycle 62)
- [Why AI Pilots Fail](https://composio.dev/blog/why-ai-agent-pilots-fail-2026-integration-roadmap) — [practitioner analysis]

**Date:** December 2025 – March 2026
**Agent level:** Cross-domain (Industry)
**What:** The access-trust gap is now quantified independently across 4+ domains, making it a Level 4 cross-domain meta-pattern:

| Domain | Access/Experimentation | Trust/Production | Gap | Source |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------|-----|--------|
| **Legal** | 83% have access | 22.1% high trust | 61 pts | Factor/Artificial Lawyer, Mar 2026 |
| **Cross-industry** | 86% plan to increase investment | 6% fully trust for core processes | 80 pts | HBR Analytic Services (n=603), Dec 2025 |
| **Cross-industry** | 80.9% past planning into testing/production | 14.4% deploy with full approval | 66 pts | Gravitee (n=900+), Mar 2026 |
| **Finance** | 54% CFOs say AI agents are priority | 15% confident in data readiness | 39 pts | Deloitte CFO Signals, 2026 |
| **All domains** | 95% run pilots | 5% reach production with ROI | 90 pts | MIT NANDA, 2025-2026 |

The pattern is consistent: access/experimentation is widespread (54-95%), but trust/governance/production readiness is rare (5-22%). The gap is 39-90 percentage points across every domain measured.

**Evidence level:** Level 4 (cross-domain meta-pattern) — same pattern observed independently across legal, finance, customer service, cross-industry surveys, with consistent magnitude (gap always >35 percentage points)
**Key claims:** The bottleneck in agent adoption is NOT technology access but organizational trust-building. Access is solved (83% legal, 86% cross-industry planning investment). Trust is unsolved (6% full trust, 14.4% with full approval, 22.1% high trust in legal).

**Caveat:** HBR study (n=603) was sponsored by Workato + AWS — vendor-sponsored research. The trust percentages align with independent sources (Gravitee, Factor, MIT), so the sponsorship doesn't invalidate the finding, but the study design should be noted. Published December 2025 — within freshness window but approaching 4 months old.

## Orientation

### Three signals this cycle:

1. **The access-trust gap is now a Level 4 cross-domain meta-pattern.** Five independent data sources across legal, finance, cross-industry, and security all show the same structure: access/experimentation is 54-95%, trust/production is 5-22%, gap is always >35 percentage points. This is the strongest finding in the entire research program. **For CTOs:** The question is not "which platform?" or "which use case?" but "how do we build organizational trust?" Platform selection is downstream of trust-building. **For our advisory:** This is the core positioning insight — Agents 102 IS the trust-building program. We don't sell technology selection; we sell the organizational capability that closes the trust gap.

2. **Toyota/Ballard is the first named supply chain practitioner with metrics.** After 62 cycles of zero named supply chain practitioners, Jason Ballard (VP Digital Innovations, TMNA) at AWS re:Invent provides specific deployment metrics. The evidence is partially vendor-adjacent (AWS/Deloitte co-presentation) but the practitioner is speaking about their own deployed system with verifiable metrics. **Action:** Add Ballard to source roster. Search for TMNA-specific follow-up (blog posts, subsequent presentations, practitioners on Ballard's team).

3. **EU Digital Omnibus timing correction confirmed.** The plenary vote is NOT happening in March — expected Q2/Q3 2026. Previous cycles were premature in assuming a March 26 vote. The August 2, 2026 legal deadline for high-risk AI remains the pressure point, but the legislative process is running behind.

### Positioning implication:
The Level 4 access-trust gap meta-pattern directly supports our core thesis: competence creates trust, trust enables scaling, scaling creates value. Platform selection is a second-order question. The organizations that close the trust gap first will win — and closing it requires training (Agents 102), not technology procurement.
