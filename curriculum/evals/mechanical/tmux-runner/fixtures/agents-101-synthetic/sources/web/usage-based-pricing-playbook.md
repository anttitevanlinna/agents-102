# The Usage-Based Pricing Playbook: Why Aligning Price with Value Lifts NRR

_Practitioner essay · "the pro case"_

The single biggest lever on net revenue retention in B2B SaaS is removing the
ceiling between a customer's growth and your revenue. Seat-based pricing builds
that ceiling in: a customer can triple their usage and pay you nothing more.
Usage-based pricing removes it. Your revenue grows when their value grows,
automatically, with no renegotiation.

**The evidence pattern.** Companies that moved from seats to usage consistently
report NRR stepping up — Snowflake, Twilio, and a long tail of infrastructure
vendors live above 120% NRR precisely because expansion is built into the meter,
not gated behind a sales conversation.

**It also fixes the "I'm not using all my seats" objection** — the most common
reason mid-market buyers push back at renewal. With usage pricing, they pay for
what they use, so the objection dissolves.

**On the bill-shock worry:** it's real but it's a *design* problem, not a reason
to avoid the model. A committed floor plus usage above it gives predictability
at the bottom and upside at the top. Add a soft cap or overage alerts and the
spikes stop being scary. The model isn't the problem; a naive implementation is.

The takeaway: if your usage is growing and your seat revenue isn't, you are
leaving money on the table every single month. Move to usage. Design the floor
well. The retention follows.
