# Redesign-Then-AI Enterprise Case OODA Cycle — 2026-05-08

**Focus area:** Find a named enterprise that redesigned roles, reporting lines, and capacity allocation first, then absorbed AI and measured outcomes 6+ months later.
**Research mode:** Transformation methods / counter-evidence search.
**Why this focus:** Tier 0 user signal. Existing cases mostly bypass or augment org structures rather than redesigning first.

## Findings

### BCG has the closest redesign-then-AI evidence, but the cases are anonymized and consultant-framed

**Source:** https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/design-your-company-for-ai-not-ai-for-your-company — [practitioner analysis / consultancy]
**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Agent level:** company
**What:** BCG describes an anonymized European energy services provider that explicitly shifted from scattered AI pilots to redesigning work structure, governance, staffing, and prioritization around end-to-end customer journeys. Three cross-functional teams owned high-impact journeys. Reported outcomes include run-rate savings in the first three months, >90% reduction in dependence on external providers, and AI-enabled journeys matching/on track to exceed human customer-satisfaction performance.
**Evidence level:** Level 1-2 signal, not admissible as named practitioner evidence. It directly matches the redesign-first sequence but fails the "named enterprise/practitioner-direct" gate.
**Key claims:** Energy services; redesign before scale; customer-journey operating model; cross-functional teams; early savings; reduced provider dependence.

**Source:** https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/design-your-company-for-ai-not-ai-for-your-company — [practitioner analysis / consultancy]
**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Agent level:** company
**What:** The same BCG article describes an anonymized global bank that redesigned HR around employee journeys, identified 80+ agent-assumable capabilities, built central governance, and organized work around small cross-functional teams integrating humans, AI agents, data, and technology. It reports a target of 30-50% workflow automation, about 3M human hours freed, payback within two years, and projected 150% five-year ROI.
**Evidence level:** Level 1-2 signal, but projected/anonymized/consultant-framed.
**Key claims:** Redesign of operating model and organizational structure; new roles such as AI product owners, model governance managers, and talent data strategists; projected outcomes.

### PwC confirms the redesign pressure statistically, not as a practitioner case

**Source:** https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/consulting/business-transformation/library/digital-trends-operations-survey.html — [practitioner analysis / survey]
**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Agent level:** cross-company / operations
**What:** PwC's 2026 Digital Trends in Operations survey of 767 operations and supply chain leaders says 83% expect AI agents and automation to accelerate breakdown of functional silos; 94% of siloed or partially integrated organizations expect to shift to horizontal/networked structures; only 41% operate that way today; only 27% have embedded AI strategy across business units.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 survey evidence for the pressure, not a case.
**Key claims:** Redesign is broadly anticipated; current organizational state lags; no named company/practitioner.

### C.H. Robinson is process-first, not org-redesign-first

**Source:** https://www.chrobinson.com/it-it/resources/blog/what-is-lean-ai/ — [practitioner direct, deployer-owned caveat]
**Date:** 2025-12-02
**Agent level:** company
**What:** C.H. Robinson began from Lean/gemba process observation and embedded AI into workflows. It has the best traditional-enterprise operating-model evidence, but the published material does not show roles, reporting lines, or capacity allocation being redesigned first. It is a process-redesign and workflow-embedding case, not a clear org-structure redesign case.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 single-case, self-reported.
**Key claims:** Gemba, real operational pain points, human feedback loop, 40% productivity, 3M+ tasks, 600 hours/day saved by orders agent.

### McKinsey describes workforce redesign, but not a named AI-era execution case

**Source:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-technology/our-insights/designing-an-end-to-end-technology-workforce-for-the-ai-first-era — [practitioner analysis / consultancy]
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Agent level:** cross-company / tech workforce
**What:** McKinsey argues CIOs need to rethink hiring, internal capability, and vendor strategy as agentic AI changes work. It mentions an anonymized global healthcare company reorganizing hundreds of technologists into a product-based operating model, but the example is not named, not necessarily AI-first, and does not publish 6+ month AI outcomes.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 context.
**Key claims:** Workforce redesign pressure; no qualifying named case.

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

- A named enterprise practitioner saying: here are the reporting lines we collapsed, the roles we removed/created, the capacity we moved, the AI absorption mechanism, and the measured business outcomes 6+ months later.
- A company-authored redesign-first playbook with before/after org charts or capacity allocation data.
- Direct evidence that Bayer-style dynamic shared ownership was redesigned for AI rather than speed/adaptability generally.
- A non-consultancy case where org structure changed before AI deployment rather than after pilots proved value.

## Orientation

The redesign-then-AI case remains missing at the required bar. BCG's anonymized energy and bank cases strongly suggest that such work is happening behind consulting walls, but the corpus cannot use them as named practitioner evidence. The public evidence has split into two weaker categories:

- **Anonymized consultant cases** with the right sequence but no named practitioner/company.
- **Named company cases** like C.H. Robinson with strong process/operating-model evidence but no explicit reporting-line/role/capacity redesign.

Current answer: **no named enterprise practitioner-direct redesign-then-AI case found.** The open research bet is to pierce the anonymized BCG cases through conference talks, customer references, or practitioner posts from European energy providers and global banks.
