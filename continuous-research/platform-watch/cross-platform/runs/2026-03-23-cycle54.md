### Finding 1: EU Digital Omnibus — Parliament Committee Votes 101-9 to Delay High-Risk AI Deadlines
**Source:** [EU Parliament committee backs AI Act delay](https://ppc.land/eu-parliament-committee-backs-ai-act-delay-with-fixed-2027-deadline/) — [general press]; [IAPP analysis](https://iapp.org/news/a/eu-digital-omnibus-analysis-of-key-changes) — [domain trade publication]; [European Parliament Think Tank](https://epthinktank.eu/2026/03/18/enforcement-of-the-ai-act/) — [academic/institutional, March 18, 2026]
**Date:** March 18-19, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** IMCO and LIBE committees voted 101-9 to postpone Annex III high-risk AI system deadlines from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027 (16-month delay). Annex I systems pushed to August 2, 2028. Parliament plenary vote expected March 26, 2026 (3 days away). Final adoption requires trilogues — not before mid-2026. Until formally adopted, August 2, 2026 remains the legal deadline. Creates maximum planning uncertainty for Nordic companies.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (institutional action, independently reported)
**Key claims:**
- IMCO+LIBE vote: 101-9 in favor of delay (verifiable at Parliament Think Tank link)
- Annex III deadline proposed: December 2, 2027 (IAPP analysis confirms)
- Plenary vote expected March 26, 2026
- August 2, 2026 remains legal deadline until formal adoption of Omnibus

### Finding 2: Colorado AI Act Dramatically Softened
**Source:** [Colorado Governor's Office announcement](https://governorsoffice.colorado.gov/governor/news/colorado-artificial-intelligence-policy-workgroup-delivers-unanimous-support-revised-policy) — [institutional, March 17, 2026]; [Colorado Sun analysis](https://coloradosun.com/2026/03/17/colorado-ai-law-tweaks-2026-proposals/) — [general press]; [Clark Hill law firm](https://www.clarkhill.com/news-events/news/co-working-group-agree-on-fix-for-controversial-co-ai-act/) — [domain trade — law firm blog]
**Date:** March 17, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Governor Polis's working group reached unanimous consensus on a complete rework: removes duty of "reasonable care" to prevent algorithmic discrimination, removes annual impact assessments and risk-management programs, removes all references to algorithmic discrimination (defers to existing law), narrows scope to tools that "materially influence" meaningful decisions, shifts to consumer notice + post-adverse-decision disclosure + meaningful human review. 90-day cure period. AG-only enforcement, no private right of action. June 30 enforcement date remains but for a much lighter law. Trump DOJ task force may challenge state AI laws — Colorado Act specifically named.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (institutional, independently analyzed by multiple law firms)
**Key claims:**
- Working group unanimous consensus on rework (Governor's Office)
- Removes algorithmic discrimination provisions entirely (Clark Hill, Colorado Sun)
- June 30, 2026 enforcement date remains but for lighter law
- Bill must be filed before legislature closes May 13

## Orientation

**Regulatory landscape shifting dramatically:** The EU Digital Omnibus vote (101-9 to delay) + Colorado rework together represent a global softening of AI enforcement timelines. For our advisory, this creates a paradox: less enforcement pressure means less urgency to prepare, but companies that prepare anyway gain competitive advantage when enforcement does arrive. Our positioning should shift from "deadline is coming" to "uncertainty is the reason to build competence now."

**SAP Joule going from zero to negative:** This is the biggest vertical SaaS update — Bloomberg + VW + practitioner hallucination reports + 60% skip rate. The gap between SAP's AI messaging and customer reality is the widest of any platform.

**Finance/accounting is the third domain crossing the agent threshold** (after coding and customer service). Three independent companies with real products, independently verified by domain trade press. This is a signal for our training content — finance is where participants should look for non-coding agent examples.

**Pattern 43 (competence-first) continues to strengthen with zero counter-evidence after 54 cycles.** Mollick's new framing ("organizational structure is the moderating factor") is directly usable in our advisory positioning.
