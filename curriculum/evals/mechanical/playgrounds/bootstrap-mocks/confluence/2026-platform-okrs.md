# Platform Engineering OKRs — 2026 H2

**Objective 1: agentic ways of working — from pilot to practice.**
- KR1.1 — every sub-team ships ≥1 agentic improvement by end Q3.
- KR1.2 — each platform engineer uses Claude Code on ≥1 shipped piece by end Q4 (85% target; the remaining 15% are named, not hidden).
- KR1.3 — adoption playbook published to store-ops / supply-chain / analytics by end Q4 with one pilot team committed in each.

**Objective 2: sub-team leads as agentic-practice coaches.**
- KR2.1 — each lead runs a "walk through a real agentic move" session by week 6 of Q3.
- KR2.2 — skeptics have a *named written counter-position* by end Q3, built against evidence the skeptic produced.
- KR2.3 — no sub-team lead runs their adoption as "just do what Maija does" — each adapts the playbook.

**Explicit non-goals:** no org-wide AI mandate; no central AI team; no dashboard of AI usage metrics; no vendor-led training.

**Named risks:**
1. Skeptic capture — mitigation: ammunition, not demos.
2. Tool-first drift — mitigation: track shipped improvements, not tool usage.
3. Classification block on security-platform — mitigation: raise with CISO by week 2 of Q3.
