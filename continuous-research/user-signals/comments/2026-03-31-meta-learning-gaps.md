Meta-learning review: what should the research system itself learn next? Four gaps identified in session reflection:

**1. Problem-finding bias.** Cycles 72-81 produced excellent problem articulation (absorption bottleneck, compound triggers, conditions creator, experience-first). Cycle 82 (solutions) was thinner — five strategies, none combined, none org-level proven. The system has a structural bias toward finding problems over finding solutions. Action: for every problem cycle, run a solutions cycle.

**2. No customer eval loop on our own output.** The system preaches "customer truth is the ceiling" but has no systematic feedback on whether its own findings are useful. User signals capture what people ASK, not whether ANSWERS changed anything. Action: pick 3 CTOs, show them the absorption bottleneck finding, measure whether it changes behavior.

**3. Practitioner signals under-invested.** The practitioner's observations in this session (SwarmAI, Little's Law framing, decision speed, engineering for broad learning) were more original than OODA web searches. The 80/20 split (80% frontier research, 20% peer premium) may need inverting in VALUE terms. Action: design the system to provoke practitioner insights, not just capture them incidentally.

**4. No behavior-change measurement.** 11 cycles, 4 findings, 2 articles, 14 signals. Does any of it change what a CTO does on Monday morning? The system optimizes for knowledge accumulation. The customer optimizes for decisions and actions. Action: add "did this change a decision?" as a follow-up question to advisory interactions.

**Practitioner assessment:** "The system works good enough for now." These are improvement vectors, not blockers.
