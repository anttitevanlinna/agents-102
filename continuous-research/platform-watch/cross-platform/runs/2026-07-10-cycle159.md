### Finding 2: Cognitive Debt Mitigation Landscape — Mostly L1; Explanation Gate is Best Controlled Evidence

**Source A:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20206 — Sankaranarayanan, Feb 2026 — [academic/research]
**Source B:** https://leaddev.com/ai/ai-coding-creates-two-kinds-of-debt-youre-only-measuring-one — Infobip/Jurić, Jun 18, 2026 — [domain trade publication]
**Source C:** https://queue.acm.org/detail.cfm?id=3807966 — Margaret-Anne Storey, Apr 22, 2026 — [academic/research]
**Source D:** https://martinfowler.com/fragments/2026-04-02.html — Martin Fowler, Apr 2, 2026 — [practitioner direct]
**Source E:** https://vivekhaldar.com/articles/cognitive-debt-is-leverage/ — Haldar, Jul 4, 2026 — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** Feb–Jul 2026
**Agent level:** team
**What:** The cognitive debt problem has reached L3 convergence, but structural MITIGATIONS remain at L1 (cultural rules) or early L2 (single controlled study). Five mitigation streams found:

1. **Explanation Gate (Sankaranarayanan, N=78):** IDE-embedded teach-back protocol. LLM-as-judge enforces teach-back before code integration. Result: 77% → 39% failure rate. STRONGEST CONTROLLED EVIDENCE. Not deployed at team scale by any named company yet.

2. **Infobip longitudinal (Jurić, 225 interns, 8 cohorts):** Mode of use determines outcome, not frequency. Two interns with identical AI usage frequency: +3 vs 0 skill growth. Practices: "try first, check with AI second," AI-free checkpoints, senior mentors as comprehension coaches. Most practically detailed team-level implementation found.

3. **Storey Triple Debt Model (ACM Queue Apr 2026):** Formalizes Technical Debt + Cognitive Debt + Intent Debt. Mitigations: architecture comprehension maps, knowledge audits (who understands what), living documentation, tests that document reasoning. One student team case study (comprehension collapse at Week 7). Level 2.

4. **Martin Fowler (Apr 2, 2026):** TDD as comprehension mitigation at scale. Insight: LLMs can help teams understand legacy code — AI as comprehension aid, not just generator. Level 1 (fragment-length opinion).

5. **Shopify (Thawar via BVP):** Cultural mandate — "2-3 layers below" rule, weekly demos instead of velocity metrics. Not automated. Level 2 but cultural enforcement only.

**Counter-position (Haldar, Jul 4, 2026):** "Cognitive Debt is Leverage" — programmers always selectively forget lower layers when climbing abstraction stacks (compilers, OS, cloud). The solution is agent verification, not human comprehension. If agents become as reliable as compilers, the comprehension argument dissolves. Structural reframe: we're stuck in the middle — enough automation to create distance, not enough to create trust. This is a reasonable structural counter-argument, not yet evidence-backed.

**Gap:** No company has confirmed deploying Litt's specific tools (code explainer docs, embedded comprehension quizzes, micro-worlds) at team scale. Litt's proposals remain personal-practice/conference-proposal stage. No company measures comprehension as a PR gate metric.
**Evidence level:** L2 for Sankaranarayanan; L2 for Infobip; L1 for all others.
