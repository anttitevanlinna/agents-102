### Finding 3: Agentforce Practitioner Lessons — Agilcon Implementation Reality
**Source:** [What implementing Agentforce in 2025 taught us](https://library.agilcon.com/what-implementing-agentforce-in-2025-taught-us/) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Agilcon (Salesforce consulting partner) published five lessons from implementing Agentforce across a region in 2025: (1) demos don't face reality — production is fundamentally different, (2) org readiness matters more than model capability, (3) 80/20 rule — focus on Level-1 ROI-ready use cases first, (4) data quality is the prerequisite, (5) change management underestimated. ## Orientation

Three signals this cycle:

1. **M&A consolidation is the structural story of Q1 2026.** IBM+Confluent, Meta+Manus, Databricks+Quotient, Zendesk+Forethought, ServiceNow+Moveworks — plus OpenAI-AWS distribution. The platform landscape is consolidating faster than it's deploying. For CTOs: the vendor you evaluate today may look different in 6 months. This reinforces the "bet small, build competence" strategy.

2. **Practitioner absence after 61 cycles is now definitive.** HR, compliance, and operations agent deployment is happening behind closed doors (vendor-driven domains), not in public practitioner channels. This is not a research gap to be closed — it's a structural characteristic of these domains. Our research method (follow people who publish) has an inherent limit here. Alternative: domain trade press interviews, conference presentations, vendor pages naming customers.

3. **SAP Joule counter-evidence approaching Level 3.** Bloomberg/VW + CIO.com 60% skip rate + E3 Magazine 60% skip + community forum technical issues = four independent negative signals converging. Joule's challenge is compounded: the product isn't ready AND customers can't absorb AI during S/4HANA migration. This is the strongest counter-evidence against any vertical SaaS agent product.
