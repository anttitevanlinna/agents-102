## Key findings

### Harvey — Spectre is internal, not a client product
Fetched both Artificial Lawyer articles (April 3 and April 14, 2026) directly.
Spectre is Harvey's own internal agent infrastructure. Used by Harvey's ~800 employees, not by law firms.
Gabe Pereyra presents it as a conceptual model for what law firms could build — but:
- No named law firm deployments of Spectre-like systems
- Pereyra explicitly says "agents still make mistakes and those mistakes can be unintuitive"
- No measurable outcomes provided
This is a Level 1 signal (opinion/framework) presented as a Level 3 finding in some coverage. Flag accordingly.

New deployments confirmed: HSBC legal platform (vendor announcement), Axiom partnership (April 8, 2026).
Known friction: ethical wall problem is unresolved and Harvey calls it "the most important unsolved problem."
Pricing opacity: 60% price cuts after pushback suggest rack rates are not real market prices.

### Sierra — crossed $150M ARR, new healthcare deployment
Sacra (independent financial analysis) confirms $150M ARR as of January 2026.
New deployment: Blue Shield of California AI voice agent via Stellarus partnership — 4.4/5 CSAT across thousands of member interactions. This is the strongest independent outcome metric found for Sierra.
New product: Level 1 PCI-compliant payments via AI agent (April 2026).
Limitations now visible in practitioner review aggregation: pricing unpredictability, support gaps during live incidents, workforce management/visibility gaps limiting long-term ROI.

### Decagon — $4.5B tender offer complete, but black box friction emerging
Tender offer completed March 4, 2026. 100+ large enterprise customers confirmed.
Black box problem: users cannot diagnose why agent did what it did — G2 Ticket Resolution 7.9/10 (lowest category score).
Contract minimums ($95K-$590K+) and 6-week white-glove onboarding filter out non-enterprise.
Heavy data quality dependency — poor internal docs degrade performance significantly.
These are the first friction signals visible beyond vendor content.

### Pilot AI Accountant — still Level 1 despite 2+ months post-launch
Launched February 4, 2026. Trade coverage (Accounting Today, CPA Practice Advisor, Insightful Accountant) is entirely CEO-quoted. Zero independent accountant or customer accounts in April 2026. SoftwareAdvice reviews note "much of the real work still needs people." 
"Zero human intervention" claim remains extraordinary and unverified.
Basis: no new developments found in April 2026 window.

### Sana — acquired by Workday November 4, 2025
No longer an independent AI-native. "Sana for Workday" rolling out to Workday customers in 2026.
Josh Bersin analysis (March 2026) is vendor-aligned — Bersin simultaneously became Workday partner.
Nordic signal: Swedish AI Reform initiative provides free access to 2.3M Swedes — public sector initiative, not enterprise agentic deployment.
For CTOs: the "third option" (buy AI-native for HR/knowledge) in this category collapsed into the Workday incumbent.

### New AI-native startups (April 2026)
Most significant new name: Distyl AI ($175M, $1.8B valuation). 
Investigation result: Distyl AI is vendor-managed enterprise AI — they run it for you, not a self-serve platform. Substack post (Distyl's own) has zero named deployments, zero outcome metrics. Pure vendor marketing content. Does not cross the three gates.
Overall: no new AI-native startup has crossed the evidence threshold in April 2026. The Q1 2026 funding environment ($300B in venture, per Crunchbase) is creating many announcements but the evidence-to-announcement ratio remains very low.
