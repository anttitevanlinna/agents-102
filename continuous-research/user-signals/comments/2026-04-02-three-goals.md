**Observation:** Three implicit goals for any company adopting agents: (1) Speed of evolution — each agent/team/process improves continuously and in parallel. (2) Speed of convergence — find the 5 winning processes out of 200, fast, through action not evaluation. (3) Maintain sufficient compliance — audit, data residency, regulatory floor. Centralized platforms optimize for #3 and sacrifice #1 and #2. Distributed agents nail #1 and #2 but the assumption is they can't do #3. Challenge: compliance through doctrine (skill files carrying rules, agents internalize them) + output auditing, not platform enforcement. This is how regulated industries govern HUMANS today — training + policies + output audit, not central approval of every decision.

**Gap:** Research has no framework for evaluating platforms against these three goals simultaneously. All platform analysis optimizes for one (usually compliance/governance) while ignoring the other two. Need to reframe platform comparison through the three-goal lens.
