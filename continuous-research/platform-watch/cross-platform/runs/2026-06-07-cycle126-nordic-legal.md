## Nordic Findings

### Deloitte State of AI in the Nordics 2026 — structural readiness/capability gap confirmed
**Source:** https://www.deloitte.com/fi/fi/Industries/technology/research/state-of-ai-in-the-nordics.html — [domain trade publication / survey report]
**Date:** April 8, 2026
**Nordic type:** Nordic-origin deployment (survey of 170 Nordic executives, 4 countries)
**What:** 55% infrastructure-ready vs. 14% talent-ready — 41-point gap. Only 5% report high expertise in agentic AI; 49% expect agentic transformation 3+ years out. Organizations with 40%+ employee AI access jumped from 37% to 56% year-over-year, suggesting breadth but not depth. Strategic preparedness declined sharply (61% → 43%). 69% have implemented AI at scale only in IT/cybersecurity.
**Evidence level:** L3 (survey-based, 170 executives, no named deployers)

## Harvey LAB / Legal AI Findings

### Harvey LAB — primary source verified, scores confirmed
**Source 1:** https://www.harvey.ai/blog/introducing-harveys-legal-agent-benchmark — [vendor press release / technical publication]
**Date:** May 6, 2026
**Source 2:** https://www.harvey.ai/blog/legal-agent-benchmark-initial-results — [vendor press release / technical publication]
**Date:** May 26, 2026
**What:** LAB = 1,250+ tasks across 24 legal practice areas, evaluated against 75,000+ expert-written rubric criteria. "All-pass" grading: a task only passes if EVERY rubric criterion passes — zero partial credit. Structure mirrors law firm assignment → execution → partner review cycle. Task design led by Harvey's Julio Pereyra and Spencer Poff; external contributors include Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Mistral, academic partners.
**Evidence level:** L2 (vendor-produced; methodology transparent and open-sourced to GitHub)

### Harvey LAB — exact model scores (verified from primary source)
**Source:** https://www.harvey.ai/blog/legal-agent-benchmark-initial-results — [vendor technical publication]
**Date:** May 26, 2026
**What:** Verified scores under all-pass standard:
- Claude Opus 4.7: **7.1%** (overall leader; leads corporate transactions + funds)
- Claude Sonnet 4.6: **5.4%** (leads privacy, tax, private-client work — statute comparison tasks)
- Claude Opus 4.6: **4.2%**
- GPT-5.5: **2.1%** (leads regulated + emerging-company work — research-heavy tasks)
- Gemini 3.5 Flash: **0.8%**

Cost/latency: Opus 4.7 = ~$50.90/task, ~22 minutes. GPT-5.5 = ~3x cheaper. Gemini 3.5 Flash = <6 minutes (4x faster). Different models lead different practice areas — no single dominant model.
**Evidence level:** L2 (vendor-produced benchmark; methodology auditable via GitHub)

### Harvey LAB — methodology criticism and market bias concerns
**Source:** https://www.lawnext.com/2026/05/some-thoughts-on-harveys-launch-of-lab-an-open-source-long-horizon-benchmark-for-legal-ai-agents.html — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** May 19, 2026
**Author:** Bob Ambrogi (LawSites/LawNext — established legal tech analyst)
**What:** Three concerns raised: (1) Market participant bias — Harvey defines "good legal work" in a benchmark used to evaluate Harvey's competitors; (2) Open-source theatre critique (Houfu Ang) — repository "maintained almost exclusively by in-house staff," limited external contributor shaping; (3) Submission normalization opacity — how scores are normalized not fully published. Competing benchmarks noted: LegalBench, CUAD, LEXam, BigLaw Bench (Harvey's prior version).
**Evidence level:** L3 (practitioner analysis from credible legal tech journalist)

### Harvey LAB — civil-law gap, HAQQ-LAB response
**Source:** https://haqq.ai/blog/civil-law-legal-ai-benchmark — [domain trade publication / vendor]
**Date:** May 2026
**What:** LAB covers common-law jurisdictions only. ~150 countries operate on civil law (60%+ of world population). HAQQ responded by open-sourcing HAQQ-LAB: 16 tasks across UAE, DIFC, Saudi Arabia, Lebanon, Egypt, Qatar + 4 out-of-jurisdiction traps. Nordic legal systems are civil-law adjacent (Nordic civil law tradition) — LAB results may not directly transfer to Finnish, Swedish, Norwegian, or Danish practice.
**Evidence level:** L2 (vendor response, methodology published)

## Orientation

The Nordic picture sharpens in one direction: infrastructure deployment is accelerating (56% of orgs at 40%+ employee access) but agentic depth remains negligible — only 5% of Nordic organizations report high agentic expertise (Deloitte, April 2026), and Nordea's mechanism gap (10,000 users → €150M savings) remains opaque. The Harvey LAB verification confirms the cycle-125 finding is accurate — Opus 4.7 at 7.1% under strict all-pass grading — but adds critical nuance: the benchmark covers common-law jurisdictions only, which means direct applicability to Nordic legal markets (civil-law tradition) is unestablished, and the benchmark's market-participant bias (Harvey grading Harvey's competitors) is a legitimate methodological concern raised by credible legal tech analysts.
