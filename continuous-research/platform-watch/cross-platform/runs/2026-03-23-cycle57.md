## Convergence Patterns (Operations/Supply Chain)

**Pattern A: Communication agents are the entry point.** DHL, Circle Logistics, Flexport, and Uber Freight all started with AI agents handling phone calls and emails — the lowest-risk, highest-volume operational communication. This is the "first rung" of agentic supply chain.

**Pattern B: Procurement negotiation is the most mature agentic use case.** Pactum (50+ enterprises), Zip ($355B processed), and emerging players all point to procurement as where autonomous agents are furthest along. Clear rules, measurable outcomes, defined boundaries.

**Pattern C: Constrained autonomy beats end-to-end.** Counter-evidence from SCMR (Torres Arpi Acero) shows narrow task-specific agents outperform ambitious end-to-end systems. Aligns with cross-domain convergence (Patterns 30, 37).

**Pattern D: Verification gap is wide.** Nearly all measurable outcomes are vendor-sourced. Independent verification is minimal. This is structural — operations practitioners don't publish.
