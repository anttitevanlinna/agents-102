## Findings

### Finding 1: WIP Explosion Confirmed at Platform Scale — CircleCI 2026 Data
**Source:** https://circleci.com/blog/five-takeaways-2026-software-delivery-report/ — [domain trade publication, own platform data]
**Date:** 2026 (covers 2025–2026 pipeline data, 8M+ PRs, 5,000+ teams)
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** CircleCI's pipeline dataset shows workflow runs up 59% YoY (generation side up), but median main-branch throughput fell 7% and main-branch success rate hit a five-year low of 70.8%. Feature branch activity climbed 15% for median teams while production delivery declined. Only 1 in 20 teams (top 5%) managed to scale both generation AND delivery simultaneously.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 — convergence across platform-scale dataset (not survey opinion)
**Key claims:**
- 59% increase in daily workflow runs (AI driving generation spike)
- 7% DECLINE in median main-branch throughput (delivery side collapsing)
- 70.8% main-branch success rate — five-year low
- 95% of teams cannot scale both generation and delivery
- Top 5% outlier: feature branch throughput 2.4x median, but Rob Bowley flags potential measurement artifact

## Pattern Assessment

**WIP explosion / Little's Law applied to AI:** This cycle crosses from Tier 0 (internally generated) to **Level 3 convergence.** The CircleCI platform-scale dataset (8M+ PRs), Rob Bowley's independent practitioner critique, Armin Ronacher's February essay, and Thoughtworks analysis all identify the same structural phenomenon from different angles: AI drives generation up, review capacity stays fixed, WIP explodes, delivery collapses. The circular pattern: individual throughput up → organizational delivery down. The evidence is now sufficient to publish.

**Connection to absorption bottleneck (existing L4 pattern):** The CircleCI findings are quantitative confirmation of the absorption bottleneck in software delivery specifically. The numbers (59% workflow increase, 7% throughput decline, 5-year-low success rate) give us concrete metrics to add to the absorption-bottleneck.md file.

**Copilot Cowork:** First independent review breaks the silence (Level 2). Review is consultant-tier, not enterprise-tier. Verdict: M365-native repeatable workflows work; exceptions require human review; standalone Claude Cowork more capable. The review drought closing with a partner review (not a customer review) is meaningful — access remains restricted.

**Claude Mythos:** Level 1 confirmed, cybersecurity-first rollout. No general timeline. Budget planning implication: fourth pricing tier above Opus.

**OpenAI Frontier:** 15th+ consecutive zero practitioner accounts. Named adopters now public but silent. This is now a finding in its own right — the announcement-to-deployment gap exceeds 60 days with zero practitioner evidence.
