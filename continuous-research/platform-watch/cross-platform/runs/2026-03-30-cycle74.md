## Findings

### Finding 1: Mollick — "Management Is the AI Superpower" (The Spec-Writer Thesis)
**Source:** [One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/management-as-ai-superpower) — [practitioner direct / academic]
**Date:** January 2026
**What:** Ethan Mollick (Wharton) ran an experimental MBA class where students built startups in 4 days using Claude Code and Gemini. The students who succeeded were experienced managers — not coders. His thesis: traditional management documentation formats (PRDs, design intent docs, Five Paragraph Orders) transfer directly as AI prompts. "The skills that are so often dismissed as 'soft' turned out to be the hard ones." The manager becomes the spec writer because management always was spec writing — communicating intent clearly enough for someone (now something) else to execute. The critical management skills: clear goal-setting, effective feedback/evaluation, and knowing "what good looks like" in your domain. Counter-evidence acknowledged: the "jagged frontier" means you don't reliably know what AI will handle well, and evaluation burden can exceed time savings on tasks under one hour.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment, n=class, well-documented). The thesis is strong but the evidence is one experimental class, not production teams. However, it connects to observable patterns at StrongDM (spec-driven development), Karpathy (program.md), and Spotify (engineers write specifications).

### Finding 2: Mollick — "Leadership, Lab, and Crowd" Organizational Model
**Source:** [One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/making-ai-work-leadership-lab-and) — [practitioner direct / academic]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** Mollick proposes a three-layer leadership model. **Leadership** must paint a vivid picture of the AI future, model AI use personally at every meeting, provide clear experimentation zones, and build massive incentives for transformational discoveries. **The Lab** is a dedicated cross-functional team (subject matter experts + technologists) that builds, tests, and distributes solutions — "building, not analysis or abstract strategy." **The Crowd** is everyone else, who need clear guidance on what to try and how they'll be rewarded. Key counter-evidence from Mollick himself: official company AI tools max at ~20% usage despite 40%+ of workers using AI secretly — indicating massive trust/incentive failures in current leadership approaches.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (opinion/framework from a well-credentialed academic). No production evidence of this model working yet. But the "secret cyborg" data point (50%+ using AI at work secretly) is independently confirmed across multiple surveys.

### Finding 3: Anthropic — Four Leadership Functions Distributed, Not Eliminated
**Source:** [benkuhn.net](https://www.benkuhn.net/leadcats/) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** July 2024 (historical context — pre-dates freshness window but describes current practice at a reference company)
**What:** Ben Kuhn (Anthropic) breaks leadership into four functions: (1) Overall Direction (mission/vision/roadmap), (2) People Management (coaching/hiring/career), (3) Project Management (cadence/delegation/progress), (4) Technical Leadership (technical direction/review). At Anthropic, these are distributed across different people — the manager is NOT the technical leader. Inference team: managers do hiring/coaching/priorities while multiple ICs provide technical leadership. Interpretability team: Chris Olah owned direction + technical leadership, Shan Carter managed people + project management. The title is "Member of Technical Staff" — anti-hierarchy. Management is function, not status.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, practitioner direct). This is the most detailed description of how an AI-native company actually distributes leadership. The distributed model challenges the "player-coach" assumption — it's not one person doing both, it's different people holding different functions.

### Finding 4: Intercom — "Player-Coach" Model Named and Operationalized for Non-Engineering
**Source:** [Intercom blog](https://www.intercom.com/blog/inside-the-ai-first-support-team/) and [Intercom blog](https://www.intercom.com/blog/how-ai-is-evolving-support-careers/) — [practitioner direct, with vendor caveat]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** Declan Ivory (VP Support, Intercom) explicitly names the "player-coach" model for AI-first support leadership: "The traditional support leader doesn't map cleanly to an AI-first organization. You need a new layer: leaders who are part strategist, part operator." These leaders analyze AI agent performance, refine content, debug handoffs, AND coach the team. Named role transitions: Beth-Ann Sher went from help center manager to Senior AI Knowledge Manager ("from administrative to strategic"). Robb Clarke (RB2B) went from Head of Technical Operations to Head of AI. Four new leadership roles defined: AI Operations Lead, Knowledge Manager, Conversation Designer, Automation Specialist. 40% of mature teams now report agents spending more time training/optimizing AI systems than handling customers.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (practitioner direct, but Intercom IS the vendor — they're describing their own transformation using their own product). The specific role transitions (named people, before/after roles) add credibility beyond pure marketing. The "player-coach" naming is significant — it's the first explicit practitioner label for the emerging leadership model.

### Finding 5: Karpathy's program.md — The Manager-as-Programmer Pattern
**Source:** [GitHub (karpathy/autoresearch)](https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** Karpathy's AutoResearch framework operationalizes the "manager as spec writer" concept literally. The human writes program.md (a specification with research directions, constraints, and baseline metrics to beat). The agent reads program.md, modifies code, runs experiments, commits improvements or reverts failures. The human never touches the code. "The human programs the organization. The agent programs the model." This is the purest expression of the management-becomes-programming thesis: the manager's artifact is a markdown specification, not a Gantt chart or a sprint backlog.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single practitioner, but Karpathy is a high-signal reference). The pattern matches StrongDM's "attractor repository" (zero code, just three markdown spec files) and Spotify's engineer role shift (natural language task description → AI generates → human reviews).

### Finding 6: Spec-Driven Development as Leadership Pattern — Convergence Signal
**Source:** [Addy Osmani / O'Reilly](https://addyosmani.com/blog/good-spec/) — [practitioner direct]; [Medium / Vishal Mysore](https://medium.com/@visrow/spec-driven-development-is-eating-software-engineering-a-map-of-30-agentic-coding-frameworks-6ac0b5e2b484) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** Addy Osmani (Google Chrome) published a detailed guide on writing specs for AI agents — treating specification writing as the new core engineering (and implicitly, management) skill. Separately, a mapping of 30+ agentic coding frameworks shows spec-driven development becoming the dominant pattern. "Specifications may become the primary interface between humans and software systems." This converges with Karpathy's program.md, StrongDM's attractor repo, Spotify's task description model, and Mollick's PRD-as-prompt thesis. When the spec IS the work product, the person writing the spec IS the leader — regardless of their title.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence). At least 6 independent signals: Karpathy, StrongDM (McCarthy), Spotify (Honk workflow), Mollick (MBA class), Osmani (Google), and the 30+ framework mapping all point to the same pattern: specification writing is becoming the leadership act.

### Finding 7: Middle Management Compression — Real But Nuanced
**Source:** [Gartner via multiple outlets](https://peoplemanagingpeople.com/hr-strategy/ai-middle-management/) — [analyst prediction, Level 0]; [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/18/corporate-america-ai-hiring-freeze-workforce-architecture/) — [general press]; [Korn Ferry survey](https://gloat.com/blog/ai-workforce-trends/) — [general press]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** Gartner predicts 20% of organizations will use AI to eliminate >50% of middle management by end of 2026. Korn Ferry survey: 41% of employees report companies have already reduced managerial layers. Dell, Amazon, Microsoft, Google reported flattening structures. The compression targets coordination and information-brokering roles specifically — the "60% of a manager's workload" that is administrative/coordination. BUT the counter-signal is real: Associations Now reports middle managers are being retrained as "translators" and "change agents," not eliminated.
**Evidence level:** Level 0 for predictions (analyst). Level 1 for the compression thesis (multiple opinion sources, no named practitioner at a specific company saying "we removed management layer X and here's what happened"). The Korn Ferry survey number (41% already reduced) is the strongest data point but lacks specifics. **This is the weakest finding — lots of prediction, almost zero practitioner evidence of it actually working.**

### Finding 8: The "Agent Manager" Role — HBR Names It
**Source:** [HBR](https://hbr.org/2026/02/to-thrive-in-the-ai-era-companies-need-agent-managers) — [general press / academic]; [Beam.ai](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/what-is-an-agent-manager-the-new-role-every-ai-company-needs-in-2026) — [vendor press release, Level 0]
**Date:** February 2026
**What:** HBR formally names and defines the "agent manager" — a person responsible for making sure AI agents deliver business results. The role involves: designing when the agent acts alone, when it asks for help, and when it hands off to humans. Key claim: "domain expertise matters more than AI expertise" — the best agent managers come from roles where they already understand the business process being automated. This maps to our "conditions creator" observation (Enterprise Case A, observation 5) and to Intercom's AI Operations Lead role. The separation: "people manager" and "agent manager" are different skill sets. An organization may need both, held by different people.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (opinion, well-argued, but no named company deploying this role with measured outcomes). The domain-expertise-over-AI-expertise claim aligns with Mollick's finding and with Pattern F (domain experts who can steer agents).

### Finding 9: Fortune — "Coherence Over Control" as the Agentic Leadership Model
**Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/09/agentic-era-leadership-most-ceos-will-make-same-mistake/) — [general press]
**Date:** February 2026
**What:** Amy Eliza Wong (Silicon Valley advisor) argues the critical CEO mistake in the agentic era is tightening control when they should be enabling coherence. "Coherence" = an operating model where independent actors (human and digital) act intelligently without constant escalation because: (1) one visible shared outcome, (2) clear decision lanes with boundaries, and (3) fast-moving truth via short feedback loops. References Deloitte confirming high-trust, data-fluent organizations lead in AI adoption while stalled firms treat transformation as training rollouts.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (opinion piece, no named company evidence). But the "coherence" framing maps precisely to what we observe at Tesla (information symmetry replaces management), Anthropic (notebooks in Slack, anyone can comment), and the enterprise case ("conditions creator" sets context then gets out of the way). The pattern is real even though this particular source only frames it as prescription.

### Finding 10: Spotify — Senior Engineers Become Specification and Oversight Leaders
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/12/spotify-says-its-best-developers-havent-written-a-line-of-code-since-december-thanks-to-ai/) — [general press]; [InfoQ / QCon London 2026](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/03/spotify-honk-rewrite/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** February-March 2026
**What:** Spotify's senior engineers shifted from writing code to: (1) defining tasks in natural language, (2) supervising and reviewing AI output, (3) redirecting when agents drift, (4) architectural decisions. "Developers act more as system architects and quality gatekeepers than as direct authors." The Honk system (built on Claude Code, triggered via Slack) enables asynchronous agent work — engineers don't pair with agents in real time, they review async. This is the specification-and-oversight model at scale (7,000+ employee company, 650+ agent-merged PRs/month). The formation story matters for leadership: engineers built Honk bottom-up, co-CEO Soderstr\u00f6m then pushed it company-wide. Seed + mandate combination.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, general press sourcing, but multiple independent reports confirm the same facts). The "specification and oversight" role description is the most concrete evidence of what the day-to-day leadership act looks like in a large engineering org.

### Finding 11: Performance Evaluation in AI Teams — The Unsolved Problem
**Source:** [DORA 2025 via InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/03/ai-dora-report/) — [academic/research]; [Milestone.ai](https://mstone.ai/blog/engineering-metrics-benchmarks-high-performing-teams-success/) — [vendor, Level 0]; [Shopify mandate](https://x.com/tobi/status/1909251946235437514) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** 2025-2026
**What:** DORA 2025 (n=5,000) confirms: traditional metrics (commit volume, PR cycle time) can't separate AI-generated from human-authored work and can't prove AI ROI. AI acts as amplifier — strengthens good teams, exposes weak ones. Shopify's answer: add AI usage to performance reviews and peer review questionnaire. This is the blunt-instrument approach — measuring usage, not effectiveness. Nobody has published a working evaluation framework for "how well does this person direct AI?" The closest: StrongDM's holdout scenario approach (measuring whether output satisfies acceptance criteria the agent never saw) and Intercom's system metrics (resolution rate, CSAT, automation coverage). Both evaluate the system's output, not the person's contribution.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 for DORA data (academic research). Level 1 for everything else. **This is the biggest gap in the landscape — no practitioner has published a working framework for evaluating human performance in AI-native teams.**

### Finding 12: Anthropic Leadership Model — Long-Form Written Discourse as Coordination
**Source:** [founded.com](https://www.founded.com/dario-amodei-leadership-anthropic-transparency-culture/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** 2026
**What:** At Anthropic, Amodei regularly addresses strategy shifts in "long written Slack posts that invite equally long replies." These exchanges create "a running archive of the company's internal debates, building shared context across thousands of team members." This operationalizes what the observation file already captures as "Notebooks in Slack" — leadership through written discourse rather than meetings, dashboards, or cascade briefings. Combined with Ben Kuhn's distributed four-function model, Anthropic's leadership approach is: distribute leadership functions across ICs + coordinate through written debate + extreme hiring bar as quality control.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, multiple practitioner sources). The written-discourse-as-coordination pattern has analogues at Amazon (six-page memos) but Anthropic's version is more interactive (Slack posts inviting debate vs. Amazon's read-then-discuss format).

## Orientation

### The Picture Has Changed Substantially

Before this cycle, we had 12 company observations with three patterns (role dissolution, ritual elimination, amplification paradox) — all about what happens TO teams. Zero about how teams are LED.

After this cycle, a **leadership pattern is emerging at convergence level (Level 3)** with at least 6 independent signals. Here's the synthesis:

### Emerging Pattern G: "The Spec Is the Leadership Act"

The leader of an AI-native team doesn't manage people doing work. They write the specification that directs agents doing work — then review whether the output meets the spec.

Six independent signals:
1. **Karpathy** — program.md as the management artifact (practitioner direct)
2. **StrongDM (McCarthy)** — attractor repository with zero code, just spec files (practitioner direct)
3. **Spotify** — senior engineers write natural language task descriptions, agents implement (multiple sources)
4. **Mollick** — MBA managers outperformed coders because management IS spec writing (academic/practitioner)
5. **Osmani (Google)** — published spec-writing guide as the new core skill (practitioner direct)
6. **30+ agentic frameworks** — spec-driven development as dominant pattern (ecosystem mapping)

**What this means for the CTO question:** The manager of an AI-native team needs the same skills as a good engineering manager always needed — clear intent, quality judgment, evaluation ability — but expressed as written specifications rather than verbal direction. The medium changed. The skill didn't.

### The Leadership Functions Don't Disappear — They Redistribute

Anthropic's four-function model (Ben Kuhn) + Intercom's player-coach naming (Declan Ivory) + Tesla's data-as-manager model (Joe Justice) suggest three different solutions to the same problem:

| Company | How leadership distributes | Scale |
|---------|--------------------------|-------|
| Anthropic | 4 functions split across ICs; management = coaching/hiring only | ~1,100 |
| Tesla | Data replaces information-brokering; KPIs replace direction-setting | ~70,000 |
| Intercom | Player-coach: strategist + operator in one person for AI systems | Mid-size |
| Shopify | CEO mandate + structural decision change (prove AI can't do it) | ~10,000 |
| StrongDM | 3 humans write specs; no traditional management at all | 3 |
| Every | ~1 person per product; coordination model unknown | ~15 |
| Spotify | Senior engineers become spec-writers + reviewers; management layer unclear | ~7,000 |

**The pattern:** Management-as-information-brokering disappears (Tesla, Anthropic, Spotify all eliminate it through transparency and tooling). Management-as-direction-setting becomes spec-writing. Management-as-people-development remains human. Management-as-quality-control becomes the primary daily act.

### Two Open Questions for Next Cycle

1. **How do you evaluate the person who writes the spec?** Nobody has solved this. It's the next frontier. The spec-writer's quality determines the AI team's output — but measuring spec quality is as hard as measuring management quality always was, just with a different artifact.

2. **Does the player-coach model scale?** Intercom names it for mid-size CS teams. Does it work at Spotify scale? At enterprise scale? Or does it split into Anthropic's distributed model as teams grow?
