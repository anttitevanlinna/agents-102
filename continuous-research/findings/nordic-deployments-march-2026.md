### 1. Legora: From $1.8B to $5.55B Valuation (March 10, 2026)

**The biggest Nordic agentic story this month.** Swedish legal AI company Legora (formerly Leya/Judilica) raised $550M at a $5.55B valuation for US expansion. This triples the valuation from our February assessment ($1.8B).

Legora now explicitly positions 2026 as "The Year of Agents in Legal AI" — distinguishing between chatbots (2023), multi-step workflows (2025), and agents completing "complex, end-to-end legal work — autonomously, in context, with human oversight built in" (2026).

Also acquired Walter AI to expand its agentic platform.

- **Company:** Legora (formerly Leya)
- **Country:** Sweden (Stockholm)
- **Domain:** Compliance & Legal
- **What the agent does:** End-to-end legal work — document analysis, contract review, legal research — with human oversight
- **Adoption stage:** Scaling (400+ organizations → US expansion)
- **Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, but massive scale + independent coverage)

### 2. Sana Labs: Acquired by Workday for $1.1B, Launched as "Sana for Workday" (March 2026)

Swedish AI company Sana Labs was acquired by Workday for $1.1B (November 2025). In March 2026, Workday launched "Sana for Workday" — a new Sana Self-Service Agent integrated into the Workday platform.

Sana's agent platform lets users query multiple LLMs, manage data/documents, create images and presentations, record/analyze meetings, and create prompts/workflows/subagents.

Josh Bersin (independent HR tech analyst) covered the launch: "Workday and Sana Unveil A Bold New Strategy For AI."

- **Company:** Sana Labs (acquired by Workday)
- **Country:** Sweden (Stockholm)
- **Domain:** HR & People Operations
- **What the agent does:** Knowledge discovery, work automation, self-service HR — agents that query across enterprise data, manage documents, create workflows
- **Adoption stage:** Platform launch (GA March 2026)
- **Evidence level:** Level 1-2 (independent analyst coverage, but no customer deployment data yet)
- **Source:** https://joshbersin.com/2026/03/workday-and-sana-unveil-a-bold-new-strategy-for-ai/ [practitioner analysis — Josh Bersin]
- **Source:** https://siliconangle.com/2026/03/17/workday-introduces-ai-knowledge-discovery-work-automation-platform-sana/ [domain trade publication]
- **Source:** https://newsroom.workday.com/2025-09-16-Workday-Signs-Definitive-Agreement-to-Acquire-Sana [vendor press release — Level 0]
- **Finding category:** Platform Announcement (just launched, no deployment evidence yet)

**Significance:** This changes the Workday Illuminate assessment. Our February finding was "zero practitioner evidence for Workday." With Sana integration, Workday now has genuine AI-native technology (Swedish-built). The question shifts from "does Workday have real AI?" to "will Sana's capabilities translate inside Workday's enterprise install base?"

### Finland AI Act Enforcement Update

Finland activated national AI Act supervision laws on January 1, 2026 — confirmed as first EU member state with fully operational enforcement powers. Key enforcement structure:

- Transport and Communications Agency (Traficom) as the primary AI authority
- Finnish Safety and Chemicals Agency (Tukes) for product safety aspects
- National Non-Discrimination and Equality Tribunal for discrimination complaints

**High-risk deadlines:** August 2, 2026 for AI systems in recruitment, HR decisions, creditworthiness, and insurance pricing. Penalties: up to €35M or 7% global revenue.

- Source: https://valtioneuvosto.fi/en/-/1410877/national-supervision-of-eu-artificial-intelligence-act-to-begin-laws-on-powers-of-authorities-to-take-effect-at-start-of-the-year [government source — authoritative]
- Source: https://www.hannessnellman.com/news-and-views/blog/eu-ai-act-latest-regulatory-developments-in-finland/ [domain trade publication]

## What We Did Not Find

1. **No new Nordic companies deploying agents in business processes beyond those already tracked.** The Nordic deployment landscape remains thin — dominated by the same 8-10 companies from February.

2. **No Nordic practitioner blog posts or conference talks about agent deployment experiences.** The public practitioner layer remains nearly invisible in the Nordics.

3. **No Danske Bank AI agent updates.** Despite being listed in our Nordic leaders, no new public information on agentic deployments found.

4. **No Nordic HR agent deployments.** With Sana Labs now inside Workday, this may change — but as of March 2026, zero evidence of Nordic companies using autonomous HR agents.

5. **No specifics on Nordea's agentic capabilities.** The 1,500 job cuts are AI-linked, but what exactly Nordea's agents do remains opaque. The "POC to 10,000 users" story lacks specifics.

6. **No Gjensidige follow-up data.** Eva's 70% claims target was set months ago — no public update on actual achievement against target.
