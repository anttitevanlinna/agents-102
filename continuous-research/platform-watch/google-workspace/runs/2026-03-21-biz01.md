### Google Workspace Studio: No-code agent builder now GA for business users
**Source:** https://aitoolanalysis.com/google-workspace-studio/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Additional:** https://workspaceupdates.googleblog.com/2025/12/workspace-studio.html — [vendor press release]
**Additional:** https://www.computerworld.com/article/4101761/with-workspace-studio-google-wants-workers-to-build-their-own-ai-agents.html — [general press]
**Date:** 2025-12 announcement, GA rollout completed 2026-03-19
**Agent level:** Personal → Team (shareable via Drive)
**What:** Workspace Studio is now GA across all Business Standard/Plus and Enterprise plans. Business users can build agents by describing workflows in natural language. Tested capabilities: invoice detection from email → Sheets logging works in 30 seconds, correctly reading PDF attachments even with misleading subject lines. However, complex conditional logic ("if amount > $500, request manager approval") requires "logic brain" — the no-code claim is approximately 80% true. Limits: 50 executions/day on standard plans, 500 on Enterprise, max 20 steps per agent, max 100 agents per user. ~10 deep integrations (Asana, Jira, Mailchimp, Salesforce) vs Zapier's 6,000+. Agents are shareable via Drive, creating a personal→team promotion path. Kärcher (with Google Cloud partner Zoi) reported 90% reduction in drafting time for product planning.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single practitioner hands-on test + one vendor-sourced customer case)

### Gemini Enterprise pricing restructured — no more pick-and-choose AI seats
**Source:** https://www.eesel.ai/blog/gemini-workspace-pricing — [domain trade publication]
**Additional:** https://workspaceupdates.googleblog.com/2026/02/google-workspace-ai-expanded-access.html — [vendor press release]
**Date:** 2026-02 to 2026-03
**Agent level:** Company (purchasing decision)
**What:** Previously, orgs could buy Gemini Business ($20/user/mo) or Gemini Enterprise ($30/user/mo) add-ons for select users. That pick-and-choose model is gone — AI features are now bundled into Workspace plans at higher subscription prices, forcing orgs to pay for all seats. Starting March 1, 2026, the promotional AI access ends; customers must purchase the "AI Expanded Access" add-on for higher usage. This is a significant cost issue for companies where only a fraction of employees use AI tools. Gemini Enterprise (the Cloud platform, distinct from Workspace AI) remains at ~$30/user/mo separately.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (pricing is verifiable fact; impact assessment from trade analysis)

**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (convergence of reliability concerns from multiple independent sources)

## Orientation

**The big picture for business users in Google's ecosystem (March 2026):**

Google now has THREE distinct layers for business agents, each at a different maturity level:

1. **Gemini in Workspace (Personal, shipping):** Chatbot-level assistance in Gmail/Docs/Sheets/Meet. Fast but inaccurate. Sheets integration disappointing. Not agentic — it's a copilot. Comparable to M365 Copilot but with weaker enterprise support.

2. **Workspace Studio (Personal→Team, just GA):** The most interesting new development. Real no-code agent building that works for simple workflows (email parsing, data extraction, scheduling). The 80/20 rule applies: 80% of tasks are genuinely no-code, 20% need "logic brain." Limits are meaningful (50 executions/day standard, 20 steps max). The sharing-via-Drive mechanism creates a primitive promotion path from personal to team. But it's brand new (GA completed March 19, 2026) — no track record.

3. **Gemini Enterprise / Cloud platform (Company, GA but unproven):** Enterprise search + agent orchestration. On paper the most powerful. In practice: no independent deployment evidence. The Agent Designer is GA but reviewers say features feel immature.

**What changed from our developer-focused research:** Workspace Studio is the genuinely new finding. It's the first Google product that puts agent-like capabilities (not just chat) into business users' hands without developer involvement. The execution limit (50/day standard) keeps it at personal/team level — you can't run a company on 50 agent executions per day. But for a sales person automating their weekly reporting or an HR manager triaging applications, it's a real starting point.

**The pricing trap:** Google eliminated pick-and-choose AI seats. Organizations now pay for all employees whether they use AI or not, plus need the "AI Expanded Access" add-on for higher usage. This creates a CTO question: "Am I paying $30/user/mo for everyone to get capabilities only 20% will use?" Microsoft has the same problem but Google is making it worse by removing the targeted option.

**Counter-evidence found:** Accuracy problems are real and documented by multiple independent sources. Regulated workflows (legal, compliance, finance) face hallucination and auditability risks. Quota surprises happen. The Forrester analyst's warning that "creating agents is beyond the skill level of most employees today" directly contradicts Google's "every employee builds agents" marketing.

**For our personal→team→company framework:**
- Personal: Gemini in Workspace (shipping, chatbot-level) + Workspace Studio (just GA, real but limited)
- Team: Workspace Studio sharing (mechanism exists, no evidence of adoption)
- Company: Gemini Enterprise Cloud (GA, no independent evidence)
- Promotion path: Workspace Studio → Gemini Enterprise is technically possible but no one has documented doing it
