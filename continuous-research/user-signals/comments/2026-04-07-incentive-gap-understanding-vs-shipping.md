**Observation:** The output-outcome gap is an incentive problem, not a technology problem. You get fired for not shipping. You rarely get fired for shipping the wrong thing (unless total disaster). This organizational immune system protects builders and punishes thinkers. AI for customer understanding and strategy is the highest-value use case but nobody pursues it because: (1) building is visible, understanding is invisible, (2) tools entered through the "coding door" so PMs don't see them as understanding tools, (3) the spec for understanding work is harder to write than the spec for building work. No amount of better AI tools fixes this — it's a cultural/incentive change. Moltke parallel: the General Staff (thinkers, not fighters) was the most prestigious posting. CTOs need to make the understanding function as prestigious as the building function.

**Gap:** Zero research on "AI for deeper customer understanding" or "AI for sharper strategy." The entire ecosystem — platforms, training, frameworks, tooling — is pointed at making things faster, not understanding better. This may be the single biggest usage gap in the agentic landscape. Need OODA cycle: who IS using AI agents for customer understanding and strategy development?
