### EU AI Act High-Risk Deadline: Formally Postponed to December 2027
**Source:** https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/ — [official legislative source]; corroborated by https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/eu-legislators-agree-to-delay-for-highrisk-ai-rules — [domain trade publication / law firm analysis]
**Date:** May 7, 2026
**Agent level:** Company/Policy
**What:** Council of EU and European Parliament reached provisional agreement on May 7, 2026 to delay Annex III high-risk AI system obligations. New deadlines: standalone high-risk AI systems — December 2, 2027; high-risk AI embedded in products — August 2, 2028. The August 2026 deadline that was a knowledge-state concern is now formally postponed via the "Digital Omnibus" deal.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (official legislative agreement, multiple law firm confirmations, EU Parliament press release)
**Key claims:**
- Standalone high-risk AI: December 2, 2027 (was August 2026)
- High-risk AI embedded in products: August 2, 2028
- Non-retroactivity: systems on market before new deadlines do NOT need to comply unless significantly modified (Article 111)
- This means RPA+agent hybrid systems already in production before Dec 2027 may never face compliance requirements
**Strategic implication:** The "live August 2026 compliance deadline" urgency for Nordic enterprises is gone. New deployments in high-risk categories (employment screening, credit scoring, critical infrastructure) still face the Dec 2027 deadline.

## Orientation

Two structural updates this cycle: (1) EU AI Act August 2026 deadline is formally postponed to December 2027, removing the compliance urgency for Nordic enterprises extending RPA investments; (2) UiPath's coding agent integration announcement (May 12) signals a strategic repositioning — they're betting that coding agents will manage UiPath automations, not that RPA teams will add agentic reasoning. This is a meaningful shift: the primary buyer for UiPath may transition from RPA-center COO to engineering-led AI team. The evidence desert for independent deployments persists at six months post-April — but the category direction is clearer: hybrid architecture, extend-not-replace, with coding agents as the new orchestration layer.
