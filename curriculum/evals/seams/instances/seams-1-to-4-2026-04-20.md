## Cross-seam observations

### What's working across all four seams

1. **Mood contracts are load-bearing and honored.** All four LEFT modules close in their deliberate mood and all four RIGHT modules open assuming that mood. The 2026-04-20 "Mood (deliberate)" paragraphs added to content-strategy.md are showing up in the actual module files — Bridges and Connections are engineered, not accidental.

2. **Bridge-to-Connections is consistently strong.** Every Bridge sentence sets up the next Connections paragraph, and every Connections paragraph credits the LEFT module's work before pivoting. The "Module 1 was for you / Last module you were the librarian / You've had three agents search" pattern is a real craft achievement — each explicitly names what the student did, then pivots.

3. **Parked moods are honored.** M3's wonder is explicitly parked for M5 and M4 does not accidentally resolve it. This is the single hardest move in the arc (it's tempting to tidy up lingering doubts) and it's executed correctly.

4. **Vocabulary is disciplined.** Almost no leaks across seams. Terms get introduced in their proper module. The "skills" term being foreshadowed in M2's Debrief as a crux-move but named as a first-class concept in M4 is a particularly clean example of progressive disclosure.

### Recurring weak pattern — implicit artifact handoff

**Three of the four seams (1, 2, 3) have minor artifact-path fraying** where a file produced by LEFT is silently assumed by RIGHT rather than explicitly named, OR a convention lives in content-strategy.md / curriculum/CLAUDE.md but not in the student-facing module files. Examples:

- Seam 1: `prework/` as the target folder for snake.html and meetings.txt — convention exists, not stated in prompts.
- Seam 2: `module-1/CLAUDE.md` scope boundary, `module-2/challenge.md` consumption by M2 exercise, scaffold zip arrival timing.
- Seam 3: `module-2/challenge.md` → `module-3/question.md` derivation, M2 memory's role during M3 live retrieval, scheduled-agent homework thread.

**Pattern:** content-strategy.md and curriculum/CLAUDE.md are the source of truth for where artifacts live and who reads them. But a student running the training alone (self-study, or reading the site without trainer facilitation) only sees module files + exercises + prework. When a convention only lives in the meta-docs, the student-facing path has a silent assumption.

**Suggested fix (one-session sweep):** add one line per seam to the RIGHT module's Meta section or Connections — "You arrive with [named files from LEFT]" — making the implicit handoff explicit. Cheap; catches all three fraying points.

Seam 4 is the counter-example: M4's Phase 1 prompt names the M3 files it consumes by path. That's the template for the other three.

### Mood-arc cross-seam pattern

The joy → compound → unease → deeper-unease sequence is working as engineered. One specific craft win worth naming: **M3 and M4 stack two flavors of unease** (output-quality doubt from M3, risk doubt from M4) without either resolving the other. This is the most fragile part of the arc — it would be very easy for M4 to accidentally lecture the student out of M3's doubt, or for M3 to leak a fix. Neither happens. The arc's load-bearing feature is that each "unease" is a DIFFERENT dimension, not the same doubt repeated — M3 is *"is this right?"*, M4 is *"is this safe?"* They stack rather than conflict.

M5's mood contract (*rescue — "ahh, this is actually fixable"*) will need to resolve M3's doubt specifically, while M4's residual stays on the table for M7 / no-resolution. This seam (M4 → M5) wasn't judged here but is the highest-risk one in the arc given what M5 has to pull off.

### No BREAK conditions found

All four seams PASS on mood (the most engineered dimension), PASS or FRAY on artifact, PASS on vocabulary, PASS on framing. No seam is in BREAK state. The fraying is cosmetic — silent assumptions rather than contradictions — and fixable with per-seam one-line edits.
