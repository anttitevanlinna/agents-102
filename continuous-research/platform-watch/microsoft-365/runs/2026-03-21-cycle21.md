### Finding 10: CUA capability cliff — academic evidence
**Source:** [arXiv — UI-CUBE benchmark](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.17131) — [academic/research]
**Date:** Late 2025
**Agent level:** Company (CUA)
**What:** UI-CUBE academic benchmark reveals a "sharp capability cliff" for computer use agents. Simple UI interactions achieve 67-85% success, but complex workflows drop to 9-19% (vs. 97.9% human performance). This is strong counter-evidence for enterprise CUA deployment — the tasks enterprises need (complex multi-step workflows) are exactly where CUA performance collapses.
**Evidence level:** Level 4 (academic research, strong methodology)
**Key claims:**
- Simple CUA tasks: 67-85% success
- Complex CUA workflows: 9-19% success
- Human baseline: 97.9%
- Gap widens with task complexity — exactly the enterprise use case

## Orientation

This cycle's most valuable output is twofold: (1) confirming what hasn't changed — the announcement-to-deployment gap remains the widest of any vendor, and (2) reaching Level 3 convergence on five patterns from the practitioner analyst community. The practitioner community is actively warning about incomplete governance, agent sprawl, licensing confusion, and security gaps — while finding zero deployment success stories to report. The DLP confidential email bug and CVE-2026-26144 Excel exploit are concrete evidence that M365 agent security is not production-ready.

**The next inflection point is May 1, 2026** (E7 + Agent 365 GA). If the gap persists through May, it transitions from "early" to "structural." The April public preview of E7 may produce the first testable signals.

**Recommended next Microsoft cycle focus:** Wait for May 1 inflection. Next rotation should prioritize Google or OpenAI unless Microsoft makes a significant move before then.
