Practitioner experience from telecom sector: CollabAI/MobAI requires extraordinary facilitation and arrangements — on-site work, dropping other responsibilities. Can be done as an extraordinary sprint with results for the short period. But it does not scale or replicate organically.

**Pattern confirmed across two independent experiences** (enterprise cybersecurity + telecom). The methodology produces results during the facilitated event but doesn't sustain or spread without the facilitator. This is structural, not incidental.

**Why it doesn't replicate organically:**
- Requires physical co-location (on-site)
- Requires full attention (dropping other work)
- Requires skilled facilitator
- Results are bounded to the sprint period
- No self-sustaining mechanism after the facilitator leaves

**Contrast with what DOES replicate organically:** Compound engineering (solo + AI) spreads person-to-person with zero facilitation overhead. One person sees another's CLAUDE.md, tries it, improves it. Peer cascade happens naturally because the practice is individual.

**Implication for training design:** CollabAI/MobAI works as a time-boxed WOW event (like a workshop) but should not be positioned as a sustained working method. The training should explicitly transition people FROM the facilitated format TO self-sustaining practices (compound engineering, SwarmAI with lightweight coordination).
