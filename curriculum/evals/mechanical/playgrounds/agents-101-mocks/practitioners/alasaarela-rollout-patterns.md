# Alasaarela — agentic rollout patterns (paraphrased summary)

Cached paraphrased summary of a practitioner post on what makes agentic rollouts land in mid-size engineering orgs vs. stall.

## What lands

- **Builder leaders, not transformation officers.** The rollouts that compound are run by people doing the work, not by program managers reporting on it.
- **Sub-team-by-sub-team, not org-wide.** Pace set by the slowest team forces the fastest team to wait — kills momentum. Each sub-team sets its own bar and timeline; the leader holds the bar, not the timeline.
- **Skeptics with a written counter-position beat skeptics with vague unease.** Forcing the bar onto paper makes the conversation tractable.
- **Forcing functions over deadlines.** Quarterly planning, all-hands demos, board updates — events the org can't push — beat dates on a roadmap.

## What stalls

- Vendor-led training. Demos at 2pm don't convert engineers whose bar is "Friday at 3am."
- Tool dashboards. Counting Claude Code logins instead of shipped improvements drifts to gaming the metric.
- Mandates without an off-ramp. *"Everyone uses X by Y"* fails; named opt-out *"opt out by writing your bar"* works.
- Centralised AI teams. Knowledge concentrates, doesn't compound across the org.

## Implications for the Kaleva rollout

- The OKR shape (every team ships OR writes a bar) matches the pattern.
- Q3 offsite as a forcing function is correct.
- Watch for tool-first drift — Maija's named risk #2.
- Watch for skeptic capture — Maija's named risk #1.
