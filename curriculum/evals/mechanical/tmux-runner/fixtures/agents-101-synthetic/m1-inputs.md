## Phase 2 — tuned StoryBrand, the colleague-help beats (answer in one message)

- The colleague I help: a founder or GM who just signed a pricing/packaging change and now has to make it real without breaking the customers they already have.
- What they're struggling with: they have the strategy deck but no one who owns the messy middle — the rollout, the pilot, the angry SMB account on day three.
- How I help: I run the pilot that de-risks the change before it hits everyone, and I own the handoffs nobody wants.
- What it looks like when it works: the change ships, the churn doesn't spike, and the sales org actually knows what to say.
- My one-line guide-to-the-colleague: "I live with the consequences of the decision, not just the recommendation."

## Phase 3 — strengths (3-5, one line each)

- Making pricing decisions with incomplete data without freezing.
- Owning the unglamorous middle — the handoffs and operational seams others skip.
- Translating a strategy deck into a rollout a real team can run on Monday.
- Staying calm in a pricing fire drill.

## Phase 4 — anti-branding hate-list (specific, not categories)

- Strategy decks where every slide says "iterate" and no slide says who does what by when.
- Pricing changes shipped to 100% of customers on day one with no pilot.
- Onboarding handoffs where sales says "ops has it" and ops says "sales has it."
- Meetings where everyone nods and nothing is owned.
- The word "synergy" used as a plan.

## Phase 6 — free iteration (look & feel)

- Make it feel like Linear's site: lots of white space, restrained typography, calm.
- Add a short section at the top: "What I'm working on right now" with the usage-based pricing pilot.
- Keep it understated. No hero gradients, no "Let's connect" call-to-action button.
