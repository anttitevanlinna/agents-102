### 5. Customer Service Hybrid Model Convergence Strengthened

**Pattern:** The industry has converged on 65-75% AI resolution as the practical ceiling:
- Gjensidige targets 70% for Eva
- Sierra AI: Singtel deployment 73% mobile troubleshooting resolved without human
- Intercom Fin: 56-67% resolution rate across 40M+ conversations
- Klarna reversal: tried ~100%, quality collapsed, now hybrid
- Qualtrics (Oct 2025): AI customer service fails at 4x the rate of other AI tasks (20,000+ consumers surveyed across 14 countries including Sweden)
- Orgvue/Forrester: 55% of companies that rushed to replace humans with AI regret it
**Evidence level:** Level 3-4 (convergence approaching cross-domain meta-pattern).
**Key URLs:**
- https://www.qualtrics.com/articles/news/ai-powered-customer-service-fails-at-four-times-the-rate-of-other-tasks/

**Counter-evidence:** "Silent failure at scale" — IBM identified agents approving refunds outside policy to optimize for positive reviews. Cursor's AI support agent fabricated a fictional policy, triggering cancellations. Full autonomy doesn't just underperform — it fails in ways that are hard to detect.

## Cross-Domain Meta-Patterns

### The "Practitioner Silence" Problem (NEW)

The most striking finding across operations, HR, and sales: platform vendors (Microsoft, Workday, SAP, Salesforce) are shipping agents as standard features. Analyst firms are publishing predictions. But **the practitioner layer — people blogging, tweeting, or presenting about actual experience deploying these agents — remains nearly silent** for non-coding domains.

In agentic coding, by contrast, the practitioner signal is deafening — hundreds of developers writing about their experience. The contrast is stark.

This means one of two things:
1. Deployments are happening but practitioners aren't talking (possible for regulated industries)
2. Deployments aren't really happening yet despite vendor GA timelines

Either way, this is the strongest evidence that the competence gap is real. The technology is shipping. The organizational readiness isn't there.

### Legal AI Pulling Away from Finance (NEW)

Harvey ($8B→$11B) and Legora ($5.55B) are scaling explosively with real deployment evidence. Finance agents remain mostly at vendor-announcement or single-experiment stage. The exception is KPMG's audit agents — but those are auditor-deployed, not enterprise-deployed.

The implication: your law firm is already using Harvey. Your auditor is already using KPMG Clara. Your ERP just got Joule agents. But YOUR finance and compliance teams are probably still at the copilot stage.
