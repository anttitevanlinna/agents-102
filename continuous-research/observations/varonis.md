# Varonis — The Only Documented Transform-in-Place Case

**Type:** Transform-in-place | **Size:** Engineering org | **Evidence:** Moderate case
**Key sources:** Varonis blog "From Hype to Culture" (2026)

---

## Why This Matters

Every other well-documented AI-native team is greenfield or born-this-way. Varonis is the **only enterprise that has documented transforming an existing engineering organisation** to AI-native with measured outcomes.

## How They Did It

**Five-phase framework:**
1. **Detect Enablers** — Identify influential technical leaders across groups
2. **Rollout** — Deploy tools with support structures
3. **Monitoring** — Track adoption and quality metrics
4. **Level-Up** — Deepen practices, build AI Guild
5. **Completion** — 100% adoption achieved

**Supporting structures:**
- **AI Guild:** Peer structure for knowledge sharing and practice development
- **Team-level adoption scorecards:** Made adoption visible between teams. "Encouraged respectful competition and surfaced friction points."
- **Champions:** Influential engineers identified in phase 1, empowered to lead adoption

## Measured Outcomes

- 100% engineering adoption
- PR coding time decreased 152%
- Review time decreased 75%
- PR cycle time decreased 96%
- Fewer production bugs (count not published)

## What We Don't Know (Needs Deeper Investigation)

- How long did each phase take?
- What resistance was encountered and how was it overcome?
- Was this tool adoption or genuine way-of-working transformation? (The distinction matters — 90% AI-assisted PRs without workflow change is common and doesn't count)
- What happened AFTER 100% adoption? Is it sustainable?
- What does the AI Guild actually do? Composition, cadence, authority?
- How were scorecards designed? What metrics, how displayed, what behaviour did they drive?

## Compound Triggers Observed

Varonis appears to have stacked multiple transition patterns:
- **Seed practitioners** (phase 1: detect enablers)
- **Peer cascade** (guild + scorecards)
- **Mandate/support** (organised rollout with monitoring)
- This combination (seed + guild + visibility) achieved what no single pattern does alone

## Key Insight

**The detect-enablers-first approach.** Varonis didn't start with tools or mandates. They started by finding the people who would make it work — the influential engineers who would become seeds. Phase 1 is identification, not deployment. This aligns with the seed practitioner finding: you need seeds, and you need to place them deliberately.
