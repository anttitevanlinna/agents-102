## Findings

### C.H. Robinson is the strongest traditional-enterprise candidate found

**Source:** https://www.chrobinson.com/pt-br/about-us/newsroom/news/2026/how-a-lean-ai-strategy-delivers-results/ — [practitioner direct, but deployer-owned/sponsored-content caveat]
**Date:** 2026-01-14
**Agent level:** company
**What:** C.H. Robinson is a 120-year-old logistics company, not a software or AI-vendor company. CEO Dave Bozeman frames the transformation as Lean-first rather than technology-first: map quote-to-cash, identify bottlenecks/waste, then deploy AI agents into routine execution while logistics experts move to complex work. Reported outcomes: 40% productivity gains, 30+ AI agents, 3M+ tasks, quotes in 32 seconds rather than hours.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 single-case, self-reported. Stronger than vendor case study because it is the deploying company, public-company leadership, and tied to operating metrics; weaker than practitioner-direct playbook because it is not an individual-authored methods post and has no independent customer confirmation.
**Key claims:** Named CEO; Lean operating model as the adoption frame; quote-to-cash workflow as the target; 40% productivity; 30+ agents; 3M+ tasks; 32-second quotes.

**Source:** https://www.chrobinson.com/en-us/about-us/newsroom/news/2026/lean-ai-growing-shipper-impact/ — [practitioner direct, deployer-owned caveat]
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Agent level:** company
**What:** C.H. Robinson reports hundreds of connected AI agents embedded in operations and orchestrated by the Always On Logistics Planner. Its analysis of truckload shipments from Jan 2024 to Jan 2026 links AI-handled orders/appointments with 11% faster speed to market on average, 7% better on-time pickups on average, 42,000 automated appointment locations, and 42% fewer unnecessary return trips for missed pickups.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 single-case, self-reported.
**Key claims:** Hundreds of agents; two-year operational analysis; customer-facing logistics metrics; orchestration across adjacent workflow steps.

**Source:** https://www.chrobinson.com/en-us/about-us/newsroom/news/2026/lean-ai-at-morgan-stanley-tmt-conference/ — [practitioner direct, investor-event summary caveat]
**Date:** 2026-03-04
**Agent level:** company
**What:** Bozeman, CFO Damon Lee, and CSIO Arun Rajan presented Lean AI as a structural operating-model advantage at Morgan Stanley TMT. The company claims every deployment is tied to KPIs, accountability, and measurable outcomes, with 450 engineers/data scientists working alongside freight experts.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 single-case, self-reported.
**Key claims:** 450 technical builders + freight experts; KPI/accountability discipline; operating model as moat.

This is not independent proof of causality, but it is closer to P&L attachment than most AI case studies.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 single-case, self-reported/public-company summary.
**Key claims:** AI tied to financial performance narrative; durable productivity claims through Q1 2026.

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

- A manufacturing, retail, industrial, or energy practitioner publishing their own Curran/Charles-style playbook with forcing-function metrics, structural changes, spend posture, marketplace mechanics, and dated commitments.
- Independent customer/shipper confirmation of C.H. Robinson's reported AI-driven logistics improvements.
- A traditional-enterprise case where the published playbook clearly starts from individual AI ability and converts it into team-level throughput.
- A traditional enterprise with the equivalent of Ramp's internal builder marketplace or Intercom's team-2x operating artifacts.

## Orientation

C.H. Robinson partially closes the traditional-enterprise gap: it is a named, non-software, logistics company with a process-first AI operating model and measurable outcomes. It does **not** fully close the Curran/Charles-style playbook gap because the evidence is deployer-owned communications, not a practitioner-authored field manual; it lacks independent customer confirmation and a visible marketplace/spend posture.

The better label is: **first strong Level 2 traditional-enterprise candidate, still not convergence-grade and not yet practitioner-playbook grade.**
