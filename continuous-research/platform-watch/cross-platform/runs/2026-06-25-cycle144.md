### Finding 2: Legion LegalTech Lawsuit — First Court Challenge to Export Control Directive
**Source:** [The Next Web June 24, 2026](https://thenextweb.com/news/legion-legaltech-sues-us-anthropic-access) — domain trade publication; [TechSpot June 24, 2026](https://www.techspot.com/news/112880-startup-sues-us-government-blocking-access-anthropic-fable.html) — domain trade publication; [Medianama June 24, 2026](https://www.medianama.com/2026/06/223-legion-legaltech-sues-us-over-anthropic-ai-export-controls/) — domain trade publication
**Date:** June 24, 2026
**Agent level:** company (enterprise business model on Anthropic stack)
**What:** San Jose-based Legion LegalTech Corp filed suit in Washington, D.C. federal court on June 24, seeking to vacate the Commerce Department Bureau of Industry and Security directive and obtain a preliminary injunction blocking enforcement. Legion builds AI-powered litigation drafting software (automating pleadings, discovery requests, court documents) and employs Canadian nationals who work remotely from Canada. When the June 12 directive required Anthropic to block foreign nationals, Legion's Canadian development team lost access — harm described as "immediate, irreparable, and existential." Legion's core legal argument: no existing export control statute or regulation covers hosted AI models or their outputs; the BIS order therefore exceeds the government's statutory authority. First-of-kind challenge — no prior court case on export control scope applied to cloud-hosted AI inference.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (court filing, independently confirmed across 3+ trade publications)
**Key claims:**
- Legion filed June 24, 2026 in D.C. federal court — first court challenge to the Fable 5/Mythos 5 directive
- Legal argument: export controls don't cover hosted model outputs (novel statutory scope question)
- Harm: Canadian remote employees (development team) lost access; legal tech platform depends on frontier models
- Seeking: vacate BIS order + preliminary injunction while case proceeds
- CTO relevance: if Legion wins, it establishes that export control authority does not reach cloud-hosted AI — would remove a new risk category that appeared in enterprise procurement this month
