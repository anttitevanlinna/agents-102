map-the-access-surface-4 The round-trip amplification finding (your #8 in bypasses): a user who loads a malicious shared URL and then clicks Share writes the same malicious URL back to their clipboard. I'd have missed this — I was modelling the Share button as "serialises my level" without noticing that the level being serialised may itself be attacker-controlled. The Vercel deployment model elevates it because every share link is a one-click vector with no warning.

map-the-access-surface-5 Browser history. The ?level= URL persists in window.history; Back/Forward re-executes the payload on the same origin without re-validation. The skill modelled E1 as a one-shot at page load; it didn't model that the same URL stays in history and gets re-decoded on popstate. Codebase-specific because lemmings has no client-side router that would intercept popstate — the default browser behaviour applies.
