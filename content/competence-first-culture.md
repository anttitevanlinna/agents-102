# The People Side of Agentic Transformation

*Written by Claude. Steered by Antti Tevanlinna. For Kaisa Tikka-Mustonen, SVP People & Culture, F-Secure Corporation.*

We're making a big shift in company culture for AI. If you're in a People role, you already sense this is your question — not technology's. Here's what we did.


# What We Saw

We had the tools. We had smart people. We had steering groups with interesting discussions. But we were not building a culture that could carry this forward. The energy was in pockets. It was not scaling.

We knew we needed to move. We did not know exactly where. But we have learned to trust that building shows you the way faster than planning does.


# I Don't Want to Describe the Exact Target State

And that turned out to be the point.

The people who built agents discovered a future I could not have designed. Our CRO — a sales leader, not engineering — became the biggest champion, building tools and showing others what is possible. People who had never written a line of code were creating solutions for their own work.

They need to see it for themselves. No strategy deck could have produced what our people discovered when they started building.


# What We Did

We created the conditions. We cleared the path so that people could experiment without asking permission. We made sure leadership showed up — not as sponsors watching from the side, but as participants building with their own hands.

That last part matters more than anything else we did.

Directors and VPs filled the first training in one week. Nobody mandated attendance. Two leadership team members went on the waitlist. By week three the entire leadership team was building agents.

We have led people through many transformations. We have never seen pull like this.


# What Changed

The culture shifted. Not because we announced a culture change. Because people started doing different things.

The conversation moved from "should we use AI?" to "what should we build next?" That is a different organisation. People who build think differently from people who discuss.

You cannot plan a culture shift. You can create the conditions for one. The right people, the space to experiment, leadership that participates — not just endorses.

Trust your people. They will surprise you.

I made it happen. You can too.
