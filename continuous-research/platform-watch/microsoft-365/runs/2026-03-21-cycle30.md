## Findings

### Finding 1: Copilot user preference collapses to 8% when alternatives available (Recon Analytics, 150K+ respondents)
**Source:** [Recon Analytics — AI Choice 2026: Why Licenses Don't Equal Adoption](https://www.reconanalytics.com/ai-choice-2026-why-licenses-dont-equal-adoption/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** January 2026 survey, published ~February-March 2026
**Agent level:** personal
**What:** Recon Analytics surveyed 150,000+ U.S. enterprise users (July 2025–January 2026). When employees have simultaneous access to Copilot, ChatGPT, and Gemini, only 8% choose Copilot (vs. 70% ChatGPT, 18% Gemini). When Copilot is the only available tool, adoption reaches 68% — proving employer provisioning, not user preference, inflates adoption metrics. 70% of users initially preferred Copilot (it's embedded in their apps), but after trying alternatives, only 8% kept choosing it. This is the strongest independent evidence that M365 distribution advantage ≠ product preference.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single large-scale survey, independent analytics firm — not vendor-sourced)
**Key claims:**
- 8% Copilot preference when all three platforms available (150K+ respondents)
- 70% → 8% preference shift after users try alternatives
- ChatGPT 70% preference, Gemini 18% in three-way comparison
- 68% adoption when Copilot is only option (provisioning effect)
- ChatGPT conversion rate 83.1% vs Copilot 35.8% vs Gemini 34.0%
- Copilot market share: 18.8% → 11.5% (39% contraction, Jul 2025-Jan 2026)
- Gemini crossed Copilot in late November 2025 to claim #2 position

### Finding 5: Copilot accuracy NPS persistently negative — distrust as primary churn driver
**Source:** [Stackmatix — Microsoft Copilot Adoption Statistics & Trends 2026](https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/copilot-market-adoption-trends) — [practitioner analysis], citing Recon Analytics survey data
**Date:** January 2026 data, page updated ~March 2026
**Agent level:** personal
**What:** Copilot accuracy NPS trajectory: -3.5 (Jul 2025) → -24.1 (Sep 2025) → -19.8 (Jan 2026). A persistently negative accuracy NPS means users who try Copilot are more likely to distrust than recommend it. 44.2% of lapsed Copilot users cite "distrust of answers" as primary reason for stopping use. This is the trust problem that makes agent adoption impossible — if users don't trust the copilot for simple tasks, they won't delegate autonomous multi-step work to agents.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (independent analytics, large sample)
**Key claims:**
- Accuracy NPS: -3.5 → -24.1 → -19.8 (Jul-Sep-Jan)
- 44.2% of lapsed users cite distrust as primary churn reason
- Trust deficit blocks agent adoption progression (personal copilot → personal agent → team agent)

## Orientation

**The Copilot trust problem is the agent adoption blocker.** The Recon Analytics data (150K respondents) quantifies what practitioners have been saying: Copilot doesn't earn user preference. The 8% figure when alternatives are available is devastating for a product with 450M+ distribution seats. The accuracy NPS trajectory (-3.5 → -24.1 → -19.8) and 44.2% distrust churn rate explain why.

**This directly blocks the agent story.** Microsoft's agent narrative requires users to first trust Copilot for simple tasks (summarize, draft), then trust agents for complex multi-step work (Cowork, Agent 365). If the foundational copilot experience has a -19.8 accuracy NPS, users won't delegate autonomous work. The personal→team→company progression assumes personal copilot trust exists. It doesn't — and the data proves it at scale.

**Copilot Cowork silence is now structural.** The March 21 search (12+ days post-announcement) finds zero independent reviews. This is the same product that Microsoft positioned as the answer to Anthropic's Claude Cowork (which triggered a $285B enterprise software selloff). If "late March" broader Frontier access had happened, someone would have written about it.

**For the CTO question:** Microsoft has the broadest distribution but the weakest user preference. The E7 strategy bundles governance (Agent 365) with a copilot that 92% of multi-platform users don't prefer. The introductory discounts (10-15%) and partner program acceleration suggest Microsoft itself expects friction. The May 1 GA is the next inflection point — if adoption signals remain absent through May, the deployment gap transitions from "early" to "structural."
