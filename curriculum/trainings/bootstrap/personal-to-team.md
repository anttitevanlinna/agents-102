# From Personal to Team

## Big Idea
Your challenge brain works for you. Making it work for your team is a different problem.

## Meta
- **Primary Bloom's level:** Analyze → Evaluate
- **Prework:** 8 short stories on scaling personal agents to team/company level (TODO: curate). Must include variance — skill-based, recurring cloud agent (cron, not enterprise platform), etc. Goal: show there's no single right way.
- **Homework:** announcement-to-deployment gap reading (inoculates against vendor pitches)
- **Materials (trainer):** access-model template, governance checklist (who can add to the brain? who changes the rules? who sees the output?)
- **Plug points:** the participant's organization — who would own this brain?

## What You'll Learn
After this module, you will be able to:
- **Identify** what is personal vs. team-ready in your challenge brain (credentials, private context, assumptions only you hold)
- **Redesign** for shared access and governance
- **Evaluate** where the brain breaks when handed to a pair partner
- **Distinguish** the promotion path: personal → team → company as different products

## Exercises
- **Promote your challenge brain** — Your Module 2 brain knows your challenge. It also knows things only you know — your read of who's political, the shortcut you take because you trust your own judgment, the credential you quietly put in a rule. List everything personal about it: credentials, private context baked into rules, assumptions only you hold. Redesign for the team: shared access, governance (who adds to the brain? who changes the rules? who sees the output?), the parts that need to be owned by a named person rather than an individual hunch. Hand the whole thing to a pair partner. Can they run it? Can they get useful output without your context? Where does it fail — and is the failure technical or a question of shared understanding?

## Key Concepts (Emergent)
- **The promotion path** — personal, team, company brains are different products. Promotion means redesigning, not just sharing.
- **The Access-Trust Gap** — 54-95% of enterprises have AI access. 5-22% have trust/production readiness. Gap always >35 points. The bottleneck is trust-building, not technology.
- **The personal → team gap is not technical.** It's about shared context, trust, and ownership.

## Plug Points
- **Ownership model.** Who in your organization would own this brain? Who's accountable when its output is wrong?

## Bridge
You can build agents, secure them, evaluate them, and scale them to a team. What if you didn't have to build each one by hand?
