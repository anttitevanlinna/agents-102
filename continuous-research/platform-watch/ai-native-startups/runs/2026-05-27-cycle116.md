### Harvey: First Commissioned-but-Independent Outcome Study (RSGI, Nov 2025)
**Source:** https://legaltechnology.com/2025/12/02/the-impact-of-legal-ai-a-deeper-dive-into-the-rsgi-harvey-adoption-report/ — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** 2025-12-02
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** RSGI (the UK research firm behind FT Innovative Lawyers) surveyed 40 Harvey customers — 29 law firms, 11 corporate legal teams across 5 regions — commissioned by Harvey but structured to be independent. Power users save 36.9 hours/month; standard users save 15.7 hours. In-house power users save 28.3 hours.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single study, Harvey-commissioned, methodology partially disclosed, named participants including A&O Shearman, Orrick, Paul Weiss, Mayer Brown)
**Key claims:**
- 36.9 hrs/month savings for law firm power users (self-reported survey, not independently audited)
- 40 customers in study; named firms include Am Law 100 members
- "Commissioned by Harvey on the basis it would be entirely independent" ≠ fully independent. Strong Level 2.
- Time savings are productivity-input claims, not outcome claims (no billing data, client satisfaction, error rates)

## Orientation

Legal AI has confirmed a two-horse race: Harvey owns US Am Law 100 depth; Legora (Swedish-origin) executing parallel at $5.55B and >$100M ARR globally. Harvey's LAB benchmark launch is a strategic move to own evaluation infrastructure before the race closes. The most important new finding: Salesforce Agentforce's 31% six-month active rate (Valoir, independent) mirrors the AI SDR churn pattern and Klarna reversal — the category-level problem across ALL AI-native and incumbent players is *activation and sustained use*, not initial adoption. No player has solved this except Clay in the narrow enrichment-only lane.
