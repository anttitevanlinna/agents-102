## Part A: Transformation Methods Findings

### Finding 1: No External Convergence on "Rotation Model" (immerse → learn → return → next cohort)

**Search result:** Zero independent practitioners describe the specific rotation model. Searches for "rotation model AI learning cohort enterprise," "immersion cohort AI deployment," and "AI adoption rotation immersion return" returned no practitioner descriptions of this model.

**Closest external signal:** Ethan Mollick's "Leadership, Lab, Crowd" framework (oneusefulthing.org) describes the "Lab" as rotating high-performing individuals from the Crowd into a central AI lab for sprint-style development. The Lab recruits "your best tinkerers from the Crowd" and runs "two-week sprints." This is structurally adjacent — taking people from operational roles to focused AI work — but does NOT describe a cohort rotation back to seed new teams.
Source: [Mollick, oneusefulthing.org](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/making-ai-work-leadership-lab-and) — [practitioner direct], date unspecified (2025-2026)

**Closest adjacent framework:** Enterprise AI adoption literature describes a "champion" model (1 champion per 8-10 engineers) and sequential department rollout, but neither is the rotation/seed model.
Source: [eLearning Industry — scaling AI adoption](https://elearningindustry.com/from-hundreds-to-thousands-scaling-enterprise-ai-adoption) — [domain trade publication], Feb 4, 2026

**What the absence means:** The rotation model as "immerse → learn → return → next cohort" is not in the practitioner literature. The cell division model (seed team → spawn new teams → coach stays available) found in our user signals also has zero external examples. Both models are ahead of what the public research community has described.

**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner signal, no external convergence)

**Counter-evidence search:** Explicit searches found zero practitioners saying rotation-based AI learning doesn't work. Absence is neutral, not negative.

### Finding 2: Skill Progression Sequence (mob → solo → swarm → scaling architecture) — Partial External Convergence

**What the signal claims:** Effective AI adoption follows mob → solo → swarm → scaling architecture as a learning sequence.

**External evidence:**

The technical AI learning path literature (agentic AI roadmaps) independently describes a solo → multi-agent → orchestration progression for technical practitioners:
- Month 1-3: Solo RAG pipelines and single agents (individual practice)
- Month 4+: Multi-agent systems for tasks "too large for a single agent"  
- Advanced: Enterprise orchestration (designing systems, not operating them)

Source: [Analytics Vidhya — Agentic AI Learning Path 2026](https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/blog/2026/01/agentic-ai-expert-learning-path/) — [domain trade publication], Jan 2026

**Structural parallel:** The progression from "individual agent user" to "multi-agent workflow designer" to "enterprise orchestration architect" matches the solo → swarm → scaling architecture arc in our signal. The "mob" (group learning) phase is not described in technical learning paths — it's added by the practitioner signal as the on-ramp.

**What the Atlassian MobAI article confirms (adjacent):** Mob programming with AI distributes knowledge — "By using AI in a mob setting, the entire team learns how to prompt, critique, and refine AI outputs. This prevents the 'AI power user' silo and spreads skills evenly." This validates mob as a group knowledge-spreading mechanism, not just individual skill building. Published December 9, 2025.
Source: [Atlassian Engineering Blog — Mobbing with AI](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/atlassian-engineering/mobbing-with-ai) — [practitioner direct], Dec 9, 2025

**Mollick (Wharton, April 1, 2026):** Framing is: people must start as AI users → develop organizational practices → transform structures. Does not name mob/swarm/scaling as steps, but the trajectory matches.
Source: [Wharton Executive Education](https://executiveeducation.wharton.upenn.edu/thought-leadership/wharton-at-work/2026/04/what-leaders-need-to-navigate-ai-and-disruption/) — [domain trade publication], Apr 1, 2026

**Evidence level:** Level 2 (structural parallel from multiple sources, no direct naming of mob→solo→swarm→scaling sequence). The sequence exists in technical learning literature (solo→multi-agent→orchestration). The mob as group on-ramp is our practitioner signal's unique contribution.

**Critical gap confirmed:** Nobody in public literature frames AI competence training as "learning to scale AI" — the progression from prompting → orchestration → verification architecture skill. Training literature uniformly says "learn to prompt." This is a genuine insight gap.

### Finding 3: Swarm AI Absorption Ceiling — Extended Beyond Engineering (Partial Level 3)

**What the signal claims:** Agents generate faster than humans can absorb. 95% right but overwhelming. New bottleneck = human absorption capacity. Cycle 89 confirmed at Level 2-3 in engineering context.

**New non-engineering evidence from this cycle:**

**Healthcare (confirmed L3):** 90-96% alert override rates. Medical alert fatigue is the direct analog. This is 40+ years of documented evidence (not a new finding, but confirms the pattern is domain-independent).

**Knowledge work broadly — new from this cycle:**

The Faros AI study (10K+ developers, July 23, 2025) quantifies the bottleneck mechanically: 98% more PRs merged per developer, 91% increase in PR review time, 154% increase in average PR size. While engineering-focused, the underlying math — output volume exceeds human processing capacity — is structurally identical to swarm AI contexts.
Source: [Faros AI — AI Software Engineering Report](https://www.faros.ai/blog/ai-software-engineering) — [practitioner analysis], Jul 23, 2025

**HBR, February 2026 (paywalled, title confirmed):** "AI Doesn't Reduce Work — It Intensifies It" — publication title confirmed by search. Unable to read content (403). The headline alone confirms external acknowledgment of AI creating MORE human work, not less. [SOURCE NOTE: Content unverified due to paywall]
Source: [HBR](https://hbr.org/2026/02/ai-doesnt-reduce-work-it-intensifies-it) — [domain trade publication], Feb 2026

**Decision speed as bottleneck (TOC framing — our practitioner signal):** Goldratt's TOC applied to AI: AI accelerates the cheap part (creation), leaves decision-making (the constraint) unchanged. Search confirmed TOC is being discussed in AI contexts in 2025-2026, but no practitioner independently named "decision speed" as the specific AI bottleneck using TOC framing.
Source: [Procurement Insights — TOC and AI](https://procureinsights.com/2025/01/23/transforming-the-theory-of-constraints-to-the-agent-based-development-and-implementation-model-for-todays-ai/) — [domain trade publication], Jan 2026
Source: [Stanford/Andy Bromberg — TOC for AI](https://andybromberg.com/toc-ai-stanford) — [practitioner direct], Feb 17, 2026

**Note:** Bromberg's Stanford lecture applies TOC to LLM product development (system prompts as constraints, not human decision speed). The "decision speed is the real bottleneck" insight from our practitioner signal is not yet externally confirmed.

**Little's Law framing (our practitioner signal):** Zero external sources apply Little's Law specifically to AI WIP management in knowledge work. The concept is used in agile/Kanban contexts but not in AI output absorption contexts. Genuinely novel framing.

**Evidence level:** The absorption bottleneck in non-engineering domains is at **Level 4** in the KB (confirmed in cycle 81 across 6 domains). New from this cycle: confirmation that even the HBR mainstream has published on "AI intensifies work" as of Feb 2026 — the insight is crossing from practitioner to mainstream.

### Finding 4: MobAI Facilitation Overhead — Partial External Convergence

**What the signal claims:** MobAI requires extraordinary facilitation, on-site, full attention. Works as sprint, doesn't replicate organically. Confirmed across 2 sectors (telecom + enterprise).

**External evidence:**

**Atlassian (Dec 9, 2025) — closest independent source:** Documents that mob AI sessions require a rotating facilitator role ("one person takes on the facilitator role: sharing their screen, driving the editor, and handling AI prompts"). Does NOT quantify the overhead, does not say sessions fail without facilitation, and does not address whether sessions scale organically. Atlassian's context is engineering teams doing planned sessions — not spontaneous peer-to-peer spread.
Source: [Atlassian Engineering Blog](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/atlassian-engineering/mobbing-with-ai) — [practitioner direct], Dec 9, 2025

**SXSW 2026 data (adjacent):** CMOs from 400 marketing organizations: "Every $1 on AI tools requires $2-3 in training and change management." Tool abandonment rate without facilitation: 60-70% within 6 months. With proper facilitation: <15% abandonment and positive ROI within 4 months. This is not mob-specific but confirms that group AI adoption fails without dedicated facilitation overhead.
Source: [Digital Applied — SXSW 2026 CMO AI Training Data](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/cmos-sxsw-2026-every-ai-dollar-invest-2-3-training) — [domain trade publication], 2026

**Counter-evidence searched:** "MobAI mob programming AI doesn't scale enterprise 2026" — zero results. No practitioners are publicly saying mob AI fails. Atlassian's article frames it positively. The absence of failure reports from mob AI could mean: (a) it's too new to have documented failures, or (b) practitioners who find it doesn't scale simply stop doing it and don't write about it.

**Evidence level:** Level 1-2 (our practitioner signal is the primary evidence; Atlassian confirms facilitation structure but not the failure-to-scale claim; SXSW confirms general facilitation-required pattern).

**The specific claim** — "doesn't replicate organically" — is NOT confirmed externally. It is internally consistent with the absorption bottleneck (MobAI teaches "human watches AI," which doesn't scale), but lacks independent practitioner convergence. Need 8-18 more independent reports to reach Level 3.
