# Experience-First Adoption — WOW → Competence → Sees Future → Acts

**Evidence level:** Level 3 (emerging convergence — 7+ independent sources) | **Last updated:** 2026-03-31 | **Source:** Cycle 78 + practitioner validation

**The reversal:** Standard change management says vision → strategy → tools → adoption. For AI, the sequence that actually works is reversed: **tool that gives the WOW → competence through doing → person sees their own future → they act.**

Nobody has formally named this sequence. Every source validates a different step. The practitioner who confirmed it across two enterprise cohorts (NPS 80-90%) over 1+ year is the only person running it as a deliberate methodology.

## Why Vision-First Fails for AI

Previous transformations (digital, agile, cloud) could be vision-led because concepts were accessible through analogy. A CEO didn't need to write code to envision a digital company. Agents are structurally different. "An agent that autonomously processes invoices across three systems" means nothing to someone who hasn't used one. The mental model gap isn't knowledge — it's experience. No slides bridge it.

The perception gap confirms this: 76% of executives believe employees are enthusiastic about AI. Only 31% actually are. Leaders are 2x off the mark.

Source: [HBR](https://hbr.org/2025/11/leaders-assume-employees-are-excited-about-ai-theyre-wrong) — [academic/research], Lovich/BCG + Meier/Columbia, Nov 2025

## The Seven Signals

### 1. WOW — Experience Creates Desire

**Mollick MBA experiment:** Non-engineers built working startup prototypes in 4 days. Management skills (delegation, evaluation, explaining intent) turned out to be the exact AI skills. "They already knew how to manage. All that training was accidentally preparing them for exactly this moment."

Source: [One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/management-as-ai-superpower) — [practitioner direct / academic], Jan 2026

**Coursera AI Sparks:** Voluntary peer-led monthly sessions. No mandates, no goalposts. "By far the most well-attended and popular meetings that we do at the company." The WOW delivered peer-to-peer, not top-down.

Source: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/12/26/coursera-ceo-greg-hart-amazon-leadership-lessons-bezos/) — [general press], Dec 2025

**BCG 5-hour threshold:** 79% of employees with 5+ hours hands-on training became regular AI users vs. 67% with less. In-person beats online. Only 33% say they've been properly trained.

Source: [BCG](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/ai-at-work-momentum-builds-but-gaps-remain) — [vendor press release] (consultancy survey, treat as directional), Jun 2025

### 2. Competence — Doing Builds Skill

BCG: "The real transformation happens when employees apply new skills to their actual work." Organizations need "structured opportunities to practice using AI in the core of their work, with coaching support." The 5-hour threshold suggests a minimum dose before competence takes hold.

**Practitioner validation:** Two enterprise cohorts (50 engineers NPS 80%, 12 business people NPS 90%). After 2 modules of hands-on training, people started building unprompted. The competence created pull — people found their own high-value processes without being told where to look.

Source: Direct practitioner observation, 2025-2026, Nordic software company (~500 people)

### 3. Sees Future — Person Maps to Own Role

**Workera:** "Skeptics convert when they see role-specific proof of AI impact." Not prompt tips — "professional judgment: when to use AI, when not to, how to verify quickly." Skeptics are "rational professionals, not technophobes."

Source: [Workera](https://www.workera.ai/blog/ai-adoption-will-remain-uneven-in-2026-heres-why-and-how-to-fix-it) — [vendor press release] (training vendor, directional), 2026

**Microsoft/HBR peer influence:** +8.9pp on heavy AI usage from peer influence. +10.4pp for agents specifically. Leadership communication: 0pp direct effect. The WOW transfers peer-to-peer because peers demonstrate role-specific possibility. "Hearing another coworker telling me how he used an AI...was a huge incentive."

Source: [HBR](https://hbr.org/2026/03/peer-influence-can-make-or-break-your-ai-rollout) — [academic/research], Microsoft Research (Baym, Dillon, Jaffe), Mar 2026

### 4. Acts — Person Changes Behavior

**McKinsey:** Workflow redesigners are 2.8x more likely to be AI high performers (55% vs 20%). But redesign requires competence which requires the WOW. The sequence: see possibility → learn to use → redesign own workflow → high performance.

Source: [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) — [vendor press release] (consultancy, N=1,993, treat as directional), 2025

**Practitioner validation:** The F-Secure finance person wasn't told to build tools with Claude Code for 9 months. She couldn't stop. Self-actualisation as the driver — business people adopt more eagerly than engineers because they've been consumers of tools their entire careers and suddenly become creators.

## The Precondition: Psychological Safety

**Infosys/MIT:** 83% of 500 business leaders say psychological safety measurably impacts AI initiative success. 22% have personally hesitated to lead an AI project due to fear of blame. Only 39% rate their org's safety as "very high."

Source: [PRNewswire/Infosys](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/infosys-and-mit-technology-review-insights-report-reveals-the-critical-role-of-psychological-safety-in-driving-ai-initiatives--with-83-of-business-leaders-reporting-a-measurable-impact-302643644.html) — [vendor press release] (Infosys-funded research, directional), Dec 2025

The full sequence is therefore: **Safety → WOW → Competence → Sees Future → Acts**

## The Leadership Role Is Indirect

Microsoft/HBR: leadership communication has 0pp direct effect on AI adoption. But each standard deviation of leader encouragement produces 0.38 SD increase in peer influence. **Leaders don't drive adoption. Leaders create conditions for peers to drive adoption.** This directly connects to the Conditions Creator pattern (cycle 72).

The leader's job: model AI use in meetings, create learning forums, encourage knowledge sharing, reward experimentation. NOT: mandate usage, put AI in performance reviews, send strategy decks.

## Counter-Evidence: Strategy Still Matters

**Deloitte:** Companies with formal AI strategy report 80% implementation success vs 37% without. Top-down direction outperforms pure bottom-up crowdsourcing.

Source: [Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-ai-in-the-enterprise.html) — [vendor press release] (consultancy), 2026

**This is real but not contradictory.** Strategy and experience-first are complementary, not competing. Strategy sets direction and provides air cover. Experience creates adoption within that direction. Shopify demonstrates: Lutke set the mandate (strategy) AND practices daily (experience). Duolingo and Fiverr had strategy without experience — both failed.

**The resolved model:** Strategy provides the FRAME. Experience-first provides the FUEL. You need both. But if you must sequence them: experience first, strategy second. A strategy without competent people is governance of an abstraction.

## Why the Sequence Can't Be Reordered

| Skip to... | What happens | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vision first | People nod politely, don't act — can't envision what they haven't experienced | 76% of execs think employees are excited; 31% are |
| Tools first | High adoption, zero value — 96% adopt, 4% ROI | BCG Nordic finding |
| Mandate first | Compliance without commitment — 2.2x organizational resistance | Amazon Kiro (6.3M orders lost), Duolingo (backtracked in weeks) |
| Strategy first without experience | 80% "success" on paper, but no mental models, no real transformation | Deloitte correlation ≠ causation |

## Connection to Other Patterns

- **Conditions Creator:** The Conditions Creator's operating method IS experience-first. Create the WOW → build competence → let people self-organise. The conditions creator doesn't manage adoption; they create the preconditions for the experience-first sequence to run.
- **Compound Triggers:** Experience-first explains WHY seed practitioners (Trigger 1) and peer cascade (Trigger 5) are the two most important triggers. Seeds deliver the WOW. Cascades spread it.
- **Absorption Bottleneck:** The four-phase competence progression (mob → solo → swarm → scaled AI) is the experience-first sequence applied to increasingly sophisticated AI skills. Phase 1 IS the WOW.
- **Competence-First thesis:** Experience-first is the mechanism that IMPLEMENTS competence-first. "Build competence before choosing platforms" — HOW? Through the WOW → competence → sees future → acts sequence.

## What We Did Not Find

1. **Nobody has formally named this reversed sequence.** Kolb's experiential learning cycle (1984) describes experience-before-theory generally, but nobody has applied it to AI adoption as a named methodology.
2. **No replication of Mollick's MBA experiment.** Single cohort, single instructor. Compelling but unreplicated.
3. **No direct measurement of the "WOW moment."** Described anecdotally everywhere, never measured as a variable.
4. **No Nordic evidence beyond the practitioner's two cohorts** (50 engineers, 12 business people) at a Finnish company.

## CTO Advice

1. **Don't start with a strategy deck.** Start with a hands-on session where your people build something real with AI in 2-4 hours. The WOW creates demand that no strategy deck can.
2. **Five hours minimum.** BCG data: 79% regular users with 5+ hours hands-on. Below that threshold, most people bounce.
3. **In-person beats online.** Every data point confirms this. The WOW requires shared experience and peer learning.
4. **Let peers carry it.** Microsoft/HBR: peer influence (+8.9pp) beats everything. Your job as leader: create the forum (Coursera Sparks model), not deliver the message.
5. **Strategy comes second.** Once people have the WOW and competence, strategy becomes lightweight — they know what's possible and can evaluate platforms against their own experience, not against vendor marketing.
6. **Mandates only work AFTER experience.** Shopify's mandate worked because Lutke already had the WOW and competence. Amazon's mandate failed because SVPs hadn't.
