### Finding 3: Agentforce — 77% B2B Deployment Failure Rate
**Source:** [Oliv.ai analysis](https://www.oliv.ai/blog/salesforce-agentforce-reviews-analyzed) — [practitioner analysis]; [Apex Hours](https://www.apexhours.com/agentforce-limitations-and-workarounds/) — [practitioner direct]; [Salesforce Ben](https://www.salesforceben.com/agentforce-for-salesforce-help-6-month-review-and-whats-improved/) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Analysis of actual user reviews shows 77% of B2B Agentforce deployments fail. Root causes: (1) data quality issues, (2) chat-based UX disrupts existing CRM workflows — sales teams must leave tasks to engage in separate chats. Only 31% achieve "sustainable value," primarily large enterprises with dedicated Salesforce dev teams. Salesforce Ben tested 6 practical use cases, only 2 delivered well. Architecture enforces 15 topics max per agent, 15 actions per topic. Mandatory Data Cloud subscription "putting many off." Despite this: 18,500 customers, ~$540M ARR, 50% QoQ growth.
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (multiple practitioner sources converging on same pattern)
**Key claims:**
- 77% B2B deployment failure rate (Oliv.ai user review analysis)
- Only 31% achieve "sustainable value"
- Chat UX disrupts CRM workflows — a new, specific failure mechanism
- Only 2 of 6 practical use cases delivered well (Salesforce Ben)
- 15 topics max / 15 actions per topic architectural limit

### Finding 5: 88% Agent-Specific Pilot-to-Production Failure Rate
**Source:** [Hypersense Software](https://hypersense-software.com/blog/2026/01/12/why-88-percent-ai-agents-fail-production/) — [practitioner analysis]; [Pertama Partners](https://www.pertamapartners.com/insights/ai-project-failure-statistics-2026) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** January-March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** 88% of AI agents never make it from pilot to production. Root causes are integration failures, not LLM capability: "Dumb RAG" (bad memory), "Brittle Connectors" (broken I/O), "Polling Tax" (no event-driven architecture). RAND: 80.3% overall AI project failure rate, 33.8% abandoned ($4.2M avg cost), 28.4% deliver no value ($6.8M cost). CEO involvement correlates with success: 68% success rate with sustained CEO involvement vs 11% without. Only 21% have mature AI agent governance despite 74% planning agentic deployments.
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (multiple independent analyses converging)
**Key claims:**
- 88% agent-specific pilot-to-production failure (more specific than MIT 95% for all GenAI)
- Root cause: integration issues (RAG, connectors, event architecture), not LLM capability
- CEO involvement → 68% vs 11% success rate
- RAND: 80.3% overall AI project failure, avg cost $4.2-8.4M per failed project

1. **Vertical SaaS counter-evidence now approaching Level 3 convergence across all three major platforms.** Agentforce (77% B2B failure), ServiceNow (debunked 90% claim), SAP Joule (Bloomberg 12+ clients). Root cause consistently = integration and organizational readiness, not AI capability. This directly validates our competence-first thesis.

Finland declaring agentic AI as 2026 national theme + free KAMK course = market is opening. Our Bootstrap sits at a different level (practitioner-deep, tool-specific) but the KAMK course is a competitive signal to monitor.

4. **Five new business-domain practitioners** narrow the roster gap in HR (Desjourdy, Chatterjee), legal (Daniels), compliance (Lawrence/Jin), and CS (Masin). Operations/supply chain remains the weakest domain.
