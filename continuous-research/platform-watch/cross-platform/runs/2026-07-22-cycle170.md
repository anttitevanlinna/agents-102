### [Finding 2] EU AI Act August 2 — what actually goes into effect (11 days)

**Source:** https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/eu-ai-act-august-2026-transparency-obligations-agency-checklist — [legal analysis / compliance practitioner]
**Date:** Fetched July 22, 2026 (page content current, no explicit pub date found)
**Agent level:** company
**What:** Two major enforcement mechanisms activate August 2: (1) **Article 50 transparency obligations** — user-facing disclosure requirements for all AI systems interacting with people; (2) **GPAI enforcement powers** — Commission gains authority to issue fines up to €15M or 3% global annual turnover for documentation, copyright, and risk assessment violations. The high-risk obligations (Annex III — employment, credit, biometrics) originally scheduled for August 2 were pushed to December 2, 2027 by the June 16, 2026 Digital Omnibus amendments.

**Confirmed scope of Article 50 from August 2:**
- **Article 50(1):** Chatbots, virtual assistants, AI agents interacting with people must disclose they are AI "at the time of first interaction" — disclosure in the interaction itself, not in terms of service. EU AI Office draft guidelines explicitly confirm "AI agents fall within Article 50(1)."
- **Article 50(2):** New AI systems generating synthetic content (text, image, audio, video) must include machine-readable marking from August 2. Systems already on market get grace period until December 2, 2026.
- **Article 50(4):** Deepfakes and AI-generated text on public-interest matters must carry visible disclosure — exception: human editorial review with named responsible editor.
- **National market surveillance authorities** gain full investigatory and enforcement powers from August 2.

**Penalties:** €15M or 3% global annual turnover, whichever is higher. Enforcement decentralized among national authorities.
**Evidence level:** L3 — three independent sources (Sidley Austin [law firm], Digital Applied [compliance practitioner], artificialintelligenceact.eu [EU civil society tracker]) all independently confirm same Article 50 scope and August 2 activation; Digital Omnibus delay of high-risk provisions confirmed by Latham & Watkins and axis-intelligence.com independently.
**Key claims:**
- Article 50 transparency + GPAI enforcement = the August 2 deliverables; Annex III high-risk = December 2027
- AI agents are explicitly named as in-scope by EU AI Office draft guidelines
- B2B / internal deployment exemption is narrow: only applies when outputs are strictly technical, accessible only to a restricted internal professional group, with safeguards against wider dissemination
- Machine-readable watermarking for NEW systems is mandatory from August 2; legacy systems get until December 2, 2026
- July 22 TODAY is the deadline to sign the EU AI Office Code of Practice on Transparency of AI-Generated Content — signing creates "strong presumption of compliance" and is a mitigating factor in fine calculation

## Orientation

**August 2 is a real enforcement date, but the most feared provisions were delayed.** The Digital Omnibus (May 7, 2026) moved Annex III high-risk obligations (HR, credit, biometrics) 16 months to December 2027. What remains live on August 2 is narrower: chatbot disclosure, synthetic content watermarking, and GPAI model penalties. For most enterprise AI agent deployments (internal workflow automation, coding agents, business process agents), the exposure is limited to Article 50(1) chatbot disclosure if those agents interact with natural persons. An internal coding agent used only by developers is likely in scope — "AI agents fall within Article 50(1)" per EU AI Office guidance.

**The compliance action is simple but the inventory is the blocker.** The Article 50(1) fix is technically trivial: add "You are speaking with an AI" to chatbot/agent interfaces. The hard part is that 40%+ of enterprises don't have a complete inventory of which AI systems are in production. You can't disclose what you haven't catalogued.

**Nordic: Finland leads, Norway is late.** Finland's enforcement (Traficom active since Jan 1, 2026) makes Finnish companies the most immediately exposed to Article 50 enforcement from August 2. Norway is still implementing its AI Act framework as of "summer 2026" — meaning Norwegian companies may be operating in uncertainty about their specific national authority.

**Today (July 22) is the Code of Practice signatory deadline.** Companies that signed commit to a compliance pathway with legal protections; those that didn't must demonstrate equivalent rigor independently.
