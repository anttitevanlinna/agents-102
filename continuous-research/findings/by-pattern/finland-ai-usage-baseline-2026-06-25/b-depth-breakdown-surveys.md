# Finland AI Usage — Layer-1 Depth Breakdown (B: industry & consultancy surveys)

**Question this file answers:** Finnish "we use AI" headlines run 38–66%. How much of that is plain conversational LLM chat (ChatGPT / Copilot) vs content generation vs integrated-in-product vs autonomous/agentic workflow?

**Date compiled:** 2026-06-25. Freshness cutoff for "current": 2025-12-25.

**Bottom line up front:** The headline denominators are inflated by chat. The single best depth signal: of Finnish AI-using firms, **91% merely *permit* GenAI experimentation, but only 28% actively pursue org-wide transformation and only 12% have fully replaced any task with GenAI** (Työterveyslaitos × Tilastokeskus, N=1,691). The EU-topping "66% use generative AI" figure is defined as using *"ChatGPT, Bard or Copilot"* — i.e. the chat tools themselves are the definition. Hypothesis (mostly chat) is **strongly supported**, with one honest caveat: no Finnish survey cleanly isolates "chat-only" as a mutually-exclusive bucket, so the split is inferred from depth/maturity gradients, not a single clean cross-tab.

## (b) THE KEY QUESTION — what share of Finnish "AI use" is conversational chat vs integrated vs agentic?

No Finnish survey publishes a single clean three-way cross-tab. But four independent depth gradients converge on the same shape:

1. **The headline definitions are chat.** The EU-topping 66% is *literally* defined as using "ChatGPT, Bard or Copilot" (EIB). Tilastokeskus's GenAI sub-categories (text 29%, image/audio 22%, language/code 20%) are all *generation* (prompt-in/content-out) — none describe embedding AI into a product or an autonomous workflow.

2. **Depth collapses as you move from "allowed" to "transformed."** Työterveyslaitos×Tilastokeskus, of AI-using firms: 91% *permit* experimentation → 49% *trained* anyone → 28% *seek* org-wide transformation → **12% have actually replaced a task** with GenAI. The funnel from "people may chat" to "the work changed" loses ~87% of the headline.

3. **Frequency is shallow.** Suomen Yrittäjät: of the 57% SME headline, only **19% use AI *regularly*; 38% *occasionally***. "Occasional" 2:1 over "regular" is the texture of a tool people open when they remember it — a chat habit, not a wired-in process.

4. **Integration is the minority even among leaders.** FAIR (leading-edge adopters only): just **25%** report AI "generated new business or integrated into products/services." If a quarter of the *front-runners* are integrated, the population share is far lower.

**Estimate (triangulated, not from one cross-tab):** Of Finnish firm AI use, the dominant mode is conversational/content-generation chat. Genuinely *integrated-in-product* use is a clear minority — on the order of FAIR's 25% among leaders, lower population-wide. **Autonomous/agentic** Finnish firm use has **no published Finnish denominator at all** (only global proxies). Best single line for the deck: *"91% of AI-using Finnish firms allow GenAI; 28% pursue real transformation; 12% have actually replaced a task."*

## (c) Confirming vs disconfirming the "it's mostly chat" hypothesis

**Confirming (strong):**
- EIB 66% defined as ChatGPT/Bard/Copilot use — chat *is* the metric. [academic/research, independent]
- ETLA citizen data: deep use *"vielä vähän"*; most-common mode is search-engine-style prompting. [academic/research]
- Työterveyslaitos depth funnel 91%→28%→12%. [official/academic]
- Suomen Yrittäjät 19% regular / 38% occasional — shallow frequency. [trade body]
- EK (dated): ~40% of the 45% AI users are on ChatGPT-type chat. [trade body, 2023]

**Disconfirming / complicating (weak-to-moderate):**
- Työterveyslaitos use-areas show AI touching customer service (53%) and processes (52%), not only marketing — *some* embedding exists, not pure toy use.
- FAIR's 25% integrated-into-products is non-trivial among leaders — a real (if minority) integrated cohort exists and is growing.
- Tilastokeskus large firms 68% / Työterveyslaitos large-service 84% — at the top end, AI is broader than a chat window.
- None of these *disconfirm* "mostly chat"; they bound it. The integrated tier is real but minority; the agentic tier is unmeasured in Finland.

**Verdict:** Hypothesis holds. Finnish AI-adoption headlines are denominated in chat; depth thins sharply with each step toward integration, and vanishes (unmeasured) at agentic.

## (d) What we did NOT find (gaps)

- **No Finnish survey with a clean mutually-exclusive chat / integrated / agentic cross-tab.** The split here is triangulated from depth gradients, not one table. State this when presenting.
- **No Finnish-specific agentic-AI adoption denominator.** Only global proxies (KPMG 42% "some agents", Capgemini 2% at scale, McKinsey 23% scaling) — all global, all consultancy, none cited as Finnish convergence.
- **Työterveyslaitos primary PDF (julkari.fi) returned 403** on direct fetch; figures sourced via the aisanomat synthesis + ResearchGate mirror. Re-verify against the primary before publishing a headline number.
- **PwC Finland CEO Survey behind 403/paywall**; 49% figure is from secondary report, ~2024, dated, consultancy self-interest — context only.
- **DigiBarometri 2025 firm-level genAI section** not opened directly (digibarometri.fi landing only); the ETLA figure used here is the *citizen* EVA study, not the firm barometer. Firm-level DigiBarometri cross-tab is an open gap for file (a) or a follow-up.
- **Teknologiateollisuus** measures AI *investment intent*, not use-type — no chat-vs-deeper split available.
