a16z article (George Sivulka): Individual AI gives 10x productivity, but institutions capture none of it. Electrification analogy — factories that electrified first lost to those who redesigned the floor. Seven pillars: coordination, signal, bias, edge, outcomes, enablement, unprompted action.

Key research tension: Sivulka frames institutional AI as something you BUY (Palantir, Cognition). The alternative thesis: institutional AI is something you BUILD — competence first, then the organization redesigns its own floor. One creates dependency, the other compounds.

Maps to: absorption bottleneck (institutions can't absorb), Intent Taktik (unprompted pillar), competence-first pattern (enablement pillar), curation value (signal pillar).

**Gap:** No systematic research on the individual-to-institutional AI value transfer problem. Who's actually redesigning the floor, not just installing better motors? What does the "redesigned factory" look like in practice? This is the electrification-to-assembly-line transition — we're tracking the motors (platforms), not the floor plans (institutional redesign).
