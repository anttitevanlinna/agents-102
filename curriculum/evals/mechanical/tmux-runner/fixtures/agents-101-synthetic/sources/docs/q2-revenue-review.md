# Q2 2026 Revenue Review — Nordveil (internal, Finance + Product Ops)

## Headline

ARR €19.4M, up 6% QoQ. **But net revenue retention slipped to 104%** from 111%
a year ago. The slip is almost entirely **seat-expansion stalling** — existing
accounts aren't buying more seats even as their usage climbs.

## The seat-sharing effect

Sampled 20 accounts. Median ratio of active dispatcher identities to paid seats
was **2.6:1**. We are billing for roughly a third of the people actually using
the product. Usage (dispatched routes) is up 40% YoY against flat seat revenue.

## Churn

Gross churn steady at 8% annual. Concentrated in **small operators** — the same
SMB segment the pricing pilot is targeting. Churn reasons cited: "too expensive
for what we use" (the per-seat complaint) and, in two recent cases, "unpredictable
costs" (a warning sign for what usage-based could do if mispriced).

## Implication for pricing

Usage-based directly addresses the seat-sharing leak and the "too expensive for
what we use" churn reason. The risk it introduces — "unpredictable costs" — is
already a churn reason today for a minority. The pilot has to show usage-based
nets out positive, not just that it lifts the mid-market.
