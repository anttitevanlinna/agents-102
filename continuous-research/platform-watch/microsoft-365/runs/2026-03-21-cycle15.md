## Findings

### E7 Pricing Skepticism — Convergent Level 1
Multiple independent practitioners and analysts are skeptical about E7 ($99/user/month) value proposition:

- **Office Watch:** "The Future or an Expensive Bundle?" — E7 is 65% price jump over E5.
  **Source:** [Office Watch](https://office-watch.com/2026/microsoft-365-e7-a-99-frontier-suite-the-future-or-an-expensive-bundle/) — [practitioner analysis]
  **Date:** March 2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 1

- **SAMexpert:** "$99 per user per month at list price is too high for broad adoption." Notes Microsoft's own Copilot discounting history failed to move adoption.
  **Source:** [SAMexpert](https://samexpert.com/microsoft-365-e7-licensing-guide/) — [practitioner analysis]
  **Date:** March 2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 1

- **Office365itpros.com:** "Is the New Agent 365 Worth $99?" — questioning value for most users.
  **Source:** [Office365itpros.com](https://office365itpros.com/2026/03/17/is-the-new-agent-365-worth-99/) — [practitioner analysis]
  **Date:** March 2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 1

- **US Cloud:** Hidden consumption costs — E7 bills could exceed $200/user/month once agentic workloads deploy due to uncapped consumption charges.
  **Source:** [US Cloud](https://www.uscloud.com/blog/microsoft-e7-and-the-new-economics-of-enterprise-ai/) — [practitioner analysis]
  **Date:** March 2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 1

- **Rob Quickenden:** "If the licence is measured per user, but applied per agent, and agents don't map to users, what exactly are organisations paying for?"
  **Source:** [Rob Quickenden blog](https://robquickenden.blog/2026/03/agent-365-nears-ga/) — [practitioner direct]
  **Date:** March 2026
  **Evidence level:** Level 1

- **Copilot adoption baseline:** Only 15M paid Copilot seats (~3.3% of 450M+ M365 commercial seats) as of Jan 2026 (Nadella, Q2 FY26 earnings). Bare fact.

**Assessment:** Convergent Level 1 from 5+ independent sources. E7 faces pricing headwinds. The 3.3% Copilot conversion rate is the factual backdrop.

## Orientation
The E7 pricing skepticism is the strongest new signal — convergent Level 1 from multiple independent sources, backed by the factual 3.3% Copilot conversion rate. This suggests Microsoft's business agent strategy faces the same distribution challenge as Copilot: great distribution, weak conversion. Everything else remains at Level 0 (vendor-only evidence). The Nordic gap is notable and persistent across all research cycles.
