**Verdict: Approaching Level 3 but NOT declaring full convergence.**

The pattern is clear among compliance platform vendors (10 independent companies, 7 passing gates, consistent 70-95% improvements). But three critical caveats prevent full Level 3:

1. **Vendor-practitioner adoption gap:** Only 18% of AML practitioners have AI in production (ACAMS survey). Convergence is among vendors building platforms, not end-user compliance teams deploying them.
2. **"Agentic" is being stretched:** Unit21's Underdog Fantasy reveals "trust-but-verify" summarization, not full autonomy. Many "agentic" claims are advanced copilots with human validation.
3. **Enterprise banking silence:** All named practitioners are vendor CEOs/CTOs or fintech compliance staff. Zero named compliance officers at major banks have published deployment results.
4. **Failure modes are real:** Sardine's 55% vs 95% gap (sanctions vs KYC) shows agents work for simple rules-based tasks but struggle with nuanced judgment.

**Report as: "Level 2+ approaching convergence" with the vendor-practitioner adoption gap as the key finding.**

The gap between "platform vendors deploying" and "compliance teams adopting" IS the story — it mirrors the same pattern we see across all domains.
