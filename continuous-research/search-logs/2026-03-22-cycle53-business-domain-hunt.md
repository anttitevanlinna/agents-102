- **Finance:** Found 1 new executive (Marco Argenti, Goldman CIO). No finance analysts/controllers.

**Why this matters for Agents 102:** Our training targets builders, not buyers. In vendor-driven domains, the "builder" is the vendor's engineering team or a systems integrator (Deloitte, Accenture). The enterprise practitioner is a buyer/configurer. This changes how Module 7 (Agent Platforms) should position the opportunity — in these domains, "building agents" means configuring vendor platforms, not writing code.

## Meta-observation

**The practitioner publication gap is structural, not temporary.** In 53 cycles we've found exactly 1 genuine non-executive HR practitioner (Rocher) publishing about building agents. The reason is clear:

1. **HR/compliance/ops practitioners don't have a "publish on GitHub" culture.** Developers share code. Finance people share models. HR people share... nothing publicly. Their work product is internal policy, not public code.

2. **The platforms they use don't generate shareable artifacts.** When a developer builds with Claude Code, they have a repo to share. When an HR person configures Workday's AI agent, they have... a configuration screen. Nothing to blog about.

3. **The risk/reward for publishing is inverted.** Developers gain reputation by publishing. HR/compliance people risk exposing proprietary processes. Legal review would kill most posts.

**Implication for our research:** We may need to shift from "find practitioners who publish" to "find practitioners who speak at conferences" — the conference circuit is where business-domain practitioners share deployment stories under controlled conditions. SHRM AI+HI Conference, NextGen Supply Chain, IBF AI Edge are the venues to monitor.
