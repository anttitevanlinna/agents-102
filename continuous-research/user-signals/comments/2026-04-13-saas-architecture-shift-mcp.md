SaaS landscape visibly shifting architecture over last 2 months. Jira/Atlassian was ahead of the curve shipping MCP — they built the connector FROM themselves rather than waiting for platforms to build connectors TO them.

Vendor incentive inversion: in the old world, lock-in = sticky UI + hard-to-extract data. In the agentic world, the SaaS hardest to reach by agents gets automated around. Smart SaaS vendors are racing to ship MCP servers as defensive openness — "be headless on our terms before someone makes us headless on theirs."

Three tiers forming:
1. Moved first — Atlassian, Stripe, API-first-from-birth tools
2. Moving now — saw MCP wave, shipping connectors
3. Will be dragged — legacy enterprise SaaS, UI-first, APIs as afterthought (Computer Use bridge)

**Gap:** No systematic tracking of which enterprise SaaS vendors have shipped MCP servers and when. This is a leading indicator of the headless shift and determines which enterprise systems coding agents can reach natively vs. which require Computer Use as a bridge. Two-month window is enough to see the pattern forming but not yet mapped.
