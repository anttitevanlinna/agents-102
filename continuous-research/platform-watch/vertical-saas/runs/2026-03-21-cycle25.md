## Orientation

**What changed since cycle 24:**

1. **Agentforce counter-narrative is now Level 2-3.** Multiple independent practitioners (Salesforce Ben, CIO.com, Greyhound Research, Monetizely) converge on same critique: autonomy promise walked back, pricing unstable, production variance problematic. This is no longer "a few skeptics" — it's convergent practitioner signal. The headline number ($800M ARR, 12K customers) remains strong, but the practitioner experience is more mixed than revenue suggests.

2. **Reddit is a strong new named deployment** (46% deflection, 84% faster). Adds to the customer service convergence pattern. But it's still customer service — no evidence of Agentforce working in sales, marketing, or other domains at similar levels.

3. **ServiceNow revenue signal strengthens but deployment evidence stays weak.** $600M+ ACV is impressive. But we still can't name a single customer with specific metrics beyond ServiceNow's own internal claims. The Deloitte partnership emphasizing "human-in-the-loop" is a maturity signal — ServiceNow may be quietly managing expectations better than Salesforce.

4. **Zendesk-Forethought is the most technically interesting development.** Self-improving agents (generate own workflows), computer use for legacy systems, voice automation. IF these capabilities work as described, Zendesk could leapfrog in customer service. But: deal not yet closed, integration timeline unknown, no combined product shipped.

5. **SAP Joule remains the weakest evidence base.** Agent Builder is GA but no independently verified customer results. Bosch quote adds a name but no numbers. The gap between SAP's "400 AI use cases" marketing and production evidence is the widest of any platform.

6. **The compounding error insight is critical for training.** 85% per-step accuracy = 20% on 10-step workflows. This is the mathematical argument for why current agents work in customer service (bounded, 2-3 steps) but fail in complex business processes (10+ steps). This belongs in Module 5 (Output Quality) and Module 7 (Agent Platforms).

7. **Nordic vertical SaaS adoption gap persists.** Nordea's choice to build internally on AWS Bedrock rather than adopt vertical SaaS agents is potentially the most interesting Nordic signal. The largest Nordic bank looked at the vertical SaaS options and chose to build. Worth investigating why.
