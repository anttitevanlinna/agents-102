The L4 meta-pattern (rules codified, correctness verifiable, talent scarce) is binary — fits or doesn't. The deeper insight: agent effectiveness follows a GRADIENT of work structure, not a binary threshold.

Coding (most structured) → Finance → CS → Legal → PM execution → Marketing execution → PM strategy → Marketing strategy → HR (least structured).

This explains three patterns simultaneously:
1. Why the execution-strategy gap appears in EVERY domain — execution is always more structured than strategy
2. Why marketing content hits quality ceilings PM doesn't — persuasion is less verifiable than spec correctness
3. Why HR is a black hole — almost nothing is structured or verifiable

The gradient also explains why coding agents are the meta-platform: they operate at the most structured end and reach into less-structured domains by bringing structure WITH them (MCP servers, evals, spec frameworks). They don't make marketing strategy logical — they make the structured parts of marketing work executable.

**Gap:** The L4 meta-pattern should be extended from binary (fits/doesn't) to gradient (degree of structure predicts degree of agent effectiveness). This would be a Level 4+ insight if supported by cross-domain evidence — which the PM and marketing OODA cycles are now providing.
