**[Finding 5.3] arXiv 2607.01904 — review capacity is the binding constraint (L3 academic)**

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01904 — [academic/research]
Authors: Hao He, Shyam Agarwal, Yegor Denisov-Blanch, Pavel Azaletskiy, Sanmi Koyejo, Bogdan Vasilescu
Date: Submitted July 2026, based on data through April 2026
What: Longitudinal study of 802 developers and 196,212 PRs at one mid-sized AI-forward enterprise running an explicit "2x merged PRs per engineer" mandate from mid-2025 to April 2026. Result: 2.09x throughput confirmed (mandate achieved). BUT: per-reviewer load roughly doubled, and automated review overtook human review. Gains are uneven — concentrated in newer repositories, "barely present in legacy code." AI-generated code now ships under automated review without a measurable penalty on coarse quality proxies (merge rate, revert rate).
Evidence level: L3 (academic/research, independent, 800+ practitioners, 18-month longitudinal)
Key claims:
- 2x throughput IS achievable — Pattern 48 data point confirmed with specifics
- The binding constraint is reviewer capacity, NOT generation capacity — as AI writes more code, human review supply runs out first
- Legacy code = near-zero gains (confirms practitioner convergence from other sources)
- Automated review replacing human review at AI-code scale = quality risk that coarse metrics (merge rate, revert rate) cannot detect
- CTO implication: a 2x mandate without a review capacity plan produces code that ships under automated review without human judgment — the mandate creates a quality accountability gap
- NOTE: Company unnamed, single case — Level 2 if solo; approaches Level 3 combined with practitioner convergence (Uber/Microsoft/Walmart from Pattern 48)
