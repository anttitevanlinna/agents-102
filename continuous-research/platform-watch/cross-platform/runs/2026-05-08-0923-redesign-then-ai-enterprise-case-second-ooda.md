**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Agent level:** company, anonymized
**What:** Exact phrases from the BCG article only returned the BCG article and reposted/irrelevant material. No named European energy provider or named global bank surfaced from the distinctive phrases: run-rate savings within three months, >90% external provider reduction, AI-enabled journeys matching human CSAT, 30-50% workflows, 3M human hours, 1,700 FTEs, or role names like AI product owners/model governance managers/talent data strategists.
**Evidence level:** Absence from this scan. It suggests anonymization is not trivially reversible through public search.
**Key claims:** BCG remains right-shape, wrong-source evidence.

### Schneider Electric is the closest named "structure first" industrial case, but it is centralize-capability-first, not redesign-then-agent

**Source:** https://www.rbc.com/en/thought-leadership/the-growth-project/trust-scale-and-strategy-how-to-build-an-ai-first-organization/ — [practitioner analysis / interview with practitioner]
**Date:** 2025-12
**Agent level:** company / capability function
**What:** Schneider Electric is named, industrial, large-scale, and structurally specific. Cedric Bureau describes a global AI Hub launched in 2021, grown to about 350 people in 12 months, headed by a Chief AI Officer reporting to the executive committee. The hub includes data scientists, MLOps engineers, product managers, and compliance; AI product managers align to business units; a four-gate funnel forces ROI/risk checks, MVP deployment, production hardening, and live ROI/drift/incident tracking.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 named-company operating-model evidence. It is not the requested case because it predates the current agentic wave, centers ML/AI capability industrialization, and does not show reporting-line/capacity redesign around human-agent collaboration.
**Key claims:** Named practitioner; AI Hub; executive committee reporting; embedded compliance; four-gate funnel; business-unit AI product managers; operating-model detail.

### Schneider clarifies a possible alternative pattern: central capability redesign may precede agentic absorption

**Source:** https://www.rbc.com/en/thought-leadership/the-growth-project/trust-scale-and-strategy-how-to-build-an-ai-first-organization/ — [practitioner analysis / interview with practitioner]
**Date:** 2025-12
**Agent level:** company
**What:** Schneider does not answer "redesign roles/reporting/capacity first, then AI agents." But it does show a plausible predecessor pattern: create a central AI capability with clear executive ownership, product managers aligned to business units, standardized tooling/governance, and deployment gates. This is more concrete than generic "AI CoE" talk and could be the old-world counterpart to agentic redesign.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 synthesis from a named industrial case.
**Key claims:** Capability centralization and governance as a path to scale; no agentic throughput evidence.
