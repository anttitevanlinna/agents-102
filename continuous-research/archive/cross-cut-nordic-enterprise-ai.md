## Pattern Analysis

### 1. Domain Entry Sequence -- Where Nordic Companies Start

**Operations is the universal gateway.** 11 of 17 companies (65%) have active AI deployments in operations. This is where quantifiable ROI is clearest: predictive maintenance, route optimization, process automation. Every industrial company (Equinor, Maersk, IKEA, Scania, Volvo, Wartsila, LKAB, Neste, Kone) entered through ops first.

**Product/R&D is the second wave.** 14 of 17 companies (82% including pilots) are investing in AI for product development -- but much of this is building AI into the product itself (Ericsson embedding AI in 5G, Volvo in vehicles, Kone in elevator services) rather than using AI agents to improve how product development works internally.

**Customer Service is the third wave.** 8 of 17 (47%) -- and this is where the banks and telecoms enter. Nordea, Klarna, Danske Bank, DNB, and Telia all started with customer-facing chatbots/virtual agents. This domain has the most mature "agentic" deployments in the traditional sense.

### 2. The Empty Domains -- Where Nobody Has Gone

**Sales/Marketing is nearly empty.** Only Klarna and H&M have active deployments; Telia is piloting. The vast majority of Nordic enterprises have no public AI agent activity in sales or marketing. H&M's digital twin models and Klarna's Agentic Product Protocol are exceptions, not the norm.

**Compliance is banking-only.** Only the three banks (Nordea, Danske Bank, DNB) and Novo Nordisk show compliance AI -- and in pharma it is regulatory document automation, not true compliance agents. No industrial company shows AI in compliance.

**HR is sparse.** Only IKEA (training), Klarna (workforce restructuring), Nordea (internal bots), Danske Bank (HR Assistant), and DNB (internal support) show HR AI. No company shows AI agents making HR decisions -- it is all information retrieval and training delivery.

**Finance is the quietest business function.** Only banks (naturally) and Kone (contract processing) show finance-domain AI. No company profiles reveal AI in budgeting, forecasting, FP&A, or treasury -- despite the obvious applicability.

### 3. Maturity Tiers

**Tier 1 -- Scaled AI with quantified impact (5 companies):**
- Equinor ($330M cumulative, $130M in 2025)
- Maersk ($300M+ annual, 700+ vessels)
- Klarna (850-agent equivalent, 2/3 of inquiries)
- H&M (270-person AI unit, 30% profit uplift)
- Kone (54K contracts automated, 70% more fault detection)

**Tier 2 -- Broad deployment, less quantified (6 companies):**
- Novo Nordisk (20K Copilot users, R&D pipeline)
- IKEA/Ingka (Locus acquisition, 70K training target)
- Nordea (12 agents, chat-first strategy)
- Danske Bank (12 GenAI solutions, 16K monthly users)

**Tier 3 -- Early/focused deployment (6 companies):**

### 4. Key Patterns for Training Relevance

**The "Ops First" pattern is universal.** Every company that shows maturity started with operations -- predictive maintenance, logistics optimization, or process automation. This is the gateway drug for enterprise AI agents.

**Banks lead in multi-domain deployment.** Nordea, Danske Bank, and DNB are the only companies with AI across 4+ business domains. Financial services regulatory pressure and digital competition create urgency across all functions simultaneously.

**Klarna is the outlier.** The only company deploying AI agents across 5+ domains with aggressive workforce implications. It is a fintech behaving like a tech company, not a bank.

**The "AI in the product" vs. "AI in the process" distinction matters.** Many companies (Ericsson, Volvo, Scania, Kone) are embedding AI into their products. Far fewer are using AI agents to transform their internal business processes beyond operations. This is the training opportunity.

**Nordic companies are infrastructure-heavy, agent-light.** Most deployments are traditional ML/AI (predictive maintenance, demand forecasting, fraud detection) rather than true agentic AI (autonomous agents making decisions, executing workflows). The agentic wave is arriving but has not yet scaled in most companies beyond Klarna and Ericsson's explicit agentic deployments.

### 5. Implications for Agents 102

1. **Operations is table stakes, not differentiating.** Everyone is already doing it. The training should acknowledge ops as the entry point but push toward the domains that are empty.

2. **The real blue ocean is Sales, HR, Finance, and Compliance agents.** These are the domains where almost no Nordic enterprise has deployed AI agents. Leaders who can build competence here will have first-mover advantage.

3. **The "agent vs. automation" confusion persists.** Many companies call their ML models and RPA "AI" -- the training must sharpen the distinction between traditional AI/ML, copilots, and true agentic systems.

4. **Banking is the most mature buyer for multi-domain training.** Banks already have AI across 4+ domains and understand the cross-functional challenge. They are natural early adopters for comprehensive agent competence programs.

5. **Industrial companies are one domain deep.** Equinor, Maersk, Wartsila, LKAB, Scania, Volvo all have deep ops AI but virtually nothing in HR, Finance, Sales, or Compliance. The expansion from ops to the rest of the organization is exactly the transformation challenge Agents 102 addresses.
