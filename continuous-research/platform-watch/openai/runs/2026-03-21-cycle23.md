## Orientation

**The picture has sharpened since cycle 20 but not fundamentally changed.** Three developments matter:

1. **The PE joint venture is the most significant new signal.** OpenAI is essentially admitting that Frontier cannot sell itself — it needs $10M-minimum FDE engagements and PE distribution channels to reach enterprises. This is the opposite of a self-serve platform. It's Palantir's model applied to AI agents. For our buyers (Nordic enterprises), this means Frontier is irrelevant unless they're in a PE portfolio or willing to spend $10M+ on OpenAI implementation.

2. **The desktop super-app pivot confirms strategic fragmentation.** OpenAI is simultaneously: (a) merging consumer products into a super-app, (b) building an enterprise agent platform (Frontier), (c) creating a PE consulting subsidiary, (d) preparing for Q4 IPO, (e) fighting Microsoft over AWS cloud deal. Five major strategic initiatives at once. For enterprise buyers, this means OpenAI's enterprise focus is one of five priorities, not THE priority.

3. **The enterprise spending data keeps getting worse.** Axios (March 18) and Ramp data now show the trend accelerating, not stabilizing. EpochAI projecting revenue crossover in August 2026. OpenAI's enterprise decline is structural, not cyclical — rooted in product architecture (single-player, sandboxed agents, inconsistent EEA access) while Anthropic ships enterprise-native features (Claude Code, Cowork, MCP).

**For Agents 102 training:** OpenAI remains the world's most-used AI product (900M users) but its business agent story is: impressive demos, zero deployment evidence, structural gaps, and an enterprise market share in freefall. The gap between OpenAI's vision (agent operating system) and reality (80% plain chat) is the widest of any vendor we track.
