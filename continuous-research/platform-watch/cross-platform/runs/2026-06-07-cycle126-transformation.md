## Orientation

The absence is structural, not a search gap. The Virgin Voyages case (1,500 agents, multi-function, record outcomes) is the closest candidate found in 126 cycles — but it is agent deployment within existing roles, not role architecture redesign. The P&G Wharton study signals that a redesign is _incoming_ at a major traditional enterprise, but no published execution has followed. The structural explanation (Cutler + Mollick: authority depends on complexity, KPIs punish redesign, secret cyborgs hide progress) continues to account for the absence: the organizations most capable of redesign are led by people whose authority depends on the complexity being redesigned away.

**Recommendation:** (1) Watch P&G 2026 annual report / CMO announcements for marketing org structural follow-through. (2) Virgin Voyages merits Level 2+ classification as multi-function agentic deployment — not floor redesign but the closest published case. (3) Barry O'Reilly book full-text access would unlock HSBC/American Airlines detail.
