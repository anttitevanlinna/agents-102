# OODA Cycle: Strategy Clarity Before Org Structure

**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Hypothesis:** Companies reach for structural solutions (reorg, OKR frameworks, org design) when the real problem is strategic ambiguity at the top.
**Verdict:** DOCUMENTED PATTERN — Level 3 convergence across 10+ independent practitioners. Not our original synthesis. We're naming a convergence that others have identified from different angles. Our contribution: connecting it to Intent Taktik and the AI-era execution-is-cheap thesis.

---

## Finding 1: Roger Martin — "Stop Letting OKRs Masquerade as Strategy"

**Person:** Roger Martin (strategy professor, Rotman School, author of Playing to Win)
**What he said:** Going directly from 'O' to 'KRs' results in OKRs masquerading as strategy. Desire (as with hope) is simply not a strategy. There's a gap that needs an explicit theory of Where-to-Play / How-to-Win choices. OKRs must be a complement to strategy, not a substitute. Without that strategic filling, "O" and "KR" are bookends around nothing.
**Date:** Original piece 2021; reaffirmed in "A Fifth Year of Strategy" (Feb 2026) and "Fixing Strategy" (Feb 2026). Listed as one of his key "screeds" in the 5-year retrospective.
**Source:** https://rogermartin.medium.com/stop-letting-okrs-masquerade-as-strategy-a57fc2cea915 [practitioner direct]
**Also:** https://rogermartin.medium.com/fixing-strategy-560e4c09ee0d [practitioner direct] — Feb 2026, argues strategy is "profoundly broken" and needs fixing through five elements: performed by general managers, driven by imagination, guided by principles, iterative and distributed.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (opinion of a named authority) — but Martin has been saying this consistently for 5+ years, and it's referenced by many practitioners below.

**Key for our thesis:** Martin names the exact gap. OKRs cascade choices that haven't been made. If the leadership team hasn't decided Where to Play and How to Win, cascading OKRs through the org is distributing ambiguity, not strategy. The OKR becomes theater — everyone's busy, nobody knows why.

## Finding 2: Mike Fisher — "Build the Right Thing" (Apr 15 2026)

**Person:** Mike Fisher (CEO MyFitnessPal, former CTO Etsy, VP Eng PayPal, co-founder AKF Partners)
**What he said:** Organizations lose their way by confusing outputs (features shipped) with outcomes (customer behavior changed). The Langley vs. Wright Brothers comparison: Langley had government funding, brilliant team, visible progress — "The outputs were impressive; the outcomes were nonexistent." Wright Brothers solved the actual problem (controlled flight) with no resources. Financial discipline applied at the wrong moment increases risk, not reduces it. When ROI dominates discovery, organizations drift into "permanent short-termism" — teams stop asking "what could be possible" and start asking "what can be defended."
**Date:** April 15, 2026
**Source:** https://mikefisher.substack.com/p/build-the-right-thing [practitioner direct]
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion, but from a serious operator)

**Related Fisher pieces (pattern across his writing):**
- "Culture Debt" (Jan 2026) — culture problems stem from leadership neglect, not structural design. Structural solutions don't fix culture. https://mikefisher.substack.com/p/culture-debt [practitioner direct]
- "Exploit vs Explore" (Mar 2026) — "a single team asked to both exploit and explore will almost always choose exploitation, especially under delivery pressure." Solution is team topology (separate modes organizationally), not willpower. https://mikefisher.substack.com/p/exploit-vs-explore [practitioner direct]
- "Local Minima" (date TBC) — departments optimize their own metrics while the company slips into a worse equilibrium. "In your company, who is looking out for the global maximum?" https://mikefisher.substack.com/p/local-minima [practitioner direct]
- "Executive Amplification" (Feb 2026) — leaders are unintentional signal-broadcasters. Ambiguous communication creates unplanned work as teams reallocate resources to satisfy perceived expectations rather than actual strategic priorities. https://mikefisher.substack.com/p/executive-amplification [practitioner direct]

**Key for our thesis:** Fisher doesn't name the "strategy before structure" pattern explicitly. But his writing circles it continuously: premature financial validation, outputs-over-outcomes, local minima, amplification of ambiguity. He's a builder-operator describing the same dysfunction from inside the machine.

## Finding 3: Rachel Bertler — "The Hidden Failure in OKR Planning" (Jan 2026)

**Person:** Rachel Bertler (OKR practitioner/coach)
**What she said:** The hidden failure is "rushed OKR development" — company objectives move to teams before shared clarity on why the priority matters. "Activity Over Impact" pattern: progress evaluated by delivery and effort, while downstream friction, rework, and repeated clarifications quietly accumulate. Alignment is assumed rather than deliberately established. Goal of problem discovery: create shared clarity about barriers preventing the organization from achieving outcomes.
**Date:** January 2026
**Source:** https://medium.com/@rachelbertler/the-hidden-failure-in-okr-planning-a94e3b0aa714 [practitioner direct]
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion)

**Key for our thesis:** Bertler names the exact mechanism: OKRs cascade before clarity exists. The result is "busy without progress" — everyone has objectives, nobody has strategy.

## Finding 5: Jessica Kerr — "Cascading OKRs: We Can Do Better" (Jan 2025)

**Person:** Jessica Kerr (Honeycomb, "Jessitron")
**What she said:** Alignment that looks nice when team OKRs roll up to department OKRs is "logical" and "terrible." The company is a complex system where actions don't simply add to outcomes. Real alignment comes from telling a story about how a team's objective feeds the company's ability to realize its strategy — not from mechanical cascade.
**Date:** January 2025
**Source:** https://jessitron.com/2025/01/12/cascading-okrs-we-can-do-better/ [practitioner direct]
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion)

**Key for our thesis:** Complex systems don't cascade. OKR cascading imposes a linear model on non-linear organizational dynamics. The alternative requires strategy clarity: you can't "tell a story about how your work feeds strategy" if strategy isn't articulated.

## Finding 8: Marty Cagan — Team Topology Requires Strategy Clarity

**Person:** Marty Cagan (SVPG, author of Empowered/Transformed)
**What he said:** The majority of strategic context for product teams comes from product leadership: product vision, principles, team topology, product strategy, team objectives. If teams don't understand the product vision, they won't know what to do. If they don't understand the product strategy or what other teams do, they can't make effective decisions. "Team topology is one of the most difficult but important responsibilities for product leaders, especially at scale."
**Date:** 2025-2026 (multiple pieces including Aug 2025 Product Therapy podcast)
**Source:** https://www.svpg.com/team-objectives-action/ [practitioner direct]
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion from recognized authority)

**Key for our thesis:** Cagan makes the sequence explicit: vision → strategy → topology → objectives. Skip to topology or objectives without vision/strategy and teams are empowered to go nowhere.

## Finding 9: Reorg-as-Avoidance — Documented Organizational Pattern

**Person:** Innovative Human Capital (organizational consultancy), drawing on McKinsey research
**What they said:** "Reorganization offers leaders an appealing illusion: that complex organizational problems can be solved through structural rearrangement, that decisive action substitutes for sustained capability building, and that agility means constant change rather than disciplined adaptation." Only 23% of restructurings fully achieve stated objectives. Over half result in productivity declines. "Most organizational problems stem from capability gaps, cultural dysfunction, or strategic confusion rather than structural misalignment." Contemporary management culture creates implicit pressure to "do something" visible — restructuring satisfies this imperative while avoiding harder conversations about strategy, capability, or culture.
**Date:** October 2025
**Source:** https://www.innovativehumancapital.com/article/when-reorganization-becomes-the-problem-breaking-the-cycle-of-structural-instability [domain trade publication]
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (cited McKinsey data + pattern description, but single source for the synthesis)

**Counter-evidence:** Gartner predicts 20% of organizations will successfully use AI to flatten structures through 2026. Some companies report efficiency gains from AI-driven restructuring (Cynergy Bank: complaints down 50%, productivity up 8%). The reorg isn't always avoidance — sometimes structure IS the problem. But the distinction matters: restructuring AFTER strategic clarity is an implementation choice. Restructuring INSTEAD of strategic clarity is avoidance.

**Date:** 2025-2026 (podcast + blog + book content)

## Finding 11: Intent Engineering — Naming the Upstream Bottleneck

**Multiple independent sources converging on the same concept in early 2026:**

### 11a: Pawel Huryn — "Intent Engineering Framework for AI Agents" (Jan 2026)
"Intent is not a task list, a prompt, or a goal metric. Intent is what determines how an agent acts when instructions run out. Agents fail not because they can't reason, but because their objectives, outcomes, and constraints are underspecified."
**Source:** https://www.productcompass.pm/p/intent-engineering-framework-for-ai-agents [practitioner direct]

### 11b: Tericsoft — "Intent Engineering: The Shift Beyond Context Engineering" (Feb 2026)
Prompt Engineering controls wording. Context Engineering controls available information. Intent Engineering controls what must be achieved. "Intent Engineering is the design of AI systems around goals, constraints, and measurable outcomes rather than prompts alone."
**Source:** https://www.tericsoft.com/blogs/intent-engineering [tech press — vendor blog for Tericsoft, treat as Level 0 for their product but Level 1 for the concept naming]

### 11c: Faisal Feroz — "Prompt Engineering Is Dead. Intent Engineering Is the Real Game." (Feb 2026)
"Context engineering tells agents what to know. Intent engineering tells agents what to want — encoding organizational purpose, goals, values, and decision boundaries into infrastructure."
**Source:** https://fferoz.medium.com/prompt-engineering-is-dead-intent-engineering-is-the-real-game-89ed83cdf3a4 [practitioner analysis]

### 11d: KodeNerds — Intent-Driven Development (2026)
"In an AI-accelerated environment, the quality of your specification determines the quality of your outcome — not your velocity, not your team size, but your clarity of intent." Traditional teams: 20% product thinking, 60% engineering execution, 20% design. AI-era teams: 60% product judgment, 30% engineering architecture, 10% design. Completed a complex healthcare platform with 30% of a traditional team.
**Source:** https://www.kodenerds.com/intent-driven-development-2026 [practitioner direct]

### 11e: intentengineering.net — consulting practice
A consulting practice has formed around the concept.
**Source:** https://intentengineering.net/ [vendor — Level 0 for claims, but signals that the concept has commercial traction]

**Evidence level for the bundle:** Level 2 approaching Level 3. Five independent sources naming the same concept within 2 months. The naming is convergent but the evidence base for each claim is thin (mostly opinion, not deployment data). The concept of "intent as bottleneck" is converging; the evidence that fixing intent produces better outcomes is still Level 1-2.

**Key for our thesis:** "Intent engineering" is being named independently across AI practitioners. This is the AI-era version of "strategy clarity before structure." When execution is cheap (AI agents), intent quality becomes the binding constraint. Our "Intent Taktik" concept (insights.md, Apr 2026) anticipated this naming by applying it to organizational leadership, not just agent systems. The practitioners above focus on agent intent. ## Original Synthesis: What We Add

The practitioners above describe two half-patterns:

**Half-pattern A (product/org people):** Strategy clarity is a prerequisite for OKRs, team topology, and org design. Without it, structure distributes confusion. Martin, Cagan, Fisher, Kerr, Kalsey, Cutler, Murphy all name this.

**Half-pattern B (AI practitioners):** When execution becomes cheap (AI), intent quality becomes the binding constraint. Huryn, Feroz, KodeNerds, O'Reilly name this.

**Nobody connects them.** That's our contribution:

> When AI makes execution cheap, the penalty for strategic ambiguity multiplies. Previously, slow execution masked unclear strategy — teams were too busy building to notice they didn't know why. Now, AI delivers results in hours. If the intent was wrong, you find out in hours too. The feedback loop compresses. Organizations that substitute structure for strategy will discover the failure faster than ever — and reorganize faster than ever, in a tightening spiral of avoidance.

This connects to **Intent Taktik** (insights.md, Apr 2026): the two-layer intent model (portfolio + product) explicitly solves the upstream bottleneck. It's not "fix your OKRs" — it's "make two decisions (where to play, what to become) and give them to agents as Auftrags."

It also connects to **The Spec Is the Moat** (insights.md, Mar 2026): Karpathy's `program.md`, Shipper's `CLAUDE.md`, our `cycle-prompt.md` — all are structured intent documents. The pattern at the agent level mirrors the pattern at the organization level: write the spec clearly and the execution follows. Write the spec badly and fast execution amplifies the error.

## Counter-Evidence

1. **Structure CAN be the problem.** Not every reorg is avoidance. When strategy IS clear and structure misaligns with it, restructuring is the correct response. The thesis is about sequence (strategy first), not about dismissing structural change.

2. **Some companies succeed with AI restructuring.** Gartner predicts 20% of organizations will flatten structures with AI by 2026. Specific cases: Cynergy Bank (complaints down 50%), British Columbia Investment Management Corp (productivity up 10-20%). These suggest structure changes CAN work — but note they had clear, bounded objectives first.

3. **OKRs work when strategy exists.** Even Martin says OKRs are fine as "bookends" when the strategic middle is filled. The pattern isn't "OKRs are bad" — it's "OKRs without strategy are bad."

4. **The 23% reorg success rate (McKinsey) is unverified.** Widely cited, original methodology unclear. Treat as [UNVERIFIED STAT] until primary source found.

## Implications for Agents 102

### For Advisory
This pattern is directly relevant to the "which platform?" question. A CTO who asks "which AI platform should we use?" before asking "what strategic problems are we solving with AI?" is doing the equivalent of cascading OKRs without strategy. Our advisory sequence should be: **Strategic intent → process discovery → platform selection** — not the reverse.

### For Training (Agents 101)
Module 1 should address this: before building agents, clarify what business problem you're solving and why. The tendency to jump to tools is the organizational version of OKR-before-strategy.

### For Newsletter
"Strategy Before Structure" or "Intent Before Architecture" is an article. The Langley/Wright Brothers analogy from Fisher is a powerful opening. Connect it to the AI-era thesis: execution is now cheap, so the penalty for strategic ambiguity is paid faster.

### For Intent Taktik
This research validates the upstream bottleneck hypothesis with practitioner evidence. The "intent engineering" naming by 5+ independent sources confirms the concept is emerging. Our contribution is extending it from agent intent to organizational intent.
