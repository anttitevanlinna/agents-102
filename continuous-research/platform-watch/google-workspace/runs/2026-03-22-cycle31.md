### Finding 2: Google's own CISO warns about shadow agents — governance gap deepens
**Source:** [Google Cloud CISO blog](https://cloud.google.com/transform/these-4-ai-governance-tips-help-counter-shadow-agents) — [vendor blog, CISO-authored]; [Anton Chuvakin (Google Cloud Community)](https://security.googlecloudcommunity.com/ciso-blog-77/shadow-agents-a-new-era-of-shadow-ai-risk-in-the-enterprise-5831) — [practitioner direct]; [UC Today](https://www.uctoday.com/productivity-automation/is-google-workspace-studios-rollout-already-ahead-of-your-governance-policy/) — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** March 20, 2026 (CISO blog); March 2026 (UC Today)
**Agent level:** Team / Company
**What:** Google's own CISO team published warnings about "shadow agents" — autonomous systems deployed without IT approval. Chuvakin (known security practitioner): "Shadow agents go beyond shadow AI — they introduce autonomous action, goal hijacking, and RCE risks." UC Today: "A quiet rollout since December means many IT teams are catching up, not getting ahead." Google lacks Microsoft-equivalent admin controls (Purview DLP, Agent Dashboard). Workspace Studio is ON by default for all users. This is Google simultaneously shipping employee-accessible agent building AND warning about the risks of uncontrolled agent building.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (CISO practitioner + trade pub analysis)
**Key claims:** Shadow agents risk acknowledged by Google's own CISO; Workspace Studio rollout ahead of enterprise governance readiness; Google lacks Microsoft-equivalent DLP/agent controls.

### Finding 3: ADK production challenges documented — multi-user, documentation gaps
**Source:** [DLabs.ai](https://dlabs.ai/blog/google-adk-production-challenges-and-how-to-solve-them/) — [practitioner analysis]

### Finding 4: Gemini 3 Thought Signatures — novel approach to long-horizon agent reliability
**Source:** [Google blog](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-3/) — [vendor press release]; [JetBrains benchmark](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/gemini-3-1-pro-on-gemini-cli-gemini-enterprise-and-vertex-ai) — [practitioner analysis, Level 2]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Platform capability
**What:** Gemini 3 introduced Thought Signatures — encrypted reasoning state maintained across multi-turn interactions, preventing context drift during long-horizon agentic tasks. Configurable thinking_level (high/low) for cost vs. reasoning depth. JetBrains reported "up to 15% improvement over best Gemini 3 Pro Preview runs" for Gemini 3.1 Pro. Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite at $0.25/1M input tokens positions Google as cost leader.
**Evidence level:** Level 0 (vendor) + Level 2 (JetBrains validation is semi-independent)
**Key claims:** Thought Signatures maintain reasoning state across multi-turn; JetBrains 15% improvement claim; Flash-Lite at $0.25/1M is cost leader.

## Orientation

**The pre-credit-cliff picture is bleak for Google's business agent story.** Three problems compound:

1. **Capacity issues unresolved** 3+ months after first reports — the platform can't reliably serve the agents already built
2. **Credit cliff in 9 days** (March 31) — agents may be throttled or stop running when promotional limits expire
3. **Practitioner silence** — 20M+ tasks claimed but zero independent deployment reports. The people who matter (builders, business users sharing outcomes) are not talking.

**The governance finding is the most actionable.** Google's own CISO publishing shadow agent warnings while simultaneously rolling out employee-accessible agent building to ALL Workspace users is a contradiction worth highlighting. This directly feeds Module 4 (Security) of our training.

**Nordic pattern strengthens:** When Nordic enterprises deploy agents, they choose Microsoft (KONE: 32K+ apps) or sovereign infrastructure (Telenor: Red Hat). Google's Nordic presence = education (strong) + hackathons (channel partner activity). Enterprise agents = zero for 7th cycle.

**The CTO answer hasn't changed:** Don't bet on Google for business agents yet. Wait for post-credit-cliff reality (April) and Cloud Next '26 (April 22-24).
