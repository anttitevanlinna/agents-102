### What We Did Not Find

**Critical gaps — stated honestly:**

1. **Non-coding agent practitioners in large traditional Nordic enterprises are nearly invisible.** We found startup founders and a few enterprise leaders (Klarna, Basware, Sitra, Helsingin Sanomat), but NO named individuals at companies like Neste, Stora Enso, Volvo, Maersk, Equinor, or Nordea who are publicly deploying agents for non-coding business functions. These companies may be doing it internally, but they are not talking about it publicly. This is a significant finding for our positioning.

2. **Finland has stronger practitioner visibility than other Nordics for agent-specific work.** Helsinki Agentics Meetup is the most agent-focused community we found in any Nordic city. Sweden has more companies (Sana, Lovable, Legora, Klarna) but less visible practitioner community for agents specifically.

3. **Norway is underrepresented.** Beyond Vic.ai and Riff, we found no active agent-specific community or meetup in Oslo. The "AI Report Norway 2025" from NHH maps 350+ AI companies but does not break out agent-specific work.

4. **Denmark's agent ecosystem is small but real.** Spektr and Fortiv are genuine agent startups in the compliance space. But we found no agent-specific meetup or community in Copenhagen.

5. **Iceland: nothing found.** Zero agent practitioners or communities identified.

6. **HR agents in the Nordics: not found.** Despite HR being listed as a prime agent domain globally, we found zero Nordic practitioners publicly building or deploying HR agents. This may be a privacy/GDPR sensitivity issue, or it may simply be early.

7. **Consultancy practitioners (Reaktor, Futurice, Solita, Gofore) are invisible at the individual level.** These companies talk about AI and agents on their websites, but no specific individuals building agent solutions surfaced in our research. Solita's FunctionAI and Gofore's data/AI team exist, but we could not name a single person leading agent work at any of them.

8. **Research lab agent work (VTT, SINTEF, RISE) did not surface agent-specific researchers.** These labs work on AI broadly, but we could not find named individuals specifically building agent systems.

**Confirmation bias check:** We set out to find practitioners "actually building." The landscape is heavier on startup founders (who have fundraising incentives to be public) than on enterprise practitioners (who typically stay quiet). The startup signal is strong. The enterprise signal is weak. This does not mean enterprises are not building — it means they are not talking about it. For our buyer persona (large Nordic companies), this gap in public evidence is itself a finding: **the people who most need agent competence are the least visible in the ecosystem.**

## Spillover Signal: Coding Agents to Other Functions

### The Thesis

Coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Lovable, Riff) demonstrated that AI can autonomously plan, execute, and iterate on complex multi-step tasks. The question: when does this pattern spread to sales, legal, finance, HR, marketing, compliance?

### Evidence Found

#### 1. Anthropic's 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report (Level 3 — convergence signal)
Anthropic explicitly identifies "scaling agentic coding beyond engineering teams" as a key 2026 trend. The report documents:
- Marketing and Legal departments using natural language agents to build internal tools
- Sales, Legal, and Operations teams solving local process problems without engineering queues
- "The barrier that separates 'people who code' from 'people who don't' is becoming more permeable"
- Legora (Sweden) cited as example of agentic workflows in legal
- URL: https://resources.anthropic.com/2026-agentic-coding-trends-report

#### 2. Riff and Lovable as spillover platforms (Level 2)
Both Riff (Norway, $16M) and Lovable (Sweden, $200M ARR) are explicitly built on the thesis that business users — not developers — should build software using agents. Riff's customers span manufacturing, financial services, logistics, healthcare. This is the spillover made product.
- Riff URL: https://arcticstartup.com/riff-formerly-databutton-raises-16m-series-a/
- Lovable URL: https://techsverige.se/en/2025/12/lovable-grundarna-anton-osika-och-fabian-hedin-vinnare-av-arets-ai-svensk-2025/

#### 3. Swedish AI Reform as mass spillover experiment (Level 2)
2.3 million Swedes (civil servants, students, teachers, researchers, nonprofits) getting free access to Sana Agents. This is the largest non-coding agent deployment experiment in the Nordics. If it works, it proves the spillover thesis at national scale.
- URL: https://sanalabs.com/ai-reform

#### 4. Klarna's cross-functional AI deployment (Level 2-3)
Klarna is the strongest single-company case of spillover: 96% of employees use AI daily, 300+ internal GPTs, AI across customer service, marketing (25% agency spend cut), and internal operations. CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski: "There is a massive shift coming to knowledge work. And it's not just in banking, it's in society at large."
- URL: https://fortune.com/2025/10/10/klarna-ceo-sebastian-siemiatkowski-halved-workforce-says-tech-ceos-sugarcoating-ai-impact-on-jobs-mass-unemployment-warning/

#### 5. Optivian and Vic.ai as function-specific spillover (Level 2)
Both companies take the autonomous execution pattern from coding agents and apply it to sales (Optivian) and finance (Vic.ai). These are not chatbots or copilots — they are agents that "do the work," which is the exact language from coding agents.
- Optivian: https://techfundingnews.com/finnish-optivian-secures-2-3m-to-build-ai-agents-that-replace-entire-sales-teams/
- Vic.ai: https://www.vic.ai/blog/vic-agents-future-of-autonomous-finance

#### 6. PwC AI Agent Survey (Level 2 — global signal)
30% of AI agent builders are business users from Product, Marketing, Sales, Customer Service, and HR — not engineers. 79% of executives say agents are already being adopted. This is the global baseline for the spillover.
- URL: https://www.pwc.com/us/en/tech-effect/ai-analytics/ai-agent-survey.html

### Spillover Timeline Assessment

The pattern is clear: **the spillover is already happening, but unevenly.**

- **Where it's working now (2025-2026):** Customer service (Klarna), legal (Legora), finance/AP (Vic.ai, Basware), sales (Optivian), compliance (Spektr, Fortiv), product growth (Skene). These are all constrained, data-rich, workflow-heavy domains.
- **Where it's emerging:** Marketing (Klarna's internal use), journalism (Helsingin Sanomat Watchdog), public sector (Swedish AI Reform).
- **Where it's not visible yet in the Nordics:** HR, supply chain operations, procurement, strategic planning.

The article thesis ("When will agentic ways in coding spill to other functions?") has a simple answer from the Nordic evidence: **it already has, but only in domains with clear workflows and abundant structured data. The messy, judgment-heavy functions (HR, strategy, procurement) are still waiting.**
