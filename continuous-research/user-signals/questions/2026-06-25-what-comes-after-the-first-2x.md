# What can a team expect AFTER an initial ~2X agentic-coding doubling?

A builder leader measured a ~2X PR-throughput gain from simple coding-agent adoption and is preparing a presentation that cites "4–8X productivity gains." Question: is 4–8X real, and what further multiple can be expected after the first doubling — and from what?

**Answered by this cycle** (`findings/by-pattern/productivity-gains-2026-06-25/SYNTHESIS.md`):
- Independent measured **throughput** converges at ~1.5–2X (the 2X is dead-center, not modest); org-**outcome** collapses to ~10%.
- 4–8X traces to code-volume share, modeled ROI, or testimonial — never independent measured value-shipped. Anthropic's own 8X is lines-of-code/day, vendor-self-disclaimed ("almost certainly an overstatement").
- The next multiple is **not a bigger number** — it is a mechanism (fleets → verification-as-infra → compound engineering → role inversion), rate-limited by org learning rate. The first 2X drains the ~16% coding fraction; Amdahl caps the rest.
