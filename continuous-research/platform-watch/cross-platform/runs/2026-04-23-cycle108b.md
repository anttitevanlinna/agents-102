**Source:** https://m365admin.handsontek.net/microsoft-agent-365-generally-available-may-1-2026/ — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** April 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Agent 365 reaches GA May 1, 2026 at $15/user/month standalone or included in M365 E7 at $99/user/month. Entra Agent IDs provide each agent with directory identity and lifecycle management, viewable in M365 Admin Center. Agents acting on behalf of licensed users are covered under the user's license — agents themselves don't need separate licenses. **Critical gap at launch:** Security posture management for Foundry and Copilot Studio agents + detection and response remain in public preview on May 1, not GA. **Evidence level:** Level 1 (vendor-documented facts + licensing specialist analysis). No independent practitioner deployment reports yet.
**Key claims:**
- (1) GA May 1, 2026 confirmed — $15/user/month
- (2) All agents acting on behalf of a licensed user covered by that user's license (no per-agent charge)
- (3) Security posture management (Foundry + Copilot Studio agents) still in preview on GA day
- (4) Runtime threat protection via Agent 365 tools gateway = public preview April, not GA

### Finding 2: Microsoft Agent 365 practitioner assessment — "right product, commercial model still catching up"

**Source:** https://robquickenden.blog/2026/03/agent-365-nears-ga/ — [practitioner direct — Rob Quickenden is an independent Microsoft MVP / digital workplace practitioner]

**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Quickenden characterizes Agent 365 as "the right product at the right time" — the governance and identity layer enterprise needs as AI shifts from assistant to actor. **Evidence level:** Level 2 — one practitioner's direct assessment of the product architecture and commercial model. Honest positive/negative split.
**Key claims:**
- (1) Entra Agent IDs and M365 Admin Center observability are structurally sound

- (3) "Commercial model still catching up with the technology" — official framing
