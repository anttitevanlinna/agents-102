### 5. Agent Security Data Deepens — Two New Survey Sources
**Source:** [HiddenLayer 2026 AI Threat Report](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hiddenlayer-releases-the-2026-ai-threat-landscape-report-spotlighting-the-rise-of-agentic-ai-and-the-expanding-attack-surface-of-autonomous-systems-302716687.html) — [vendor press release, but survey data], [Saviynt/Cybersecurity Insiders CISO AI Risk Report](https://saviynt.com/ciso-ai-risk-report-2026) — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Two new reports quantify the agent security gap that Meta's Sev 1 incident illustrated:
- **HiddenLayer (250 IT/security leaders):** Autonomous agents account for >1 in 8 reported AI breaches. 35% cite supply chain (malicious models in public repos) as top breach source. 31% don't know if they had an AI breach. 53% withheld breach reporting due to fear of backlash.
- **Saviynt (235 CISOs, US/UK, 5K+ employee companies):** 47% observed AI agents exhibiting unauthorized behavior. Only 5% confident they could contain a compromised agent. 92% lack full visibility into AI identities. 75% found shadow AI with unmonitored credentials. 86% don't enforce access policies for AI identities. Only 16% effectively govern AI agent access to core systems.

Combined with cycle 47's Gravitee survey (88% had agent security incidents, 14.4% deploy with full approval), this is now **Level 3 convergence on agent security gap** — three independent surveys with consistent findings.
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (survey data, not deployment evidence, but convergence across three independent surveys)
**Key claims:**
- 47% of CISOs observed unauthorized agent behavior (Saviynt, n=235)
- Only 5% confident in containment capability

- 92% lack visibility into AI identities
- Only 16% effectively govern agent access to core systems

## Orientation

**Three patterns crystallize this cycle:**

**Pattern 48a: Compound engineering = Level 3 convergence (coding methodology).** With 12 independent practitioner signals across startups (Every), mid-size (Imprint), enterprise (Autodesk), platform builders (Anthropic), and Microsoft CVP, compound engineering has crossed the convergence threshold. The methodology is becoming the default workflow for serious AI-assisted development. Counter-evidence is real (6-month ramp, high initial failure rate, scaling questions) but doesn't invalidate the pattern. The "beyond coding" extension remains weak — only one practitioner in economics. This is significant for our flywheel thesis: coding agents are the validated domain, but the business-domain leap hasn't happened.

**Pattern 48b: Agent security gap = Level 3 convergence (quantified + three named incidents).** Three independent surveys in March 2026 (Gravitee n=900+, HiddenLayer n=250, Saviynt n=235) converge on the same finding: enterprises deploy agents faster than they govern them. 47% observe unauthorized behavior, only 5% can contain compromised agents, 92% lack visibility, >1 in 8 breaches linked to agents. **Three named-company incidents in one month:** Meta Sev 1 (confused deputy), McKinsey Lilli (vulnerability exploitation, 46.5M messages), OpenClaw CVE-2026-25253 (supply chain poisoning, 820+ malicious skills). Three different attack vectors, three different companies, one month. Governance tools exist (ConductorOne, Entro, Stacklok) but zero are deployed. The gap between "we need this" and "we have this" is the widest in the entire agent landscape.

**Pattern 48c: March 31 deadline will pass quietly.** Cowork (14th zero), credit cliff (zero complaints), Zendesk-Forethought (still pending) — all three are non-events so far. This tells us the entire business agent space remains pre-chasm. The deadlines that would matter if adoption were real are barely noticed because adoption isn't real.
