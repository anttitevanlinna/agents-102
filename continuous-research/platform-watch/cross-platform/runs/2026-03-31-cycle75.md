### Finding 8: Counter-evidence — the solo + AI productivity question
**Source:** [METR — Measuring the Impact of Early-2025 AI on Experienced Open-Source Developer Productivity](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/) — [academic/research]
**Date:** July 2025
**What:** Rigorous study found that allowing AI actually increased completion time by 19% for experienced open-source developers. AI tooling slowed developers down on tasks they knew well. This challenges both "solo + AI = 5x" AND "mob + AI = better" claims — the baseline assumption that AI consistently improves individual productivity is itself questionable for experienced developers.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single study, academic rigor)

**Additional counter-evidence sources:**
- Saarland University 2025 study: human-AI pairs had fewer "broad" conversations than human-human pairs; developers accepted AI suggestions more readily, even when subtly wrong — [academic/research] [SOURCE NEEDED — referenced in search summary, specific URL not retrieved]
- Stack Overflow 2025: only 33% of developers trust AI output; 46% actively distrust it — [practitioner survey] [SOURCE NEEDED — referenced in aggregate statistics]
- Anthropic 2026 study: AI users finished slightly faster but scored 50% on post-task knowledge vs. 67% for non-AI control group — learning penalty — [academic/research] [SOURCE NEEDED — referenced in search summary]

**The compound engineering counter-argument:** Every Inc's model (Dan Shipper) shows 1 person + AI compounding over time. If one person with AI can do the work of 5, why add 3-4 more humans to a mob? The mob adds coordination overhead. The counter-counter: mob maintains shared context and prevents architectural divergence when AI-assisted developers each go their own way.

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

1. **Quantitative results from any MobAI practitioner.** Zero hard metrics found. No velocity comparisons, no before/after data, no controlled measurements. The Scania team reports qualitative benefits only. The Solita/ISS metrics (100x, 500x) come from a vendor case study and are extraordinary/unverified. This is the biggest gap.

2. **Independent evaluation of MobAI or CollabAI.** Every source is either Joe Justice himself, someone who attended his workshop, or a consultancy selling the methodology. No academic study. No independent practitioner who tried it and reported results without connection to Justice or the consulting ecosystem.

3. **Woody Zuill's opinion on AI + mob programming.** The originator of mob programming has not publicly commented on the AI-augmented version (at least not in any source I could find). This is notable — if the methodology's creator endorsed or critiqued it, that would be significant signal.

4. **Large-scale adoption of MobAI.** All evidence is workshop-level or single-team level. No organization has adopted MobAI at scale (beyond one team). The Scania case is 4 people. The Solita/ISS case is a single project.

5. **Head-to-head comparison: mob + AI vs. solo + AI.** Nobody has run this experiment. The flurdy blog argues theoretically that mob + AI beats solo + AI, but there is zero empirical evidence either way.

6. **Enterprise results from JoeDX workshops.** Joe Justice runs workshops across Europe (Crisp in Sweden, Agilizer Academy in Switzerland/Germany, various conferences). Not a single attendee organization has published measurable results from implementing MobAI.

7. **Convergence signal.** We found ~5 independent practitioners/teams connected to MobAI or CollabAI (Scania team, Virpi Rowe/Sofigate, Marko Taipale/Solita, WikiAgile robot project, flurdy blog). But all are connected to Joe Justice's speaking/consulting network. Independent convergence = zero.

## Orientation

### What this means

**MobAI and CollabAI are real methodologies being practiced, but the evidence is thin.**

The picture that emerges:

1. **Joe Justice is the hub.** Everything traces back to him — directly or through one degree of separation (attended his workshop, read his material, heard him speak). He coined MobAI in 2023, evangelizes through JoeDX workshops and conference keynotes, and has built a consulting/training ecosystem around it. Strong Nordic distribution channel via Crisp (Sweden) and Lean Forum (Sweden).

2. **CollabAI is the Finnish branch.** Marko Taipale (Solita) took similar ideas — synchronous collaboration, role rotation, AI in the loop — and formalized them as "CollabAI" with a book (Jan 2026). Joe Justice wrote the foreword, confirming the connection. The Solita/ISS "Twin Project" is the closest thing to a controlled experiment, but the metrics are vendor-reported.

3. **The Scania/TRATON team is the best evidence.** A real 4-person team, at a real Swedish enterprise, practicing daily MobAI for months, with a third-party account (Crisp blog by Michael Göthe). But: no quantitative results. Just qualitative "it feels better" and "splitting up was painful."

4. **The methodology is plausible but unproven.** The core thesis — that mob programming's shared context advantage becomes even more important when AI amplifies individual divergence — is logically sound. But there is zero empirical evidence that mob + AI outperforms solo + AI. The METR study's finding that AI doesn't even reliably help experienced solo developers makes the entire premise shakier.

5. **This is Level 1-2, not Level 3.** We have practitioner opinions (Level 1) and a couple of single experiments (Level 2). We have zero convergence (Level 3). The "practitioners" are almost entirely within the Joe Justice consulting network, so independence is questionable even for Level 2.

### What to watch for

- **Independent teams adopting mob + AI without connection to Joe Justice.** If non-Justice-network teams independently arrive at "we mob with AI and it's better," that would be convergence signal.
- **Scania/TRATON publishing quantitative data.** They're the most credible practitioner. Hard numbers would upgrade to solid Level 2.
- **The Solita Twin Project getting independent review.** If an academic or independent analyst reviews the 100x/500x claims, that would be highly valuable either way.
- **Woody Zuill commenting on AI + mob.** The originator's perspective would clarify whether MobAI is evolution or marketing.

### Implications for the research system

MobAI/CollabAI represents a **distinct AI-native team pattern** worth tracking alongside Anthropic's tiny-teams and Tesla's data-as-manager models. The pattern is: **synchronous co-creation with AI as participant, not asynchronous delegation to AI as tool.** This is the opposite of the compound engineering model (solo + AI). Both could be valid for different contexts — mob + AI for learning velocity and shared context, solo + AI for maximum output velocity.

The Nordic signal is genuinely strong here: Swedish enterprise practitioner (Scania), Swedish consulting/training hub (Crisp + Lean Forum), Finnish methodology author (Taipale/Solita), Finnish practitioner (Rowe/Sofigate). This is one of the few AI-native practices with a distinctly Nordic cluster.

### Risk assessment

**Confirmation bias risk: HIGH.** This entire space is a consulting ecosystem built around Joe Justice. It would be easy to write this up as "Nordic innovation in AI-native teamwork" when it might be "one speaker's workshop circuit producing enthusiastic but unmeasured adopters." The absence of independent evidence and quantitative results is a serious credibility gap. We should report what we found honestly — including the gap — rather than overclaim.
