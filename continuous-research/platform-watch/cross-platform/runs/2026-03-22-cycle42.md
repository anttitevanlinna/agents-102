### Finding 5: Agentforce Architectural Limits — 15 Topics, 20 Agents Per Org
**Source:** [https://www.apexhours.com/agentforce-limitations-and-workarounds/](https://www.apexhours.com/agentforce-limitations-and-workarounds/) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Agentforce enforces strict boundaries: 15 topics and 15 actions per agent, 20-agent limit per org. Data Cloud subscription mandatory (significant additional cost). Typical implementation $50K-$150K. These constraints explain why beyond-CS adoption is thinner — enterprises hitting architectural ceilings when trying to scale Agentforce across departments.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (practitioner documentation of technical constraints)
**Key claims:**
- 15 topics / 15 actions per agent architectural limit
- 20 agents per org limit
- Data Cloud subscription mandatory
- Implementation cost $50K-$150K typical

## Orientation

The vertical SaaS picture is getting more nuanced. **Agentforce has real revenue ($800M ARR) but practitioner counter-evidence now shows adoption friction is real** — sub-20% workflow adoption, architectural limits (15 topics/20 agents), and $50-150K implementation costs. The IRS and Williams-Sonoma successes from cycle 40 are genuine but may represent the best cases, not the norm.

**ServiceNow is strengthening** — CVS Health CISO providing independent governance detail is the kind of practitioner signal we need. ServiceNow's governance-first positioning (AI Control Tower governing multi-vendor agents) is unique.

**The Cowork absence in its promised window is now the strongest it's been.** If no independent reviews appear by April, the Cowork late-March promise was either delayed or rolled out under NDA. Either way, the business agent surface that Microsoft was supposed to deliver via Anthropic integration remains unverified.

**Pattern emerging: vertical SaaS reality is "strong in CS, immature everywhere else."** Even Agentforce, which leads, shows sub-20% workflow adoption and architectural limits that constrain multi-department scaling. The gap between CS success and other-domain success is wider than the positive findings suggested.
