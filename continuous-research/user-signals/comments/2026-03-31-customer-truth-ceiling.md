Practitioner's partial answer on the "broad learning" ceiling question, from their published article "Shifting toward customers - finding the truth" (LinkedIn, Feb 2026):

**The ceiling is how well you can capture the elusive "truth."** Customer truth, encoded as evals, is the external loop that corrects internal learning. Without it, broad learning is broad hallucination — confident wrongness at scale.

**The eval + iteration model:**
1. Turn customer needs into evaluation criteria (evals)
2. AI iterates against evals autonomously
3. Prototype to customers
4. Customer feedback → richer evals → repeat

"If you don't have evals, you cannot let AI loose to iterate autonomously toward the goal. You are all the time babysitting and nudging."

**Three ceilings for three types of learning (agreed in discussion):**
- Products → customer eval loop (customer truth)
- Processes → operational metric loop (Karpathy-style scalar metrics)
- Strategy → human judgment loop (what to learn, irreducibly human for now)

**Connection to research system findings:**
- Verification infrastructure = encoding truth into checkable criteria
- Absorption bottleneck = slows the feedback loop to customers, delays eval improvement
- Specs = hypotheses about customer truth that the eval loop validates or kills
- Zombie stat audit = what happens when internal learning has no external truth check
