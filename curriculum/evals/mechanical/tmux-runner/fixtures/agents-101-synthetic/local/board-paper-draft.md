# Q3 board paper — fleet-dispatch pricing move (DRAFT, mine)

Working notes for the board paper due in three weeks. Rough, not for circulation.

## The ask
Approve the Q3 pilot: move our fleet-dispatch SaaS from per-seat to usage-based
(priced on dispatched routes) for an 8-account cohort. Committed monthly floor
plus per-route overage.

## Why now
Seat revenue has flatlined as accounts share logins. Dispatched-route volume is
climbing ~40% year on year. The current model leaves that growth uncaptured.

## What I still owe the board
- The floor level. Set it too low and we leak the overage; too high and the SMB
  accounts balk on day one.
- The churn case. The pilot cohort is deliberately SMB-heavy, and SMB volume is
  the most volatile. I need a defensible read on whether a variable bill drives
  these accounts out, or whether a floor-plus-cap design holds them.
- The sales narrative. The field team needs three sentences they can say in a
  renewal call without sounding like a price rise.

## Open question I keep circling
Is the SMB churn risk a pricing-design problem (fix it with the floor and a cap)
or a segment problem (usage-based is simply wrong for volatile SMB)? The board
will ask. I do not yet have the answer.
