## Finding 1: ServiceNow Autonomous Workforce — Paul Fipps confirms L1 elimination

**URL:** https://diginomica.com/servicenows-paul-fipps-enterprise-ai-llm-reasons-its-platform-executes
**Source type:** [domain trade publication] — Diginomica interview with ServiceNow executive
**Date:** ~March 2026
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, vendor-internal deployment)

**What was learned:** Paul Fipps, President of Global Customer Operations at ServiceNow, stated in a Diginomica interview: "We no longer have a level one Help Desk at ServiceNow. All of that level one help desk activity is now handled autonomously using agentic AI." Displaced L1 workers were moved to higher-value roles — "many of them are now working in cloud operations, or at level two, or in data centers." This is the strongest practitioner-level statement yet about ServiceNow's internal deployment.

**Assessment:** This is a vendor executive talking about their own company's internal deployment — credible on the "what happened" but not independent. The displacement-to-upskilling narrative is vendor-favorable framing. No external customer has confirmed equivalent results. Still Level 2 (single experiment, vendor-internal).

## Finding 5: SAP Joule — Companies still wary (E3 Magazine / Horváth study)

**URL:** https://e3mag.com/en/companies-are-still-wary-of-sap-joule/
**Source type:** [domain trade publication] — E3 Magazine (SAP ecosystem trade press)
**Date:** January 23, 2026
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (independent survey of 200 SAP customers)

**What was learned:** Horváth study of 200 SAP-using companies (>€200M revenue): six out of ten are still in S/4HANA transformation phase. SAP Joule adoption is "early bird stage" — most experience based on tests and proof-of-value projects, not production. Experts recommend focusing initially on "mass processing or standard cases" where benefit is immediate. Horváth says there is "virtually no risk — apart from the risk of getting bogged down."

**Assessment:** This is the most useful independent evidence on SAP Joule adoption reality. The structural barrier is clear: companies still converting to S/4HANA cannot meaningfully adopt Joule agents. This explains the evidence vacuum — it's not that Joule doesn't work, it's that most SAP customers aren't in a position to try it yet.

**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (financial data independently confirmed; deployment claims mostly vendor-sourced)

**What was learned:**
- **FY2026 revenue arc:** Started with a miss (Feb 2025 forecast below estimates, citing slower Agentforce adoption). By Q3 FY2026 (Dec 2025), raised guidance to $41.45-41.55B. Final FY2026 results per state file: $800M ARR, 29K deals, 12K deployed.
- **New deployment evidence (not in state file):**
  - **Engine (travel):** $2M anticipated savings, CSAT 3.7→4.3, deployed in 12 days. Source: CX Today, independently reported.
  - **Nexo:** 62% case resolution as early adopter, despite "technical debt, data cleanup" challenges. Source: CX Today.
  - **Engie:** 83% user assistance rate with Agentforce. Source: CX Today.
  - **Fisher & Paykel:** Self-service 40%→70%. Source: CX Today (forward-looking language).
  - **Safari365:** Turned 15% efficiency goals into 30%+ reality with 35 employees. Source: CX Today.
  - **Precina Health:** A1C reduction from 9.6 to 6.4 in 50 patients over 12 weeks. Source: vendor.

- **Technical limitations confirmed by practitioners:**
  - 20-agent limit per org remains
  - BYOM unsupported — locked into Salesforce AI ecosystem
  - Token burn problem: agent keeps trying approaches, consuming credits
  - Topic conflicts: overlapping keywords cause Atlas Reasoning Engine to pause
  - External data latency impact on inference speed
  - Healthcare: 23% inaccuracies in automated inventory orders

**Assessment:** The deployment evidence base is widening beyond customer service (Precina Health, RBC Wealth Management), but most new named deployments use forward-looking language or come from vendor case study pages. The CX Today compilation (independently reported) adds Engine, Nexo, and Engie as Level 2 evidence. Technical limitation evidence continues to accumulate from practitioner sources (Apex Hours). ## Finding 10: Vertical SaaS AI agent landscape — new entrants and investment thesis

**Date:** Q1 2026
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (opinion, market thesis)

**What was learned:**
- **Bessemer Venture Partners:** Vertical AI "represents a fundamentally larger opportunity than vertical SaaS ever did" — competing for labor budgets, not software budgets.
- **Y Combinator:** Vertical AI companies could be 10x larger than traditional SaaS they replace (~$300B+ opportunity).
- **New named entrants:**
  - **Sierra** ($150M ARR, customer service — see Finding 9)
  - **Writer** (enterprise AI platform, raised $200M+)

  - **Lindy** (sales and operations automation)
