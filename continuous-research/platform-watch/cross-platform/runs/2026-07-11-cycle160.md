### Finding 5: CodeRabbit Sonnet 5 regression — CORRECTION to cycle 159 framing

**Source:** https://www.coderabbit.ai/blog/claude-sonnet-5-review — [practitioner analysis — third-party code review tool]  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Agent level:** personal (developer tool)  

**What:** Cycle 159 reported only the headline ("CodeRabbit bug detection 63%→50-51%"). The full finding is materially different. Two metrics changed:
1. Recall (bug catch rate): 63% (Sonnet 4.6) → 50-51% (Sonnet 5) — **decline**
2. Precision (true positive rate among flags): 29% (Sonnet 4.6) → 38-40% (Sonnet 5) — **significant improvement**

Max effort setting: 2x cost, no meaningful additional bugs found. CodeRabbit conclusion: "a reviewer that flags fewer wrong things but also catches fewer real bugs is not obviously more accurate and depends entirely on which mistake costs you more."

**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single comprehensive study from named practitioner tooling company)  

**CORRECTION to cycle 159 synthesis:** The regression framing overstates the case. This is a precision/recall tradeoff, not a regression. For teams where false positives waste engineering time (most teams), Sonnet 5 may be a net improvement despite lower recall. For safety-critical codebases where missing a bug is catastrophic, Sonnet 4.6 remains better. Flag for synthesis update.
