Practitioner challenge: getting stuff "fully right" was always the engineering problem. Bigger amounts of uncertain change don't help — unless you can iterate to perfection fast.

Key reframe: the question isn't "can you iterate fast" but "can you redesign the iteration loop for 5x volume at 1.7x uncertainty?" Human-speed iteration doesn't scale against AI-speed generation. Machine-speed iteration (StrongDM holdout scenarios, Karpathy autoresearch, Anthropic Code Review) does — but only where acceptance criteria are machine-checkable.

**The dividing line:** Domains with machine-checkable acceptance criteria can iterate to perfection at AI speed. Domains without them are stuck with human-speed iteration against AI-speed generation. That IS the absorption bottleneck — and it's why it manifests in every domain where output requires human judgment.

**Connection to decision speed:** Even with automated iteration, someone decides WHAT to build, what "good" looks like, what the acceptance criteria are. That's the spec-writing skill. The human bottleneck that doesn't automate.

**Gap:** Who is building automated iteration loops for non-engineering domains? StrongDM solved it for code. Who's solving it for legal review, financial analysis, marketing content?
