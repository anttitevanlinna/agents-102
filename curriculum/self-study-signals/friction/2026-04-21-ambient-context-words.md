**Context:** Reading `what-is-an-agent.md` on the recap site (browser). Live test 2026-04-21.

**Signal:** "Close the tab — the chat ends" reads ambiguously when the student is *inside* a browser tab reading the doc. "Tab" gets reinterpreted as the reading surface, not the chat surface the sentence is talking about. Same problem in `context-is-king.md` ("Gone when you close the tab") and earlier in the same supplementary doc ("conversation ends when you close the tab").

**Gap:** General principle — **ambient context words** (`tab`, `window`, `app`) get reinterpreted by the reader through whatever surface they're currently in. A student reading in browser tab #3 with Claude Code in window #2 and a Builder window in #1 will route "the tab" / "the window" through their immediate surroundings, not the abstract example.

**Fix applied:** All three instances changed to "the chat window" — concrete, unambiguous, names what's actually being closed.

**Generalize:** scan student-facing curriculum for any use of `tab`, `window`, `app`, `screen` that depends on the reader knowing which surface is meant. Replace with the specific noun (chat window, Claude Code window, browser tab, etc.). The reader's eye routes ambient words through their environment first.
