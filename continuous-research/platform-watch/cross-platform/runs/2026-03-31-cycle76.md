### 1. Addy Osmani's "Comprehension Debt" — the closest named concept
**Source:** https://addyosmani.com/blog/comprehension-debt/ — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** March 2026
**What:** Osmani coined "comprehension debt" — the gap between code a system contains and code any human understands. AI generates 5-7x faster than developers absorb. Only 48% of developers consistently check AI-assisted code before committing, even though 38% find reviewing AI-generated logic requires MORE effort than reviewing human-written code. A junior engineer can now generate code faster than a senior engineer can critically audit it — the rate-limiting factor that kept review meaningful has been removed.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion with supporting survey data)
**Key claims:**
- AI generates 5-7x faster than developers absorb [SOURCE: Osmani's blog, survey data referenced]
- Only 48% of developers consistently check AI-assisted code before committing [SOURCE NEEDED — survey cited but not linked in search snippet]
- 38% find reviewing AI-generated logic requires more effort than reviewing human code [SOURCE NEEDED — survey cited]
- "What used to be a quality gate is now a throughput problem"
**Relationship to absorption bottleneck:** Comprehension debt describes the ACCUMULATION of the absorption gap over time. Absorption bottleneck is the THROUGHPUT constraint that creates comprehension debt. They are complementary concepts — Osmani names the debt, the practitioner observation names the flow constraint.

### 2. Osmani's "80% Problem in Agentic Coding"
**Source:** https://addyo.substack.com/p/the-80-problem-in-agentic-coding — [practitioner direct]

Developers succeeding with agentic coding spend 70% of time on problem definition and verification, 30% on execution — a complete inversion from traditional development. **Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner analysis synthesizing multiple data points)

- No decrease in overall workload despite 10+ hours/week saved on coding

### 3. Faros AI Productivity Paradox Report — quantitative evidence
**Source:** https://www.faros.ai/blog/ai-software-engineering — [practitioner analysis] (Note: Faros is a vendor but this is telemetry research across 10,000+ developers, not a product pitch)
**Date:** 2025
**What:** Telemetry from 10,000+ developers across 1,255 teams. Teams with high AI adoption: 21% more tasks completed, 98% more PRs merged, BUT PR review time increased 91%. PR size increased 154%. Bug rates increased 9%. No significant correlation between AI adoption and better DORA outcomes at the company level.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single large-scale measurement study)
**Key claims:**
- 21% more tasks completed per developer
- 98% more PRs merged
- 91% increase in PR review time
- 154% increase in PR size
- 9% increase in bug rates
- No correlation between AI adoption and improved DORA metrics at company level
**Critical finding for absorption bottleneck:** The 91% review time increase against 98% more PRs is the absorption bottleneck measured in engineering metrics. Volume up ~2x, review capacity effectively unchanged. The gap IS the bottleneck.

### 4. CodeRabbit "State of AI vs Human Code Generation" — quality compounds volume

**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single study, transparent methodology, vendor source — treat as supporting)
**Key claims:**
- 1.7x more issues per PR in AI-generated code
- 320 AI-coauthored PRs vs 150 human-only PRs analyzed
- Logic errors up 75%, security up 1.5-2x, performance up 8x
**Absorption multiplier:** The 1.7x quality gap COMPOUNDS the volume increase. If you have 2x more PRs, each with 1.7x more issues, the review burden isn't 2x — it's 3.4x. This is the mechanism that makes absorption nonlinear.

### 7. Karpathy: "Humans are the bottleneck" — autoresearch
**Source:** https://winbuzzer.com/2026/03/23/karpathy-humans-bottleneck-ai-research-xcxwbn/ — [general press]
**Also:** https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** March 2026
**What:** Karpathy's autoresearch ran 700 autonomous experiments over 2 days, found 20 additive improvements yielding 11% training speed gain. His framing: "Human researchers are now the bottleneck in any AI domain with a clear, computable metric." Role shifts from "experimenter" to "experimental designer." **Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment, replicated by Shopify)

**Relationship to absorption bottleneck:** Karpathy's approach BYPASSES absorption by constraining the domain to scalar-metric problems. The human doesn't review 700 experiments — they review the 20 that improved the metric. This is a coping strategy, not a refutation. It works because training loss is verifiable. Most business domains don't have a single scalar metric.

### 9. Simon Willison's "Slop" — the asymmetric effort framework
**Source:** https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/23/neurotica/ — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** Coined May 2024, refined March 2026
**What:** Willison defined "slop" as unwanted AI-generated content — analogous to "spam" for email. Key refinement (March 2026): "Slop is something that takes more human effort to consume than it took to produce." This is the absorption bottleneck expressed as an economics principle — when production effort approaches zero but consumption effort remains constant, the effort asymmetry becomes infinite.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (practitioner opinion/definition, but widely adopted)
**Key claims:**
- "Slop" = unwanted AI-generated content, analogous to spam
- Consumption effort > production effort = slop
- Not all AI content is slop — only content that is "mindlessly generated and thrust upon someone who didn't ask for it"

### 10. Leon Furze: "The Effort Economy of Slop" — academic framing
**Source:** https://leonfurze.com/2026/03/28/the-effort-economy-of-slop/ — [academic/research]
**Date:** March 28, 2026
**What:** Formalizes the effort asymmetry: "If the effort to consume what you've made exceeds the effort you put into making it, you are producing slop." The consumer does genuine cognitive work that the producer declined to do. Sending someone raw AI output is "disrespecting the value of someone else's time." Links to academic paper "Why Slop Matters" (arXiv 2601.06060) which identifies three features: superficial competence, asymmetric effort, mass producibility.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (academic opinion/framework)
**Key claims:**
- Effort asymmetry as the defining characteristic
- Consumption as labor — cognitive work transferred from producer to consumer
- Three features: superficial competence, asymmetric effort, mass producibility

### 12. Academic paper: "Overloaded Minds and Machines" — cognitive load framework
**Source:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-026-11510-z — [academic/research]

### 13. Aviation alarm fatigue — the established parallel
**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alarm_fatigue — [reference]
**Also:** https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/Meetings/a42/Documents/WP/wp_083_en.pdf — [academic/institutional]
**Date:** Established field, Three Mile Island (1979) as canonical example
**What:** When information volume exceeds processing capacity, critical signals get missed. Three Mile Island operator: "I would have liked to have thrown away the alarm panel. It wasn't giving us any useful information." Aviation solution: track 10,000+ data points, but alarm threshold set so <10% of flights trigger any alert at all. False positives are avoided because they make people tune out.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (decades of convergent evidence in human factors research)
**Key claims:**
- Alarm fatigue = desensitization from excessive alerts → missed critical signals
- Aviation principle: 10,000 data points monitored, <10% of flights trigger alerts
- False positive avoidance is MORE important than detection sensitivity
- Solution is aggressive filtering, not more information
**Direct parallel:** AI-generated output at volume IS the alarm fatigue scenario. 95% accuracy at overwhelming volume triggers the same desensitization. The aviation solution (aggressive filtering, prioritization, suppression of low-confidence signals) directly applies.

### 16. Progressive disclosure as absorption management

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

1. **The term "absorption bottleneck" in prior literature.** Nobody uses this exact phrase. The concept exists under multiple names (comprehension debt, cognitive overload, review bottleneck, effort asymmetry, alarm fatigue) but nobody has named the specific THROUGHPUT constraint where AI generation exceeds human evaluation capacity. The closest is Osmani's "comprehension debt" but that names the accumulated DEFICIT, not the FLOW constraint.

2. **Non-engineering domains experiencing absorption bottleneck explicitly.** Legal and financial compliance show the PRECONDITION (volume increasing, human review required) but I found no practitioner accounts of "we deployed AI and now we're drowning in its output." The legal domain is still in the "AI as triage" phase — using AI to reduce review volume, not yet at the stage where AI generates volume that needs review. The engineering domain is ahead because coding agents are more mature.

3. **Practitioners who scaled AI output review WITHOUT hitting absorption bottlenecks.** Only Karpathy's autoresearch approach, which constrains to scalar-metric domains. No counter-evidence in non-metric-constrained domains. The DX 2025 data (135,000 developers) shows NO companies where higher AI adoption correlated with better DORA outcomes at the organizational level. This is remarkable absence of counter-evidence.

4. **A formal model of the absorption-generation gap as a function.** Nobody has modeled: if generation increases at rate G and review capacity is fixed at R, what happens to quality Q over time? The closest is the Faros data (2x volume, 91% review time increase, 9% bug rate increase) which gives empirical data points but not a formal model.

5. **The word "absorption" used in this context by any major practitioner.** This appears to be genuinely novel framing.

## Orientation

### Is the Absorption Bottleneck a known phenomenon?

**Yes and no.** The PHENOMENON is widely observed and independently reported by multiple practitioners, researchers, and measurement studies. But it exists under at least SIX different names, none of which capture the specific throughput constraint:

| Name | Who | What it captures | What it misses |
|------|-----|------------------|----------------|
| Comprehension debt | Osmani | Accumulated understanding deficit | Throughput constraint |
| AI Productivity Paradox | Faros, NBER | Macro outcome failure | Root cause mechanism |
| Alarm fatigue | Aviation/nuclear | Desensitization under volume | AI-specific generation dynamics |
| Slop / effort asymmetry | Willison, Furze | Production-consumption imbalance | Organizational/team dynamics |
| Societal cognitive overload | Arxiv 2504.19990 | Systemic institutional paralysis | Practitioner-level actionability |
| Review bottleneck | DORA, DX, Cursor | Engineering-specific flow constraint | Cross-domain generalizability |

**The absorption bottleneck is the UNIFYING concept.** It names the specific flow constraint that:
- Creates comprehension debt (Osmani)
- Drives the productivity paradox (Faros/NBER)
- Triggers alarm fatigue dynamics (aviation parallel)
- Makes slop harmful (Willison/Furze effort asymmetry)
- Causes institutional cognitive overload (academic)
- Manifests as review bottleneck in engineering (DORA/DX/Cursor)

### The three-stage model

The practitioner's original observation holds up and can be formalized:

1. **Generation** — solved. AI produces at superhuman speed. (Every source confirms this.)
2. **Verification** — known problem, being addressed. Tests, evals, LLM-as-judge, agent-evaluates-agent. (Active investment by Anthropic, Cursor, academic community.)
3. **Absorption** — the NEXT bottleneck. Even verified output must be absorbed by humans to inform decisions, maintain understanding, and steer the system. This is bounded by human cognitive capacity, not technology. (No solution yet beyond progressive disclosure and aggressive filtering.)

### Naming opportunity

"Absorption bottleneck" is a genuinely novel name for a convergent phenomenon. The existing names each capture one facet. "Absorption bottleneck" captures the THROUGHPUT constraint (bottleneck), the HUMAN cognitive limit (absorption), and implies the three-stage progression (generation → verification → absorption). It is more precise than "comprehension debt" (which is the accumulated result, not the constraint) and more actionable than "societal cognitive overload" (which is too abstract).

### Coping strategies observed

1. **Constrain to scalar metrics** (Karpathy): only review what improved the metric. Works for research/ML. Fails for business processes.
2. **Agent-evaluates-agent** (Anthropic Code Review, LLM-as-judge): AI filters before human sees output. Reduces volume but introduces second-order trust problem.
3. **Progressive disclosure** (UX pattern): layer information Index → Summary → Detail. Reduces initial cognitive load but doesn't reduce total review volume.
4. **Aggressive filtering / alarm threshold** (aviation principle): only surface high-confidence alerts. Requires confidence scoring infrastructure.
5. **Effort asymmetry awareness** (Willison/Furze): social norm — don't send raw AI output. Reduces interpersonal slop but doesn't help individual practitioners reviewing their own agents' output.

### What this means for the research system

This is a **nameable, Level 3 convergent finding** that connects coding, research, compliance, and organizational behavior. It belongs in `findings/by-pattern/` as a cross-domain pattern alongside hybrid-beats-autonomous and competence-first. The three-stage model (generation → verification → absorption) extends the existing verification infrastructure finding.

The absorption bottleneck is also the mechanism behind the "organizational learning rate" thesis in CLAUDE.md: "The real ceiling is organizational learning rate — how fast can the org learn to create the right context for agents to run?" Absorption IS the learning rate constraint. If the org can't absorb what agents produce, it can't learn from them, and the ceiling stays low regardless of generation capacity.
