## Agion — What It Actually Does

**URL:** [agion.dev](https://agion.dev)

### The Product
Agion bills itself as **"The Agentic OS for the Sovereign Enterprise"** — a full-stack operating system to deploy, guide, govern, and audit thousands of AI agents.

**Core framework:** AANG (Agion AI-Native Governance)
- Translates business KPIs into executable missions for AI agents
- Ethics and policies become automated, unbreakable rules ("Governance-as-Code" / GaC)
- Real-time trust scoring system: agents earn autonomy — higher trust = more permissions
- Claims O(1) governance complexity, 95%+ automated decisions, GDPR & EU AI Act compliance

**Positioning:**
- Cloud-agnostic, LLM-agnostic, governance-first
- European values: "built on Nordic principles of trust, transparency, and human-centric design"
- Sovereign: data, platform, agents, and infrastructure remain under client control; Agion provides the control layer
- Targets: public sector, financial services, industrial and regulated industries

### Founding Story
- Founded **December 2025** by Mikko Alasaarela (Co-Founder & Chairman) and **Janne Pulkkinen** (Co-Founder & CEO)
- Pulkkinen was formerly **Chief Innovation Officer at Kela** (Finland's social security institution) — led dozens of AI projects benefiting millions of Finns, member of EU Blockchain Partnership
- They describe the ambition as building "a credible European AI Cloud giant" from the Nordics
- **AI COO:** Jukka Ala-Mutka
- Team size: ~1-10 employees

### Traction
- **Investors:** Wolt co-founder **Juhani Mykkanen** and Oura founder **Petteri Lahtela** invested (undisclosed amount) and joined the board ([source](https://www.mainsights.io/ma-news/founders-of-wolt-and-oura-invest-in-finnish-ai-company-agion))
- **Revenue:** Nearly EUR 1 million in domestic sales in first 6 months (per investor announcement)
- **Go-to-market:** Fixed-fee 1-day intro workshop (analyze systems, build roadmap for first agentic processes), then subscription + agent-complexity pricing. Separate purchasing model for government.

### Assessment of Agion
This is a real company with real revenue and credible investors. The governance-as-code angle is genuine and differentiated. The "European AI Cloud giant" ambition is a stretch for a ~10-person company, but the public sector + regulated industry focus is smart positioning for a Nordic company. The Kela connection via Pulkkinen gives direct public sector credibility.

**What is NOT clear from public sources:** actual deployed customer names, specific case studies, technical architecture details, or how many agents are actually running on the platform.

## His Thinking on Agents

### Core Positions (synthesized from podcasts, talks, and post titles)

**1. "Everything will be agentic" — maximalist position**
- Believes AGI will arrive ~2026-2027 (revised from earlier skepticism)
- LinkedIn post title: "AGI will land in 2026. We still have a chance..." ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alasaarela_agi-will-land-in-2026-we-still-have-a-chance-activity-7290630717091291139-UtS6))
- Predicted in Helsingin Sanomat that "a large portion of public administration" could be automated with AI agents ([Verkkouutiset](https://www.verkkouutiset.fi/a/arvio-tekoalysta-hsssa-suuri-osa-julkishallinnosta-voitaisiin-automatisoida/))
- Podcast title: "Julkinen sektori agenttifarmiksi" (= "Public sector into an agent farm") ([Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/fi/podcast/julkinen-sektori-agenttifarmiksi-alasaarela-neuvottelija/id1497033284?i=1000721909536))

**2. Agent governance is the key problem**
- Agion's entire product thesis is that deploying agents is easy; governing them at scale is hard
- AANG framework: trust scoring, Governance-as-Code, automated compliance
- "The modern IT infrastructure has been replaced" — LinkedIn post suggesting a paradigm shift ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alasaarela_the-modern-it-infrastructure-has-been-replaced-activity-7271205862961008640-UwwF))

**3. AI-native organizations, not AI-augmented**
- Talked at SHIFT about "what it truly means to be AI-native" — challenged traditional leadership thinking ([SHIFT](https://theshift.fi/news/how-to-build-an-ai-native-organization-mikko-alasaarelas-vision-for-the-future/))
- "How to Build an AI-Native Organization" — co-presented with Teemu Linna (Sitra as a case study) at AI Monday Helsinki, May 2025
- Claims most Finnish people either don't use AI or use it only for "menial tasks" — needs a change in ways of thinking

**4. Machine customers and autonomous agent economies**
- On AI Experience Podcast: discussed "machine customers" — agents that buy, negotiate, and interact independently
- Discussed "agentic organisations where swarms of AI agents coordinate autonomously and serve both internal operations and external customers"
- This is a future-forward position — closer to Gartner's "machine customer" thesis

**5. Ethical framing**
- Self-describes as "ethical AI hacker"
- Book "The Servant Influencer" on ethical algorithmic influence
- Advised Finnish Parliament on AI strategy
- This is consistent across years — not a recent pivot

### Thinking Trajectory
His trajectory visible from public output:
- **Pre-2023:** Algorithmic influence, social media filter bubbles, emotional AI, blockchain/tokens (Inbot). Published on Medium about smart contracts + AI, emotional AI, and AGI tokens.
- **2023-2024:** Shifted toward AI governance and AGI preparedness. Was previously skeptical of rapid AGI, then revised views.

**Key observation:** He went from AI-influence-theorist to agent-governance-practitioner in about 18 months. The pivot is sharp and recent.

1. **Thought leader / evangelist:** Heavy podcast presence (4+ appearances in 6 months, all in Finnish). LinkedIn as primary broadcast channel. Speaker bureau presence. Maximalist on AGI timeline and agent adoption.
