## Findings

### 1. Stanford Enterprise AI Playbook — 51 Deployments, Workflow Redesign = #1 Factor
**Source:** https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/publication/enterprise-ai-playbook/ — [academic/research]
**Date:** March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Brynjolfsson, Pereira, and Graylin studied 51 AI deployments across 41 organizations in 7 countries. 77% of the toughest challenges were invisible organizational costs — change management, data quality, process redesign. High performers 3x more likely to fundamentally redesign workflows. The specific AI model doesn't matter much; strong processes and disciplined execution matter more.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence — 51 deployments, cross-industry pattern)
**Key claims:** (1) 77% of challenges are organizational, not technical. (2) High performers 3x more likely to redesign workflows. (3) Model choice is secondary to organizational readiness.

### 2. UK Government Copilot Trial — Cleanest "Just Deploy Tools" Experiment
**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/microsoft-365-copilot-experiment-cross-government-findings-report/microsoft-365-copilot-experiment-cross-government-findings-report-html — [academic/research — government controlled experiment]
**Date:** June 2025
**Agent level:** Personal
**What:** 3-month controlled trial, 1,000 licences, 30% randomly selected. "We did not find robust evidence to suggest that time savings are leading to improved productivity." Users performed 1.14 Copilot actions per working day. Email faster, but Excel and PowerPoint slower or lower quality.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment, but methodologically strong — controlled government trial)
**Key claims:** (1) Zero productivity evidence from tool deployment alone. (2) 1.14 actions/day average usage. (3) Quality actually decreased in some tools.

### 3. The Productivity Paradox Is Real — Level 3 Convergence
**Source:** Multiple:
- Atlanta Fed: https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/publications/working-papers/2026/03/25/04-artificial-intelligence-productivity-and-the-workforce-evidence-from-corporate-executives — [academic/research] — March 2026
- Goldman Sachs via Fortune: https://fortune.com/2026/03/03/goldman-earnings-ai-anxiety-no-meaningful-impact-productivity-economy-30-percent-in-2-areas/ — [general press] — March 2026
- NBER/CEO survey via Fortune: https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/ai-productivity-paradox-ceo-study-robert-solow-information-technology-age/ — [general press] — Feb 2026
- Faros AI: https://www.faros.ai/blog/ai-software-engineering — [practitioner analysis] — June 2025
**Date:** Feb-March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** 5+ independent sources converge: individual AI productivity does not aggregate to institutional value. NBER: 89% of managers saw no change despite 61→71% adoption. Goldman: no economy-wide relationship, but 30% gains in two specific zones (customer support, coding). Faros AI: company-level AI adoption has zero correlation with company-level performance metrics, even though individual engineers are faster.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence)
**Key claims:** (1) 89% of managers: no productivity change (NBER). (2) 30% gains only in customer support + coding (Goldman). (3) Zero company-level correlation with AI adoption (Faros AI, 10K developers).

### 4. Block "From Hierarchy to Intelligence" — Most Documented Redesign Model
**Source:** https://sequoiacap.com/article/from-hierarchy-to-intelligence/ — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** March 31, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Dorsey/Botha eliminated middle management, replaced with AI "world model" for coordination. Three remaining roles: Individual Contributors, Directly Responsible Individuals (90-day cycles), Player-Coaches. But: Block cut ~4,000 employees (~40%) BEFORE publishing this essay. Reads as post-hoc rationalization, not design-first experiment.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment — documented but unproven)
**Key claims:** (1) 3-role model replaces management layers. (2) AI "world model" handles coordination. (3) ~40% workforce reduction preceded the essay.

### 5. DAIN Studios "The Agent-Centric Enterprise" — Nordic Signal
**Source:** https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/0mrfxamu/release/3 — [practitioner analysis, published in academic journal]
**Date:** Winter 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Helsinki-based DAIN Studios published "The Agent-Centric Enterprise: Why 2-10x Productivity Gains Demand Radical Workflow Redesign" in Harvard Data Science Review. Introduces "Agent OS" — organizational operating system with AI agents as primary actors, humans as supervisors/coaches/exception-handlers. A.G.E.N.T. Framework for organizational redesign. Faculty for Harvard's Agentic AI Intensive.
**Evidence level:** Level 1-2 (framework from consultancy, but published in academic journal)
**Key claims:** (1) Redesign with agents as primary actors. (2) Nordic-origin contribution. (3) Faculty at Harvard AI program.

### 6. Klarna Reversal — Strongest Evidence That Subtraction ≠ Redesign
**Source:** https://www.charterworks.com/what-klarna-learned-from-its-ambitious-ai-rollout/ — [domain trade publication]; https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/klarna-ceo-reverses-course-by-hiring-more-humans-not-ai/491396 — [general press]
**Date:** 2025-2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Cut ~700 CS positions, replaced with AI. Quality degraded. CEO: "We went too far." Now re-hiring for hybrid model. 55% of companies that made AI-driven layoffs regret the decision (Orgvue survey, 1,100 C-suite). Klarna subtracted humans — they did NOT redesign workflows or decision rights.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment — well-documented failure)
**Key claims:** (1) Reversal after quality degradation. (2) 55% layoff regret rate (Orgvue, N=1,100). (3) Failure mode = subtraction without redesign.

### 7. P&G "Cybernetic Teammate" — 1 Person + AI = 1 Team
**Source:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5188231 — [academic/research]
**Date:** March 2025
**Agent level:** Team
**What:** Harvard/Wharton field experiment at P&G, N=776. Individuals with AI matched team-without-AI performance. Non-core professionals matched experienced teams when using AI. AI dissolved traditional expertise boundaries. BUT: also reduced social connectivity within teams — may destroy informal coordination.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single experiment, rigorous — pre-registered, 776 professionals)
**Key claims:** (1) 1+AI ≈ 2 humans without AI. (2) Expertise boundaries dissolved. (3) Social connectivity reduced.

### 8. Three Tiers of Organizational Change (Synthesis Finding)
**Source:** Synthesized across counter-evidence search — no single source
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Sivulka presents a binary (redesign or fail). Evidence supports three tiers: (1) Individual tool gains — real but don't aggregate. (2) Cultural adoption + light org change — Shopify model: changes norms, incentives, infrastructure, NOT org structure. (3) Full institutional redesign — Block/Bayer model: changes roles, hierarchy, decision rights. The middle tier is where most value lives for most organizations, and Sivulka ignores it entirely.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (synthesis of multiple L2 findings)
**Key claims:** (1) Shopify represents a viable middle path. (2) Cultural change + constraint (no new headcount without AI justification) produces results without restructuring. (3) The binary framing is too simple.

### 9. AI-Native Startups — The Endpoint Without a Path
**Source:** https://x.com/marty_kausas/status/1892257884194423170 — [practitioner direct]; https://andrew.ooo/posts/mercor-45m-revenue-per-employee-ai-training/ — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Midjourney (~40 people, ~$400M revenue), Cursor (~50 people, $500M+ ARR), Mercor (~30 people, $50M ARR). Revenue/employee $1M-10M vs traditional $150K-500K. Born AI-native, never redesigned. Solo founders: 36.3% of startups (up from 23.7% in 2019). "Super-IC" role emerging: orchestrates AI agents rather than manages people.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (convergence — 10+ independent signals)
**Key claims:** (1) $1M-10M revenue per employee. (2) Teams of 15-50 doing what required 200+ in 2020. (3) Born this way — no transition playbook exists.

### 10. 37% Erosion + Absorption Bottleneck Confirmation
**Source:** https://www.accountingtoday.com/news/37-of-ai-time-savings-eroded-by-need-to-review-and-revise-outputs — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** 2026
**Agent level:** Personal/Team
**What:** 37% of AI time savings eroded by review and revision need. "Workslop" — AI output that looks polished but makes no sense. Without process redesign to handle verification, gross time savings don't become net productivity. Directly confirms the absorption bottleneck pattern.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single study)
**Key claims:** (1) 37% time savings erosion. (2) Verification burden absorbs individual gains. (3) Process redesign needed for net productivity.

## What I Looked For But Did Not Find

1. **No documented successful transition from traditional org to AI-native org at scale.** Block cut then wrote about it. Bayer redesigned for bureaucracy reasons. AI-native startups were born that way. The transition playbook does not exist.

2. **No single company that captured measurable INSTITUTIONAL value with zero organizational change.** The counter-evidence agent searched 20+ queries. Closest: Shopify, which changed norms, incentives, and infrastructure. Not "zero change."

3. **No convergence on a specific organizational model.** Block: 3 roles + world model. Bayer: autonomous teams of 6-10. Shopify: hiring constraint + performance reviews. Fundamentally different approaches. Convergent DIRECTION (flatter, smaller, human-at-edge) but no canonical form.

4. **No business-function-specific redesign documentation.** Lots of engineering/coding org redesign. Zero on "how we restructured the finance team for AI" or "how HR operations was redesigned." The business function gap is total.

5. **No Nordic company that has documented organizational redesign for AI.** Nordic financial services cutting jobs (5,000 expected 2026), but headcount reduction ≠ redesign. DAIN Studios (Helsinki) published the framework — no Nordic company published the execution.

6. **No practitioner-derived framework for AI organizational design.** Academic frameworks (Stanford, Harvard) and consultant frameworks (Deloitte, BCG) exist. Zero practitioner-built "here's what we did" frameworks.

## Orientation

**The diagnosis has Level 3 convergence. The prescription has zero convergence. The transition playbook is the white space.**

This is the most significant research gap we've identified since the absorption bottleneck. At least 15 independent voices — Stanford (51 deployments), Harvard (P&G experiment), Fed economists (multiple), practitioners (Sivulka, O'Reilly, Mollick), operators (Shopify, Klarna, Block) — converge on the same finding: individual AI productivity does not aggregate to institutional value without organizational change. The evidence level is solid Level 3.

But nobody has the answer. The "redesigned floor" is being described from the outside (academics, VCs) and from the endpoint (AI-native startups). The path from 5,000-person traditional org to AI-native operating model is undocumented. That's the gap — and it maps directly to our value proposition: competence first, then the organization redesigns its own floor.

**Three tiers framing is the key nuance:** Sivulka's binary (redesign or fail) is too clean. Shopify shows a middle path — cultural change + infrastructure + incentive alignment, without structural reorganization. Most organizations should aim for Tier 2 before attempting Tier 3. Competence training IS Tier 2.

**Connection to existing patterns:**
- Absorption bottleneck: CONFIRMED and strengthened (37% erosion, P&G social connectivity loss)
- Competence-first: CONFIRMED (every redesign failure correlates with skipping competence)
- Intent Taktik: Block's "world model" is essentially Intent Taktik built — AI coordination layer so leaders focus on intent
- Hybrid beats autonomous: Klarna reversal is the strongest evidence yet

**New pattern candidate:** "The Transition Playbook Gap" — the diagnosis converges, the endpoint is visible, but nobody has the map from here to there. This is where Train → Curate → Connect → Advise lives.
