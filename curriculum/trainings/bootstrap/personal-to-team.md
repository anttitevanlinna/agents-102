# From Personal to Team

## Big Idea
Your agent works for you. Making it work for the team is a different problem.

## Meta
- **Primary Bloom's level:** Analyze → Evaluate
- **Prework:** 8 short stories on scaling personal agents to team/company level (TODO: curate). Must include variance — skill-based, recurring cloud agent (cron, not enterprise platform), etc. Goal: show there's no single right way.
- **Homework:** announcement-to-deployment gap reading (inoculates against vendor pitches)
- **Materials (trainer):** access-model template, governance checklist (who can ingest? who changes schema? who sees output?)
- **Plug points:** the participant's organization — who would own this system?

## What You'll Learn
After this module, you will be able to:
- **Identify** what is personal vs. team-ready in your system (credentials, context, guardrails, assumptions)
- **Redesign** for shared access and governance
- **Evaluate** where the system breaks when handed to a pair partner
- **Distinguish** the promotion path: personal → team → company agents as different products

## Exercises
- **Promote from personal to team** — List everything personal about your system: credentials, context, assumptions baked into guardrails — everything that breaks when someone else runs it. Redesign for the team: shared access to the brain, governance (who can ingest sources? who changes schema? who sees output?). Hand the whole thing to a pair partner. Can they run it? Can they get useful output without your context? Where does it fail?

## Key Concepts (Emergent)
- **The promotion path** — personal, team, company agents are different products. Promotion means redesigning, not just sharing.
- **The Access-Trust Gap** — 54-95% of enterprises have AI access. 5-22% have trust/production readiness. Gap always >35 points. The bottleneck is trust-building, not technology.
- **The personal → team gap is not technical.** It's about shared context, trust, and ownership.

## Plug Points
- **Ownership model.** Who in your organization would own this system? Who's accountable when the output is wrong?

## Bridge
You can build agents, secure them, evaluate them, and scale them to a team. What if you didn't have to build each one by hand?
