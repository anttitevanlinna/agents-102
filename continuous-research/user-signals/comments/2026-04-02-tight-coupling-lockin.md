Practitioner insight: the management chain is a tightly coupled system that can't be partially upgraded.

The chain (strategy → planning → coordination → resource allocation → execution → review → decision) has 12+ links, all assuming human-speed actors and human-format interfaces. An LLM could run 95% of this better than humans — coordination, dependency resolution, constraint optimization, scenario simulation. There's no reason to stop at 35 requirements; 100 or 200 is tractable for a modern LLM. The LLM would do better at most of what organizations call "decision-making and coordination" — which is actually optimization, not judgment.

**But nobody can extract any single link.** Each link's interfaces are coupled to the links before and after it through implicit human assumptions — meeting formats, document lengths, approval workflows, trust relationships, political negotiations. You can't run link 4 at AI speed when links 3 and 5 run at human speed. The 5% that genuinely requires human judgment (values, risk appetite, accountability) locks the other 95% at human speed — not because the 95% needs humans, but because everything is coupled.

**This is why "the whole chain at once feels impossible to give to AI."** It's not a capability problem. It's a coupling problem. The proof: the practitioner already demonstrated AI can do the analysis (2.5 days, 35 requirements, contradiction-free). The system can't absorb the result because the organizational chain wasn't designed for AI-speed, AI-volume output.

**Intent Taktik resolution:** Don't extract links from the 12-link chain. Replace it with a 3-link chain: Intent → Execute at AI speed → Trajectory check. The 95% doesn't get extracted — the chain gets redesigned to have fewer links. Coordination, planning, dependency resolution collapse into the execution phase.

**The chicken-and-egg:** You need Intent Taktik to make the decision to adopt Intent Taktik. The old system can't approve its own replacement because the replacement produces output the old system can't absorb.

**Gap:** How do you agents-101 Intent Taktik inside a tightly coupled management chain? The PM entry point may be the seam — PMs sit at the junction between strategy and execution, and transforming how they work changes the most important interface without requiring the whole chain to change simultaneously.

**Connects to:** Intent Taktik, absorption bottleneck (L4), PM entry point strategy, tight coupling as adoption barrier.
