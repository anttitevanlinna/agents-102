**Source:** https://www.okta.com/products/govern-ai-agent-identity/ — [vendor press release]; https://siliconangle.com/2026/03/16/okta-unveils-new-framework-manage-ai-agents-upcoming-okta-ai-agents-platform/ — [tech press, bare facts] — March/April 2026

**Date:** GA confirmed April 30, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Okta for AI Agents reaches GA on April 30, 2026 — making it the **first enterprise identity/governance product to reach general availability** in the agent governance space. (All others — ConductorOne, Stacklok, Vorlon, Astrix, NVIDIA OpenShell — remain pre-GA or early preview as of this cycle.)

Core capabilities at GA:
- **Agent discovery + registration:** Discovers unknown/unsanctioned agents, registers them as first-class identities in Universal Directory
- **Access certification workflows:** Agents brought into standard review cycles with human owners assigned
- **SIEM integration:** Agent activity (tool calls, authorization decisions, access attempts) logged to existing SIEM
- **8,200+ platform integrations** extended to include Boomi, DataRobot, Google Vertex AI as agent-aware platforms
- **Agent Gateway:** Centralized control plane — admins aggregate and expose tools from Okta's MCP registry (virtual MCP server capability)

**Evidence level:** Level 0 for capabilities (all Okta sources). Level 1 — bare fact of GA timing is independently confirmed by MSSP Alert (domain trade publication) and SiliconAngle (tech press).

**Key claims:** This is the GA event the entire agent governance category has been waiting for — Okta's move from EA to GA on April 30 is the first time any major enterprise identity vendor has committed to production-ready agent governance. Zero independent practitioner deployment evidence yet. The product shipped; the deployments haven't happened.
