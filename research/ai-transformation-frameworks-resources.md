# AI Transformation Frameworks — Resource Catalog

OODA Round 1 | 2026-02-15

---

## 1. Andrew Ng's AI Transformation Playbook (2018)

- **Author/Publisher:** Andrew Ng (Landing AI)
- **Type:** Step-by-step adoption playbook (5 steps)
- **Covers:** Pilot projects, in-house AI team, broad AI training, AI strategy, internal/external communications
- **Target audience:** C-suite, especially non-tech executives
- **Agents coverage:** None (predates agentic era)
- **Influence:** Extremely widely cited; the default "how to start" reference for executives entering AI. Thousands of LinkedIn reshares. Simple, actionable, and vendor-neutral.
- **Key idea:** Do pilot projects first, then develop strategy — not the other way around.
- **URL:** https://landing.ai/ai-transformation-playbook/

## 2. McKinsey — State of AI / Superagency / Gen AI Shifts (2023-2025)

- **Author/Publisher:** McKinsey & Company / QuantumBlack
- **Type:** Annual survey + strategic frameworks
- **Covers:** Adoption maturity, value capture, workforce transformation, "Factory vs Artisan" deployment patterns, reimagining enterprise architecture for gen AI
- **Target audience:** C-suite, boards, technology leaders
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — the 2025 State of AI report is subtitled "Agents, Innovation, and Transformation." Distinguishes autonomous agent workflows (Factory) from AI-assisted human work (Artisan).
- **Influence:** The most-cited annual benchmark for AI adoption. Shapes board-level conversations globally.
- **Key ideas:** Only 1% of companies consider themselves "mature" in AI deployment. 92% plan to increase AI investment. Four gen AI shifts reshaping enterprise technology.
- **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/superagency-in-the-workplace-empowering-people-to-unlock-ais-full-potential-at-work

## 3. Gartner — AI Maturity Model & Hype Cycle (ongoing)

- **Author/Publisher:** Gartner
- **Type:** Maturity model (5 levels) + annual Hype Cycle positioning
- **Covers:** AI maturity from awareness to transformational; strategic technology trends; market predictions
- **Target audience:** CIOs, IT leaders, technology strategists
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — Agentic AI named a top strategic technology trend for 2025. Predicts 15% of daily work decisions made by agentic AI by 2028; 40% of enterprise apps will have task-specific agents by end of 2026.
- **Influence:** Gartner's maturity model and Hype Cycle are the lingua franca of enterprise IT planning. Procurement and strategy teams reference these constantly.
- **Key idea:** Five levels of AI maturity: Awareness, Active, Operational, Systemic, Transformational.
- **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2024-10-21-gartner-identifies-the-top-10-strategic-technology-trends-for-2025

## 4. Deloitte — Tech Trends 2026: Agentic AI Strategy (2025)

- **Author/Publisher:** Deloitte Insights
- **Type:** Strategic framework for agentic AI orchestration
- **Covers:** Strategic orchestration of agent ecosystems; lifecycle from process reimagining to agent alignment; avoiding "agent sprawl"
- **Target audience:** CTOs, CIOs, enterprise architects, digital transformation leads
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — entirely focused on agentic AI. Warns about agent sprawl, technical debt amplification, and the need for unified strategy.
- **Influence:** Deloitte Tech Trends is widely read by enterprise leadership. This is one of the first Big Four publications to put "agentic" in the title.
- **Key idea:** Organizations need a strategic orchestration framework to prevent uncontrolled agent proliferation.
- **URL:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends/2026/agentic-ai-strategy.html

## 5. MIT Sloan Management Review — The Emerging Agentic Enterprise (2025-2026)

- **Author/Publisher:** MIT Sloan Management Review (with BCG)
- **Type:** Research report / strategic framework
- **Covers:** How leaders navigate the shift to agentic AI; decision-making delegation; human-agent accountability boundaries
- **Target audience:** Senior executives, strategy leaders
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — directly addresses the "agentic enterprise" concept. Core premise: agentic AI makes increasing numbers of decisions while humans retain the decisions that matter most.
- **Influence:** MIT SMR carries strong academic credibility with executive audiences. BCG co-authorship adds consulting weight.
- **Key idea:** The radical premise is organizational — not technical. It is about which decisions humans keep and which they delegate.
- **URL:** https://sloanreview.mit.edu/projects/the-emerging-agentic-enterprise-how-leaders-must-navigate-a-new-age-of-ai/

## 6. IBM Institute for Business Value — Agentic AI Operating Model (2025)

- **Author/Publisher:** IBM IBV
- **Type:** Operating model framework
- **Covers:** Moving from incremental AI gains to net-new impact; managing agents as workers; organizational readiness and governance
- **Target audience:** C-suite, operations leaders, IT leaders
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — explicitly about agentic AI. Frames agents as a new category of "worker" requiring governance, boundaries, and human accountability for critical decisions.
- **Influence:** IBM IBV reports are widely distributed to enterprise clients. "Managing agents as workers" framing is gaining traction.
- **Key idea:** Many agentic AI implementations are failing because organizations treat them as technology deployments rather than workforce transformations.
- **URL:** https://www.ibm.com/thought-leadership/institute-business-value/en-us/report/agentic-ai-operating-model

## 7. Google Cloud Consulting — Blueprint for Enterprise-Wide Agentic AI Transformation (2026)

- **Author/Publisher:** Google Cloud Consulting (published via HBR Sponsored Content)
- **Type:** Enterprise transformation blueprint
- **Covers:** Enterprise-wide agentic AI adoption; multi-agent ecosystems
- **Target audience:** Enterprise technology and business leaders
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — entirely agentic-focused.
- **Influence:** Published in HBR (sponsored) gives it reach. Very recent (February 2026).
- **Key idea:** Knowledge graphs as coordination hubs connecting specialized agents across departments.
- **URL:** https://hbr.org/sponsored/2026/02/a-blueprint-for-enterprise-wide-agentic-ai-transformation

## 8. Microsoft — AI Adoption Framework & Copilot/Dynamics 365 Agentic Strategy (2023-2025)

- **Author/Publisher:** Microsoft
- **Type:** Adoption framework + product-embedded strategy
- **Covers:** AI readiness assessment, responsible AI principles, Copilot deployment playbooks, agentic business applications
- **Target audience:** IT leaders, business application owners, Microsoft ecosystem customers
- **Agents coverage:** Yes — Microsoft's Convergence 2025 declared "the era of agentic business applications." Copilot agents embedded across Dynamics 365.
- **Influence:** Massive reach through Microsoft's install base. Shapes how millions of enterprise users first encounter AI agents.
- **Key idea:** Agents are not standalone — they are embedded into existing business applications (ERP, CRM).
- **URL:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics-365/blog/business-leader/2025/12/09/the-era-of-agentic-business-applications-arrives-at-convergence-2025/

## 9. Google PAIR (People + AI Research) Guidelines (2019, updated)

- **Author/Publisher:** Google PAIR team
- **Type:** Human-AI interaction design guidelines
- **Covers:** UX patterns for AI products; how to design human-AI collaboration; mental models, explainability, error handling
- **Target audience:** Product designers, UX researchers, product managers
- **Agents coverage:** Limited — focused on earlier AI paradigms (recommendations, predictions). Being updated but not primarily agentic.
- **Influence:** Influential in product/design communities. Less known among C-suite.
- **Key idea:** Design for human-AI collaboration, not just AI accuracy.
- **URL:** https://pair.withgoogle.com/

## 10. World Economic Forum — AI Governance Frameworks (2024-2025)

- **Author/Publisher:** WEF (with various partners)
- **Type:** Governance and responsible AI frameworks
- **Covers:** AI ethics, governance structures, regulatory readiness, workforce impact
- **Target audience:** Government leaders, board members, policy makers, CHROs
- **Agents coverage:** Growing — recent publications address autonomous decision-making governance.
- **Influence:** Sets the tone for regulatory and ESG-oriented conversations. Referenced by boards and compliance teams.
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/communities/centre-for-the-fourth-industrial-revolution/
