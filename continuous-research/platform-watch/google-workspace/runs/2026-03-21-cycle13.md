## Findings

### 1. Workspace Studio GA plagued by capacity issues
**Source:** https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/constant-capacity-issue-in-gemini-workspace-studio-we-are-at-capacity-well-be-back-soon/111858 — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** December 2025 – March 2026
**Agent level:** Personal
**What:** Multiple paying users on Google's own developer forums report persistent "We are at capacity" errors when running Workspace Studio agents. Even paid-plan users hit immediate "Quota Exceeded" errors. Capacity throttling at infrastructure level, not just tier limits. UI bugs (unresponsive buttons, disappearing sidebars), execution termination errors, and broken monitoring. 63 user-reported outages in 24 hours around GA date (March 19).
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (multiple individual reports, not yet convergence)
**Key claims:**
- Users blocked by "at capacity" messages for days at a time
- Quota errors on paid plans with minimal usage
- 63 user-reported outages in 24-hour period around GA

### 2. Architectural limitations make Studio unsuitable for enterprise automation
**Source:** https://dev.to/alifar/google-workspace-studio-technical-overview-a63 — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** ~March 2026
**Agent level:** Company (architectural constraints affecting enterprise deployment)
**What:** Three structural gaps: (1) No external event ingestion or webhook triggers — cannot serve as general automation backbone, only internal Google orchestration. (2) No explicit state management — no state machine, no transactional persistence, no replay mechanism. (3) No observability — no decision traces, making debugging and stakeholder explanation impossible. Combined with 100-agent cap, 20-step max, ~10 integrations vs Zapier's 6,000+, this positions Studio as personal/team tool, not enterprise platform.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single practitioner analysis, corroborated by Google's own docs)
**Key claims:**
- No webhook/external event triggers
- No state persistence or replay
- No decision trace/observability
- Max 20 steps, 100 agents per org

### 3. Shadow AI / agent sprawl is the real governance concern
**Source:** https://www.uctoday.com/productivity-automation/is-google-workspace-studios-rollout-already-ahead-of-your-governance-policy/ — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** ~March 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** No-code Studio creates agent sprawl risk structurally different from shadow IT. A shadow app stores data; a shadow agent acts on it — reading emails, sending messages, running on schedules. When employees leave, their agents may keep running. Forrester analyst J.P. Gownder: "creating agents is beyond the skill level of most employees." 98% of orgs report unsanctioned AI use. Tool is ON by default for all Workspace users; governance isn't ready.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (trade assessment, corroborated by multiple sources)
**Key claims:**
- Workspace Studio available to all Business/Enterprise users by default
- No built-in agent lifecycle management for orphaned agents
- Forrester: agent creation "beyond skill level of most employees"

## Orientation

Workspace Studio's GA is significant as the first real no-code business agent builder from Google, but the ground truth is sobering: capacity failures on day one, architectural limitations that prevent enterprise use, and zero independent deployment evidence. Google has two separate agent products (Studio for personal, Gemini Enterprise for company) and neither has proven production value. The governance gap (tool ON by default, governance not ready) is a concrete advisory opportunity. Microsoft maintains the enterprise lead through integration breadth and model flexibility.
