# Enterprise Agentic Transformation — Practitioner Observations

**Context:** A ~500-person European cybersecurity company, one year into agentic transformation (March 2026). Observations from hands-on transformation leadership — training, workshops, greenfield AI-native teams, and cross-functional initiatives.

**Evidence level:** Direct practitioner observation. Single organisation. Directional, not generalisable without cross-validation.

---

## What We've Seen Work

**Greenfield AI-native teams achieve breakthrough velocity.**
Small teams (3-5 people) assembled with agentic coding as entry criterion went from zero to product launch in 6 months. The leader's personal practice level determined the team's trajectory — not training programmes, not mandates.

**Hands-on training for business users produces agent practitioners at high rate.**
Two cohorts: 50 engineers (NPS 80%), 12 business people (NPS 90%, all building agents independently). The business cohort scored higher — driven by self-actualisation. People who had ideas stuck behind tool barriers for years suddenly discovered they could build. Not adoption. Liberation.

**C-suite going through training changes the leadership dynamic.**
CRO, CTO, CPO all went through the same hands-on training. Built agents personally. One mandated AI-first in their function after the experience. Another demoed personally-built dashboards at a company townhall. They became evangelists through lived experience, not through being briefed. The research says 70% of C-suite uses AI <1hr/week (HBR, Feb 2026). Having your C-suite in the 30% that actually builds is a competitive advantage.

**Agentic workshop format replicates well.**
AI-first workshops (agents do discovery and synthesis, humans provide inputs and validate) ran 4+ times in 2 months across different functions. The format scales. Participants consistently rate them highly. One workshop produced 272 findings from 35 contributors in 2 days.

**Business people adopt more eagerly than engineers.**
Counterintuitive but consistent. Engineers have existing workflows to unlearn. Business people just start building. The self-actualisation driver is strongest in people who've been consumers of tools their whole career and suddenly become creators.

## Original Observations

### 1. Scepticism Is a Skill Problem, Not a Belief Problem

People who try AI tools, get bad output, and lack the iteration skill to improve it conclude "this doesn't work." The scepticism isn't about belief in the technology — it's about personal capability to make it work. Fix the skill (teach iteration), scepticism dissolves. Maps to the Infosys/MIT "Security" need: "Will I retain value?"

### 2. The Adoption Sequence Is Reversed

Standard change management: vision → strategy → tools → adoption.

What actually works: **tool that gives the WOW → competence through doing → person sees their own future → they act.**

The strategic vision is embedded in the training (culminating in "agents building agents"), not presented separately. People don't need a strategy deck. They need the WOW, and then competence to sustain it.

### 3. "AI-Native" Is Current Operating Mode, Not Origin

Nobody was born AI-native. Every practitioner BECAME one recently — some in months. The distance from "not native" to "native" is shorter than anyone thinks. The barrier isn't the distance. It's surviving the first false start (see observation 1).

### 4. Verification Infrastructure Is the Gate for Cross-Domain Scaling

Engineering has test automation — machine-checkable quality that doesn't depend on a human reviewer. Every other domain relies on "run it past Sarah." As long as expert review is the only way to verify agent output, the dependency bottleneck persists regardless of how fast agents generate.

Three levels of verification maturity:
1. Expert-in-the-loop (bottleneck moves from generation to review)
2. Rule-based automated checks (human reviews exceptions only)
3. Eval-based verification (agent evaluates agent, system improves over time)

You can't skip levels. Level 2 is the near-term target for most domains.

**The principle:** Verification infrastructure turns "only the expert can do this" into "anyone with an agent can do this."

### 5. The Conditions Creator Role

The most effective transformation approach observed is NOT project management, change management, or executive sponsorship programmes. It's someone who sets up the conditions — tools, training, peer networks, methodology, cultural safety — and then gets out of the way. People self-organise around capability once they have it.

Observable at Anthropic (Ben Kuhn's "glue" function), Citi (champion program team), GitHub (playbook maintainers). Not formally studied or named by any researcher.

### 6. Small Workshops Beat Big Charters

Time-boxed, decision-focused cross-functional workshops (2 days, clear deliverable) survive organisational priority pressure. Big-bang transformation charters (multi-month, cross-functional, requires sustained SVP coordination) compete poorly for roadmap space. The workshop format produces results. The follow-through after the workshop is where organisational reality bites.

### 7. The Leader's Practice Level Determines Team Trajectory

One seed practitioner with genuine depth changes a team faster than any training programme aimed at the whole team simultaneously. Capability propagates through proximity — working together, watching, asking, trying. But the seed must be in a position that sets team norms. A junior seed in a team led by someone without the WOW produces individual productivity gains, not team transformation.

## Open Questions From This Case

- Does the WOW → Competence → Vision → Action sequence hold in other industries and cultures?
- How do you accelerate the middle management layer (the people who set team working patterns) when C-suite and individual contributors are already there?
- Can function-level AI-native adoption converge into cross-functional transformation without a formal cross-functional owner?
- What does verification infrastructure actually look like for marketing, sales, HR, finance?
- How do AI-native systems degrade over time? Maintenance is undocumented.
