## Orientation

**The story hasn't changed — it has solidified.** Three defining patterns are now confirmed across multiple cycles:

1. **Cowork silence is structural, not early.** 14+ days, zero reviews, other Frontier features shipping. Ben Thompson's analysis adds strategic depth: Cowork's reliance on Claude proves model-agnosticism is dead for agentic work. Microsoft must partner for the thing it's selling as its future.

2. **Foundry GA is marketing-grade, not production-grade.** One week post-GA, the practitioner signal is exclusively bug reports. Every customer is vendor-sourced. Some practitioners evaluating Claude as fallback — a remarkable signal for Microsoft's own PaaS.

3. **The governance-before-agents pattern is now fully established.** Shadow AI Detection GA, Agent 365 launching May 1, Entra Agent ID — all governance infrastructure for agents that don't exist in production. Microsoft is governing the future, not the present.

**For the CTO question:** Microsoft has the broadest surface area and the strongest governance story, but zero evidence that business agents work in production. The E7/Agent 365 May 1 launch is the next inflection point — if the deployment gap persists through May, it transitions from "early" to "structural."
