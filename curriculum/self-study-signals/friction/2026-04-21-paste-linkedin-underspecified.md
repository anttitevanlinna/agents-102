**Context:** Module 1 personal-site exercise, Phase 1. Live test 2026-04-21.

**Signal:** *"Paste your LinkedIn profile into the chat"* under-scaffolds for a business-leader audience. They will hesitate — *"the whole page? including the navigation chrome and the sidebar mess? that can't be right."* Without the explicit micro-steps and the reassurance that mess is fine, the student stalls before the first prompt even runs.

**Fix applied:** rewrote Phase 1 opener as three numbered micro-steps (open profile in browser → select all + copy → paste into Claude session, then send prompt) plus an explicit *"the mess is the point"* reassurance — *"Claude sorts the signal from the page chrome. If you over-curate the input now, you'll never feel how much the later context changes the output."* The hesitation is preempted; the pedagogical reason for the messiness is named.

**Generalize — micro-step rule:** the first time the student does any "copy from app X, paste into Claude" move, spell out the keystrokes and acknowledge the rough texture of the result. After Module 1 they'll have the muscle; before then, *"paste your LinkedIn"* is too compressed. Same likely true for the meeting-screenshot move (Path B in Task 2 prework — already mostly fine but worth a check) and any future "grab content from your work app and dump it in" prompt.
