### Finding 1: Workspace Studio GA rollout actively being throttled — capacity issues persist

**Source:** [Google Support — Access to Workspace Studio](https://support.google.com/workspace-studio/answer/16782648) — [vendor documentation]
**Additional:** [Google Support — Troubleshoot Workspace Studio](https://knowledge.workspace.google.com/admin/studio/troubleshoot-workspace-studio-for-your-users) — [vendor documentation]
**Additional:** [StatusGator — Workspace Studio](https://statusgator.com/services/google-workspace/google-workspace-studio) — [independent monitoring]
**Date:** March 19-21, 2026 (ongoing)
**Agent level:** Personal / Team
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (vendor acknowledgment + independent monitoring + user reports)

**What:** Two days after full GA, Google's own support documentation confirms: "To ensure the best experience, Google is adjusting the rollout pace. If you are waiting for access, the feature might take longer to appear in your account than originally scheduled." Users report inability to open Studio side panel, specific steps unavailable, and previously-created agents encountering runtime errors. StatusGator logged 63 outage reports in the 24 hours around GA launch. Google AI Developers Forum threads from December 2025 show the "We are at capacity" error has been persistent for 3+ months without resolution.

**Change from cycle 22:** No improvement. Same capacity issues, same acknowledgment. The throttled rollout means many paying customers who expected GA access on March 19 still don't have it.

**Key claims:**
- Rollout pace actively slowed to manage demand (verifiable in support docs)
- Previously-created flows failing at runtime — not just new access denied
- 63 outage reports on GA day (StatusGator, verifiable)
- Capacity error persistent since December 2025 (3+ months, verifiable in forum thread)
- Promotional limits expire March 31 — capacity likely to worsen further

## Orientation

### The picture is unchanged from cycle 22. Key dynamics:

**Capacity crisis deepening, not resolving.** Google launched GA while actively throttling access. The "at capacity" error has been continuous since December 2025. With promotional limits expiring March 31 and no communicated capacity expansion, the situation may worsen. For a CTO, this is a reliability red flag — an automation platform that can't reliably run automations is not an automation platform.

**The deployment evidence gap is now conspicuous.** Five months since Gemini Enterprise launch. Three months since Workspace Studio preview. One named Studio deployment (Karcher, vendor-sourced). Zero independent practitioner accounts of building anything with either product. Zero metrics from any named Gemini Enterprise customer. At this point, the absence of evidence is becoming evidence of absence — or at minimum, evidence that deployments are too small or too early to talk about.

**The two-tier problem persists.** Business users get Workspace Studio (limited, ungoverned, unreliable). Developers get ADK 2.0 + Vertex AI (powerful, governed, enterprise-grade). There is no bridge between them. The "every employee builds agents" narrative collides with 20-step limits, 50 executions/day, no lifecycle management, and capacity errors.

**Cloud Next '26 (April 22-24) is the next inflection point.** Google's "experimentation to operations" framing suggests they know the gap. The question is whether they ship governance + capacity + real customer evidence, or deliver another round of announcements without deployment proof.

**Nordic signal: still zero.** Six consecutive cycles. Google Workspace is available in the Nordics. Enterprise agent adoption is not happening — or not visible.

### For the CTO question:
Google's agent platform in March 2026 is a promise, not a product. Workspace Studio cannot reliably serve its paying customers. Gemini Enterprise has no proven deployment willing to share results. ADK 2.0 is a solid developer framework but irrelevant to business users. **Wait for Cloud Next '26 evidence, not announcements.**

### What to watch next:
1. **March 31 promotional limit expiry** — what happens to capacity and execution limits?
