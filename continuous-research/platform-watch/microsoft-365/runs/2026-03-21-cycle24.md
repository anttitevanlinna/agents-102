### 2. Agent 365: Practitioner analysis reveals licensing ambiguity ahead of May GA
**Source:** [Rob Quickenden blog](https://robquickenden.blog/2026/03/agent-365-nears-ga/) — [practitioner direct, Level 2]
**Date:** March 2026
**Agent level:** Company (governance)
**What:** Rob Quickenden (Microsoft MVP) provides the most detailed independent analysis of Agent 365 pre-GA. Key finding: the $15/user/month licensing model creates genuine confusion — agents don't map 1:1 to users, so unclear whether orgs license builders, users, or both. "The commercial model is still catching up with the technology." Frontier early-access orgs get 25 free licenses through Dec 2026. Agent 365 works with first-party, org-built, AND third-party agents — not ecosystem-locked. But specific governance enforcement mechanisms remain undocumented.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (practitioner analysis, single source)
**Key claims:**
- $15/user/month licensing unclear: who gets licensed — builders or users?
- 25 free licenses for Frontier program orgs through Dec 2026
- Works with non-Microsoft agents (third-party support)
- Governance enforcement mechanisms not yet documented
- "Tens of millions of agents" appeared in registry in 2 months of preview

## Orientation

The Microsoft story hasn't materially changed since cycle 21. The governance layer (Agent 365) is maturing faster than the agents it governs — a pattern consistent with Microsoft's enterprise DNA (secure the perimeter first, then let things in). Copilot Cowork's "late March" broader access hasn't arrived, extending the gap between announcement and availability. The most useful new signal is the practitioner analysis of Agent 365 licensing ambiguity — this is likely to be a significant adoption friction point at GA. The picture remains: **strongest governance vision, weakest deployment evidence.**
