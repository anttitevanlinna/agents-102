## Findings

### 1. Moderna — Strongest Enterprise Competence-First Validation (Level 2)
**Source:** [HBS case study](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=66910) — [academic/research]; [BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/moderna-openai-gpt-generative-ai-biotech-dose-id/714140/) — [domain trade publication]; [Inc.](https://www.inc.com/ben-sherry/why-vaccine-maker-moderna-is-injecting-ai-across-the-company/91188434) — [general press]
**Date:** 2025-2026 (HBS case study + ongoing reporting)
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Moderna is the most completely documented competence-first adoption at enterprise scale. Sequence: (1) CEO-led vision, (2) educated entire workforce BEFORE deploying advanced tools, creating organic demand, (3) employees built 1,000s of custom GPTs for their own needs — nobody told them what to automate, (4) legal team reached 100% adoption, (5) merged HR and Tech departments. CIO: "Successful AI implementation means putting our employees at its center." Directly mirrors our F-Secure observation (competence creates pull) at massive scale.
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (single company, but extensively documented across academic and independent sources)
**Key claims:**
- Workforce-wide education preceded tool deployment
- 1,000s of custom GPTs built organically by employees
- Legal team: 100% AI adoption
- HR and Tech departments merged
- CEO-driven but bottom-up adoption pattern

### 2. Mollick's "The Shape of the Thing" — Agent Management Era (Level 1-2)
**Source:** [oneusefulthing.org/p/the-shape-of-the-thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/the-shape-of-the-thing) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** Mid-March 2026
**Agent level:** Personal / Team
**What:** Mollick declares phase shift from "co-intelligence" (prompting) to "managing AIs" (assigning hours of work, getting results in minutes). **Evidence level:** Level 1-2 (practitioner opinion from most credible researcher, with named case study)
**Key claims:**
- Phase shift from prompting to managing agents
- StrongDM 3-person Software Factory (named case study)
- "Remarkably little has changed in most organizations"
- "Organizational inertia is a delay, not a moat"

### 4. MIT 95% Study — Methodological Criticism Emerging (Counter-evidence)
**Source:** [Marketing AI Institute critique](https://www.marketingaiinstitute.com/blog/mit-study-ai-pilots) — [practitioner analysis]; [YourEverydayAI critique](https://www.youreverydayai.com/do-95-of-ai-pilots-fail-why-you-should-ignore-mits-viral-new-ai-study/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** Late 2025 / Early 2026
**Agent level:** N/A (methodology)
**What:** The MIT 95% pilot failure statistic we cite heavily has significant methodological problems: (1) success measured within 6-month window — enterprise tech often takes 1-2 years, (2) based on 52 interviews — directionally accurate but small sample, (3) contradicted by IDC survey (4,000 decision-makers): $3.70 ROI per $1 invested, (4) Snowflake/ESG survey (1,900 leaders): 92% early adopters report positive ROI. **We should treat 95% as directional, not definitive.**
**Evidence level:** Counter-evidence to our cited Level 3
**Key claims:**
- MIT study based on only 52 interviews
- 6-month measurement window too short for enterprise
- IDC 4,000-person survey contradicts with $3.70 ROI/$1
- Truth likely between "95% failure" and "92% positive ROI"

### 7. Semantic Fragmentation as Failure Root Cause (Level 1)
**Source:** [Alexander Shereshevsky/Graph Praxis](https://medium.com/graph-praxis/why-enterprise-ai-agents-are-failing-387c6fc21591) — [practitioner direct]
**Date:** January 19, 2026
**Agent level:** Company
**What:** Enterprise AI agents fail because of "semantic fragmentation" — departments built incompatible data models over decades. Marketing's customer segmentation differs from Sales' account taxonomy. Agents working in one department produce nonsense in another. Fix: "build semantic foundations before deploying agents." References TOVE Project (U of Toronto). Not a model quality problem — data architecture problem.
**Evidence level:** Level 1 (single practitioner opinion, well-argued)
**Key claims:**
- Semantic fragmentation = incompatible data models across departments
- Agent works in one department, fails in another
- Requires "semantic foundations" before agent deployment

## Orientation

Three findings shift the picture this cycle:

**1. Competence-first reaches Level 3.** Moderna (HBS-documented, 1,000s of GPTs, trained-first) joins F-Secure, Mollick's framework, Quintero's 100 companies, and Klarna's counter-example. **3. Business-domain practitioner gap narrowing slowly.** Walmart/Uppuluri (supply chain) and Rocher (HR) are first genuine practitioner voices in previously empty domains. But the structural insight holds: business-domain practitioners don't publish like developers. Conference circuits may be better discovery channels.
