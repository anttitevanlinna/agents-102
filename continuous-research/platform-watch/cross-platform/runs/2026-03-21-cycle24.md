### 1. Microsoft Copilot Cowork Ships — Powered by Anthropic Claude, Not OpenAI
**Platform:** Microsoft / Anthropic
**Source:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/09/microsoft-copilot-cowork-ai-agents-anthropic-e7-m365-saas/ — [general press, bare facts]
**Also:** https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/microsoft-announces-copilot-cowork-with-help-from-anthropic-a-cloud-powered — [tech press]
**Also:** https://www.geekwire.com/2026/microsofts-new-copilot-cowork-integrates-anthropics-claude-in-rollout-of-new-e7-licensing-tier/ — [tech press]
**Date:** March 9, 2026
**What:** Microsoft announced Copilot Cowork as Wave 3 of M365 Copilot — a cloud-based agentic automation tool that can complete multi-step, long-running work across M365 apps. The critical detail: it is built with Anthropic and powered by Claude, using the same "agentic harness" as Anthropic's Claude Cowork. Claude is now available across the full Copilot Chat experience, not just Researcher and Excel. This is Microsoft's clearest move toward a multi-model strategy, breaking its exclusive OpenAI dependency. Copilot Cowork runs within a customer's M365 tenant with existing security/governance. Currently in Research Preview with limited customers; broader access via "Frontier program" late March 2026. New M365 E7 tier at $99/user/month (bundles Copilot + Entra + Agent 365, available May 1).
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (multiple independent press sources confirming the same facts; product is real and shipping to preview customers)
**Why it matters for our buyers:** The Microsoft-Anthropic partnership signals that enterprise AI is now genuinely multi-model. Organizations investing in M365 are no longer locked into OpenAI. The governance story — Cowork runs within existing M365 security boundaries — directly addresses enterprise trust concerns. The $99/user/month E7 pricing sets a market anchor for enterprise agent costs.

### 2. Google Workspace Studio GA — 20M Tasks, But Capacity Walls Persist
**Platform:** Google
**Source:** https://workspaceupdates.googleblog.com/2025/12/workspace-studio.html — [vendor blog, Level 0]
**Also:** https://support.google.com/gemini/thread/393815872 — [practitioner direct, user reports]
**Also:** https://support.google.com/a/thread/393169706 — [practitioner direct, user reports]
**Also:** https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/constant-capacity-issue-in-gemini-workspace-studio-we-are-at-capacity-well-be-back-soon/111858 — [practitioner direct, user reports]
**Date:** March 19, 2026 (GA rollout to all domains)
**What:** Workspace Studio launched to all Rapid Release and Scheduled Release domains on March 19. Google claims 20M+ automated tasks in the past month. However, multiple user-reported threads on Google support forums document persistent "We are at capacity, we'll be back soon" errors. StatusGator showed 63 user-reported outages in the 24 hours before March 19. Additionally, promotional access to higher usage limits ends March 31 — after that, customers need the AI Expanded Access add-on to maintain current limits. Workspace Studio caps agents at 100 per org and 20 steps per agent.
**Evidence level:** Level 2-3 (vendor claim for 20M tasks = Level 0; capacity issues confirmed by convergent user reports on support forums = Level 2-3)
**Why it matters for our buyers:** The capacity issues are the real story. Google has distribution (every Workspace customer gets access), but the product cannot reliably serve demand. The March 31 pricing transition creates a decision point for every enterprise Workspace customer. The 20-step-per-agent limit constrains complex business workflows.

### 3. NIST Agent Identity Standards — Three Deadlines in the Next Two Weeks
**Platform:** Cross-platform (regulatory)
**Source:** https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/02/announcing-ai-agent-standards-initiative-interoperable-and-secure — [government/regulatory]
**Also:** https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/other/2026/02/05/accelerating-the-adoption-of-software-and-ai-agent/ipd — [government/regulatory, concept paper]
**Also:** https://www.biometricupdate.com/202603/nist-concept-paper-explores-identity-and-authorization-controls-for-ai-agents — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** February-March 2026 (deadlines: March 31, April 2)
**What:** NIST's Center for AI Standards Innovation launched the AI Agent Standards Initiative on Feb 17. Three critical deadlines are imminent: (1) Agent security RFI closed March 9; (2) Draft automated benchmark evaluations closes March 31; (3) NCCoE concept paper on AI Agent Identity and Authorization — comments close April 2. The concept paper focuses on adapting existing IAM frameworks (OAuth, OpenID Connect, SCIM) for AI agents — not building new ones. NIST's track record: the AI RMF went from voluntary to appearing in executive orders and state laws within 18 months. The Gravitee 2026 report finding that only 14.4% of agents go live with full security approval gives these standards operational urgency.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (government standards initiative with industry-wide participation; convergent concern from multiple independent sources about agent identity gaps)
**Why it matters for our buyers:** Agent identity is about to become a governance requirement, not a nice-to-have. Nordic enterprises that start treating agents as managed identities now will avoid the compliance scramble when these standards hit procurement requirements. This is a concrete topic for our Agents 101 training — "your agents need identities just like your employees do."

### 4. Protocol Convergence: MCP + A2A Under Single Foundation, Major Vendors Adopting Both
**Platform:** Cross-platform
**Source:** https://www.anthropic.com/news/donating-the-model-context-protocol-and-establishing-of-the-agentic-ai-foundation — [vendor announcement, Level 0 for Anthropic's claims but the AAIF founding is a verifiable fact]
**Also:** https://www.ciodive.com/news/big-tech-develop-open-standards-agentic-ai/807608/ — [domain trade publication]
**Also:** https://www.salesforceben.com/salesforce-announces-agentforce-3-0-command-center-mcp-and-apps/ — [domain trade publication]
**Also:** https://www.cio.com/article/4012370/could-agentforce-3s-mcp-integration-push-salesforce-ahead-in-the-crm-ai-race.html — [domain trade publication]
**Date:** December 2025 – March 2026
**What:** MCP and A2A are now both governed under the Linux Foundation's Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), co-founded by OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, AWS, and Block. MCP has crossed 97M monthly SDK downloads. Salesforce Agentforce 3 shipped native MCP + A2A support, positioning itself as a "central nervous system for multi-agent orchestration." The protocols are settling into complementary roles: MCP = vertical (agent-to-tools), A2A = horizontal (agent-to-agent). Salesforce's AgentExchange marketplace now exposes agents and skills via both protocols. This is no longer a standards war — it's convergence.
**Evidence level:** Level 3 (multiple independent adopters implementing the same pair of protocols; AAIF governance is a structural fact)
**Why it matters for our buyers:** The "which protocol?" question is resolving faster than expected. Organizations don't need to pick — they need both, and they're complementary. This simplifies the agent platform advisory conversation. MCP for connecting agents to enterprise systems; A2A for agent-to-agent coordination. Salesforce's implementation is the most concrete enterprise example of both protocols in production.

## Orientation

**The three biggest cross-platform shifts since Cycle 23:**

1. **Multi-model is now the default enterprise strategy.** Microsoft — OpenAI's closest partner — built Copilot Cowork on Anthropic's Claude. OpenAI Frontier supports non-OpenAI agents. Salesforce Agentforce supports MCP connections to any model. The single-vendor AI strategy is dead for enterprise. This is the most important signal for our buyers: you are not choosing a model, you are choosing a governance layer.

2. **Protocol convergence is happening faster than governance.** MCP + A2A are settling into complementary roles under the AAIF. Salesforce shipped both natively. But NIST is just now defining agent identity standards (comments close April 2). The Gravitee finding that only 14.4% of agents go live with full security approval means the plumbing is outrunning the controls. This gap — protocols moving fast, governance lagging — is a key training insight for Module 4 (Security) and Module 7 (Agent Platforms).

3. **Distribution beats capability.** Google Workspace Studio has the broadest distribution (every Workspace customer), but capacity issues undermine it. Microsoft Copilot Cowork has M365 distribution + Anthropic's reasoning. Salesforce has CRM distribution + protocol interoperability. OpenAI Frontier has the best models but the narrowest distribution (limited preview, FDE-dependent). The winner won't be the best agent — it will be the agent that works where people already work.

**Implications for Agents 102 training:**
- Module 7 (Agent Platforms) needs to frame the landscape as "governance layers" rather than "agent platforms." The model is increasingly irrelevant; the governance/deployment surface is everything.
- Module 4 (Security) should incorporate the NIST agent identity framework — treating agents as managed identities with OAuth/OIDC is a concrete, teachable concept.
- The agent platform advisory offering gains urgency: customers face real decisions in Q2 2026 (Workspace Studio pricing transition March 31, Copilot Cowork broader access late March, NIST deadlines April 2).
