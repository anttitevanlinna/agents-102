# Citi — Champion-Driven AI Adoption at Scale

**Type:** Champion-driven adoption | **Size:** ~182,000 | **Evidence:** Moderate case
**Key sources:** Artificial Intelligence News (2026), Lead With AI (2026), Citi Gen AI Summit (2025)

---

## How They Did It

- **4,000 "AI Accelerators"** — peer champions embedded across functions in 84 countries
- **70%+ adoption** of firm-approved AI tools across 182,000 employees
- **385,000+ logged tool interactions**
- Built over two years
- Approach: distribute usable capability to thousands rather than concentrating AI knowledge in a small expert group

## The Champion Model

Champions are:
- Volunteers, not appointed
- Embedded in their own teams (peer trust, not authority)
- "Part coach, part translator, part feedback loop" (GitHub's parallel description)
- ~30-60 min/week commitment
- Influence comes from demonstrating value, not from mandate

## What Transfers

- **The champion-at-scale model is the most proven large-enterprise adoption approach.** 182K people, 84 countries, 70% adoption. Nobody else has this at this scale.
- The 4,000:182,000 ratio (~1:45) suggests the champion density needed
- Peer influence is the mechanism — confirmed by Microsoft/HBR quantitative study (8.9pp increase)

## Limitations

- Adoption ≠ AI-native transformation. 70% tool adoption doesn't mean teams changed how they work.
- Banking context (regulatory, compliance-heavy) — may not apply to all industries
- Two-year timeline — not quick

## Key Insight

**People copy people, at scale.** Citi proves the peer influence mechanism works at 182K. The champion model is the operational implementation of the research finding. Every CC101/training graduate is a potential champion — the question is whether you name them and support them.
