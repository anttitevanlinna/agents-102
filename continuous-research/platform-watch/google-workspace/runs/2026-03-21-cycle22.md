### Finding 1: Workspace Studio capacity issues — officially acknowledged, NOT resolved

**Source:** [Google Support — Access to Workspace Studio](https://support.google.com/workspace-studio/answer/16782648?hl=en&ref_topic=16430802) — [vendor documentation]
**Additional:** [Google AI Dev Forum — capacity thread](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/constant-capacity-issue-in-gemini-workspace-studio-we-are-at-capacity-well-be-back-soon/111858) — [community forum / practitioner reports]
**Additional:** [StatusGator](https://statusgator.com/services/google-workspace/google-workspace-studio) — [independent monitoring]
**Date:** March 19-21, 2026 (ongoing)
**Agent level:** Personal / Team
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (multiple independent user reports + vendor acknowledgment)

**What:** Google has now **officially acknowledged** the capacity problem in their support documentation. The Access to Workspace Studio help page states: *"Google is currently receiving a high volume of requests and access may be impacted. They are working to resolve this immediately."* Users may experience: inability to open Studio, specific steps unavailable, and **previously-created flows encountering runtime errors**. Google is **"adjusting its rollout pace"** — slowing down because they can't handle the load.

**Change from cycle 19:** Escalated from "unacknowledged forum complaints" to "officially acknowledged in support docs with service degradation warnings." This is worse, not better — Google launched GA knowing they couldn't handle demand. StatusGator: 63 outage reports in 24hrs around GA; 21,228 outage reports in February 2026.

**Key claims:**
- Google officially acknowledges capacity impact (verifiable in support page)
- Rollout pace being slowed to manage demand (verifiable)
- Previously-created flows failing at runtime — not just new access denied (verifiable)
- 21,228 outage reports in February 2026 (StatusGator)
- Promotional higher limits expire March 31 — capacity may worsen

### Finding 2: Hard limits documented — confirms personal/team tool, not enterprise backbone

**Source:** [Google Support — Workspace Studio limits](https://support.google.com/workspace-studio/answer/16765942?hl=en) — [vendor documentation]
**Additional:** [dev.to — Workspace Studio Technical Overview](https://dev.to/alifar/google-workspace-studio-technical-overview-a63) — [practitioner analysis]
**Additional:** [aitoolanalysis.com — Workspace Studio Review](https://aitoolanalysis.com/google-workspace-studio/) — [practitioner analysis]
**Date:** March 2026
**Agent level:** Team / Company
**Evidence level:** Level 2 (practitioner analysis + vendor documentation converge)

**What:** Hard limits now documented:
- **20-step maximum** per flow — rules out complex multi-stage business processes
- **25 active Gmail-triggered flows** per user
- **50 executions/day** on Business Standard/Plus; **500 executions/day** on Enterprise
- **Daily run cap** — when hit, ALL flows pause until next 24-hour cycle
- **~10 integrations** at launch (Salesforce, Jira, Asana); Slack, HubSpot, SAP "planned" (vs. Zapier's 6,000+)
- **Webhooks in limited preview** only

Practitioner analysis reconfirms cycle 19 architectural findings:
- No state persistence: *"state exists only as long as it is derivable from Workspace context"*
- No observability: *"logs focus on outcomes rather than reasoning"*
- No decision trace for auditability
- For complex 20% of tasks: *"you'll still want a technical friend nearby"* — no-code promise breaks down

**Key claims:**
- 50 executions/day Business, 500/day Enterprise (verifiable)
- 20-step maximum per flow (verifiable)
- State is "derivable, not persisted" — no replay, no accountability (practitioner analysis)
- Webhooks in limited preview only (verifiable)
