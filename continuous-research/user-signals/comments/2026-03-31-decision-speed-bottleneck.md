Practitioner insight: on the business side, the real bottleneck is decision speed, not creation speed. If AI does not speed up decision-making, end-to-end speed does not improve.

AI accelerates the cheapest part of the value chain (creation) while leaving the most expensive part (decisions) untouched. A marketing team generating 50 drafts instead of 5 still needs someone to decide which one to run. End-to-end cycle time is dominated by decisions, not creation.

This is Goldratt's Theory of Constraints: optimizing a non-constraint is waste. If the constraint is decision-making, speeding up creation is optimizing the wrong thing.

**Three lenses on the same bottleneck:**
1. Cognitive: absorption bottleneck — humans can't evaluate at volume
2. Queuing theory: Little's Law — WIP explodes when generation outpaces review
3. Business: decision speed is the constraint, not creation speed

**Gap:** Does AI speed up decision-making? Where? The only case where AI demonstrably speeds decisions is when it reduces options to a recommendation (e.g., agent-evaluates-agent producing a ranked shortlist). But that requires trust in the AI's judgment — which is exactly what's declining (ManpowerGroup: confidence DOWN 18% while usage UP 13%).

**Implication for training and advisory:** Stop selling AI as "faster creation." Start selling AI as "better decisions" — which requires verification infrastructure, trust building, and human judgment skills. This connects directly to Phase 4 skills (verification architecture) and the experience-first sequence (competence builds trust).
